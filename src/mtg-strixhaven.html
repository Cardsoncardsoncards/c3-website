How We Calculate EV for Australian Players

Expected value (EV) in TCG is calculated by multiplying the pull rate of each card rarity by the average secondary market value of cards in that rarity tier, then summing across all cards in a pack and multiplying by the number of packs you are opening.

For Australian players, we use AUD-denominated prices sourced from the Australian secondary market rather than USD TCGPlayer prices, which can differ by 15 to 30 percent after currency conversion and import costs. Our AUD values are based on recent eBay Australia sold listings for cards in each rarity tier.

Is Secrets of Strixhaven worth opening in Australia? ▼
At AU$259.95 per box the answer depends on what you pull in the Mystical Archive slot. A Force of Will or Ancestral Recall in that slot significantly shifts EV. For players who enjoy the opening experience, Strixhaven is a solid choice. For pure value, compare EV to the cost of buying your target singles directly.
What is the expected value of a Secrets of Strixhaven booster box? ▼
Use the calculator above for a current AUD figure. EV shifts as secondary market prices settle after release. The Mystical Archive slot is the most variable factor, as commons are worth near nothing while Force of Will can be worth AU$30+.
What does EV mean in TCG? ▼
Expected Value (EV) is the average value of cards you would expect to open across a large number of packs, based on each card's pull rate multiplied by its secondary market price. If a booster box has an EV of AU$180 and costs AU$260, you are paying AU$80 above EV for the experience of opening packs.
Should I buy the Strixhaven box or buy singles? ▼
If you want specific cards, buying singles from the C3 eBay store is almost always more cost-efficient than opening packs. If you enjoy the experience of opening packs or are playing Limited, a box makes sense. Run the EV calculator and compare it to the cost of the singles you actually want.
How accurate is the EV calculation for Australian players? ▼
Our EV uses AUD secondary market prices from the Australian eBay market rather than USD TCGPlayer prices converted to AUD, which makes it more accurate for Australian buyers. However, EV is always an average estimate. Your individual box results will vary significantly from the expected value.
