Good Games has launched its 2026 Championship Series, a national tournament run across 20 stores
spanning New South Wales, Victoria, the ACT, Tasmania, South Australia, Queensland and Western
Australia. The series carries a combined $3,000 prize pool across five headline games: One Piece
Card Game, Magic: The Gathering, Riftbound, Yu-Gi-Oh! and Pokemon. Tickets went on sale at 5pm on
13 July 2026.

Alongside the main Championship events, Good Games has introduced "Battles," a new tier of
smaller, regional tournaments capped at 20 players. The idea is straightforward: not every player
wants to travel to a flagship store for a major event, and a 20-player cap keeps a Battle
genuinely local and approachable, while still feeding into the wider Championship structure. A
handful of stores, including Albury, Mildura and Hobart, are running Battles rather than full
Championship events, giving smaller regional communities a real seat at the table without asking
them to compete for a slot at a capital-city venue.

## Five games, one series

The five headline games reflect what's actually being played competitively across Good Games'
network right now. One Piece Card Game has become one of the fastest-growing tournament scenes in
the country, helped along by weekly Friday constructed nights running at multiple stores. Magic:
The Gathering remains the most established tournament game in the space, with decades of
competitive infrastructure behind it. Yu-Gi-Oh! brings its own dedicated, highly technical
community built around precise combo lines and archetype support. Pokemon TCG continues to draw
both younger players and long-time collectors, helped this year by a milestone 30th anniversary
push from The Pokemon Company. And Riftbound, Riot Games' League of Legends card game, is the
newest addition to the lineup, riding a wave of momentum after a packed 2026 release schedule that
has seen three major sets land in under twelve months.

It's a deliberately broad selection. Rather than backing a single game, Good Games has built a
Championship structure that gives players across five quite different communities a reason to
show up to the same series, at the same stores, in the same window.

It's also worth being clear about what isn't part of the headline five. Games like Gundam, Dragon
Ball Super Fusion World and Disney Lorcana all have active organized play running through Good
Games' regular weekly calendar, they're simply not part of this particular Championship
structure. That doesn't make them any less established locally, and we'll be covering each of
them individually over the coming days, alongside the five headline games.

## Store spread and access

Twenty participating stores across seven jurisdictions is a wide net for a single
tournament series to cast. New South Wales carries the heaviest concentration, with stores across
metro Sydney and regional centres like Newcastle and Albury. Western Australia follows closely,
anchored by several Perth-metro locations. Smaller states get proportionally fewer venues, but
none are left out entirely, a deliberate choice that ties back to the Battles format, giving even
single-store states like the ACT and Tasmania a genuine path into the series.
