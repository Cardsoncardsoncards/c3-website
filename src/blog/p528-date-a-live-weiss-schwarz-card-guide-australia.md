# Date A Live in Weiss Schwarz: Spirits, Standby Play and Neo-Standard Depth

Date A Live centres on Shido Itsuka's efforts to save a cast of Spirits, and its Weiss Schwarz card pool has built up genuine deck building depth, including a documented neo-standard construction feature that lets newer material combine with earlier releases. If you already play and want the deck building detail, jump ahead. If you are newer to Weiss Schwarz, the primer below covers the core rules, with the full mechanics glossary linked at the end.

## The set at a glance

Date A Live spans 3 tracked releases, the original set (26 March 2021), the Date A Bullet spin-off (December 2021), and Vol.2 (March 2023), totalling 443 cards.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## Standby Play and Neo-Standard Construction

Date A Live's card pool is built around its confirmed core cast, Tohka Yatogami, Origami Tobiichi, Kotori Itsuka, Yoshino, and Kurumi Tokisaki, each Spirit bringing her own card line. Community deck guides document a "standby" play style built around specific character combos, for example Tohka builds that combine a level 0 and level 2 version of her card line for board presence. Confirmed directly via Bushiroad's own product listings, Date A Live booster material is also built for neo-standard construction, meaning newer card numbers can be combined with earlier release material rather than treating each release as a fully separate collecting lane, similar in spirit to how Madoka Magica's Magia Record material extends that property's deck building pool.

Weiss Schwarz does not use a rotating standard format, so older cards generally remain playable, though always check a specific event's rules and neo-standard construction requirements before entering.

**How does the four copy limit affect building around Date A Live's five named Spirits?**
It forces a choice, since you cannot run four copies of every Spirit's key cards at once, most decks lean on one or two Spirits' lines rather than spreading evenly across the confirmed cast.
