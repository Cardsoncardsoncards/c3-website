## What Is Secrets of Strixhaven?

Strixhaven University is a plane-spanning school of magic founded by five Elder Dragons, each representing a distinct magical college. The original Strixhaven set (2021) introduced the colleges, the students, and the Mystical Archive bonus sheet. Secrets of Strixhaven picks up where that story left off, returning to Arcavios after the events of March of the Machine and exploring what the students and faculty are up to now.

This is a main set, not a Universes Beyond crossover. No licensed IP characters, no branded artwork from outside the Magic universe. For a large part of the player base that has been experiencing crossover fatigue, that is a meaningful distinction and a big reason why community hype for this set has been unusually strong well before release.

The set releases into Standard, Legacy, and Vintage legality. The five Commander precon decks are Commander-legal, as expected.

## The Five Colleges

Each of the five magical colleges at Strixhaven specialises in a different combination of mana colours and magical discipline. Your college preference will guide which Commander deck makes sense for your playstyle.

**Silverquill (white/black):** The college of words and rhetoric. Magic through eloquence, debate, and the power of language. The Silverquill Commander deck focuses on Auras and using the Goad mechanic to make opponents attack each other.

**Witherbloom (black/green):** The college of life and death magic. Herbalism, necromancy, and the cycle of decay and growth. The Witherbloom deck is an Aristocrats build, using sacrifice and life drain to grind out value.

**Lorehold (red/white):** The college of history and archaeology. Magic drawn from ancient artefacts and the spirits of the past. Spirit tribal synergies and graveyard interaction.

**Prismari (blue/red):** The college of elemental arts. Magic expressed through elemental forces and creative performance. Big spells and Elemental synergies, with the new Opus mechanic rewarding instant and sorcery casts.

**Quandrix (blue/green):** The college of mathematics and nature. Magic through numerology and the patterns underlying the natural world. Big creatures and the Fractal token strategy with +1/+1 counter synergies.

## New Mechanics

**Prepare:** A new keyword that puts a card in the prepared state when it enters the battlefield. Prepared creatures have access to bonus effects, typically accessible by tapping or triggering certain abilities. Emeritus of Ideation is the most prominent card using this mechanic.

**Opus:** A mechanic for the Prismari (blue/red) faction. When you cast an instant or sorcery, a card with Opus triggers. If you spent five or more mana on that spell, Opus provides an additional, stronger reward. Colorstorm Stallion is an early example revealed.

**Ward:** Returns as an evergreen mechanic from the original Strixhaven. Creatures with Ward require opponents to pay an additional cost to target them with spells or abilities.

## The Mystical Archive Returns

One of the most celebrated elements of the original Strixhaven set was the Mystical Archive: a bonus sheet of 65 iconic instant and sorcery spells from throughout Magic's history, each with brand new artwork, with one guaranteed in every pack.

The Mystical Archive returns in Secrets of Strixhaven. One Mystical Archive card appears in every Play Booster pack, and Collector Boosters include at least three. Confirmed cards on the Mystical Archive include reprints of powerful and iconic spells including Force of Will and Vampiric Tutor. The presence of Force of Will on the Archive alone makes the Collector Booster version of this set particularly attractive to Legacy players.

Each Mystical Archive card also has a Japanese variant with alternate artwork and full Japanese text, available in Japanese-language Play Boosters and in English Collector Boosters.

## Chase Cards: What Everyone Will Be Looking For

**Emeritus of Ideation (Serialised Headliner)**
The chase card of the set. Only 500 serialised copies exist, all in Collector Boosters, featuring a double rainbow foil treatment and throwback full-art frame by Mark Poole, the original artist of Ancestral Recall. The card itself lets you cast Ancestral Recall while in the prepared state. As a mechanically unique serialised card with Power Nine DNA, expect this to be among the most valuable individual Magic cards released in 2026.

**Ral Zarek, Guest Lecturer and Professor Dellian Fel**
The only two planeswalker cards in the main set. Both receive borderless treatments. With only two planeswalkers in the entire set, they occupy a unique position in terms of collector scarcity.

**Lorehold, the Historian and the Elder Dragon Cycle**
All five Elder Dragons representing the Strixhaven colleges are mythic rares with borderless treatments. As potential Commanders with thematic depth, the borderless versions are expected to be strong sellers.

**The College Dual Lands**
Strixhaven brings back its signature land cycle with each land themed to one of the five colleges. Full-art spellcraft land treatments are available in Play and Collector Boosters.

**Special Guests (10 Cards)**
Ten Special Guest cards appear in the set, featuring cards from across Magic's history reimagined with Strixhaven-themed artwork. Sylvan Library is the confirmed example. Special Guest cards can be non-foil in Play Boosters and traditional foil in Collector Boosters.

## Is Secrets of Strixhaven Worth Pre-Ordering?

**For Commander players:** Yes, if any of the five precon decks match your colour preferences. Five decks at once is unusually generous and means there is almost certainly a deck suited to your playstyle. Commander precon pre-orders carry lower risk than booster box pre-orders because the precon has a fixed, known card list rather than RNG pulls.

**For draft players:** Pre-ordering a Play Booster Display makes sense if you have a regular draft group. The Mystical Archive guarantees consistent pack value and the set has genuine drafting depth as a proper Magic plane.

**For collectors:** The set has a clear and exciting collector proposition. Serialised headliner, Mystical Archive reprints including Force of Will, borderless planeswalkers and elder dragons, Japanese Mystical Archive variants. Collector Boosters for this set are more compelling than many recent sets.

**For speculators:** The Mystical Archive reprint of Force of Will in particular may have pricing implications for the Legacy market. Serialised cards of this nature have historically commanded strong premiums. These are considerations rather than recommendations, as sealed product speculation always carries risk.

No. It is a main Magic set set on the Arcavios plane, returning to Strixhaven University. No licensed IP characters.

Yes. It becomes Standard legal on release day, 24 April 2026.
