# That Time I Got Reincarnated as a Slime in Weiss Schwarz: Rimuru's Demon Continent Cast

That Time I Got Reincarnated as a Slime brings Rimuru Tempest and the wider Jura Tempest Federation cast into Weiss Schwarz, built around the confirmed 《Demon Continent》 character trait that anchors the property's synergy. If you already play and want the combo detail, jump ahead. If you are newer to Weiss Schwarz, the primer below covers the core rules, with the full mechanics glossary linked at the end.

## The set at a glance

This property spans 3 tracked releases, the original set (31 July 2020), Vol.2 (October 2021), and Vol.3 (July 2023), totalling 481 cards.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## Documented Combo Lines Around Rimuru and Milim

This property's confirmed card text builds around the 《Demon Continent》 character trait, with Rimuru, Milim, Shion, Shizu, Shuna, Benimaru, and Souei all forming part of the confirmed named cast. Bushiroad's own deck recipe material documents multiple distinct combo approaches. One centres on Rimuru's "Inherited Form" and "Nameless Story" climax combo, allowing repeated character swaps and clock healing when set up correctly. A separate documented combo spotlight features "Destroyer" Milim as an early-game damage source thanks to her high power and double trigger check ability. A third documented approach centres on Benimaru's "Breaking the Enemy's Formation," which boosts other characters while doubling as a finishing burn effect. This gives genuinely different game plans depending on which character anchors your build, similar in spirit to how Saekano offers two distinct level 3 build paths.
