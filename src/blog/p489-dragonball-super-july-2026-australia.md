## Dragon Ball Super Masters vs Fusion World: What Is the Difference?

Dragon Ball Super TCG runs two parallel product lines in Australia, and it is worth understanding which one you are buying for.

**Dragon Ball Super Card Game Masters (the main line):** This is the original DBS card game, running since 2017. DBS-B31 is a booster set in this line. It is the format that most existing DBS competitive players are familiar with. The Masters line features a broader card pool and a more established meta.

**Dragon Ball Super Fusion World:** Fusion World is a newer, simplified version of the DBS card game, designed for more casual play and easier entry. FB10 Cross Force is the tenth booster in this line. Fusion World has been gaining traction with newer players and those who find the main Masters line complex.

If you already play DBS, you know which line you are in. If you are new to DBS and considering getting into the game, Fusion World is the more accessible starting point, while Masters offers deeper strategic complexity.

## DBS-B31 and Prime Day: Should You Wait?

DBS Masters B31 releases on 3 July. Prime Day opens 7 July. The gap is four days. This creates the same situation as Yu-Gi-Oh Chaos Origins: a very new set arriving just before a sale event.

Amazon AU does not typically apply significant Prime Day discounts to product released within the previous week. A DBS-B31 booster box at a meaningful discount during Prime Day is possible but unlikely. A small reduction of AU$5 to AU$10 is more realistic than a 15% or 20% discount.

The practical advice: if you need DBS-B31 for competitive play, buy at release on 3 July. If you are a casual collector who can wait a few days to check, look at the Amazon AU listing when Prime Day opens on 7 July before buying. If the price has not moved by day two of Prime Day, buy at regular retail without waiting further.

## Fusion World FB10: Better Prime Day Prospects

Fusion World FB10 Cross Force released on 13 June, giving it roughly 3.5 weeks on shelves before Prime Day opens. That is still on the early side for a significant discount, but Fusion World booster boxes sit at a lower retail price than the Masters line, meaning even a small Prime Day reduction produces meaningful savings.

If you are a Fusion World player who has not yet picked up FB10, checking the Amazon AU price when Prime Day opens is worth a few seconds of your time. A modest discount on an already affordable product is a good buy.
