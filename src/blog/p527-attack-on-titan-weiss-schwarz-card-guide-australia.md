# Attack on Titan in Weiss Schwarz: Four Colours, One Squad

Attack on Titan brings a genuinely different structure into Weiss Schwarz compared to most licensed properties, its card pool is built across all four colours at once, with each colour representing a different faction or focus within the story rather than one or two dominant colours. That gives collectors and deck builders a wider starting palette than almost anywhere else in the catalogue. If you already play and want the colour breakdown, jump ahead. If you are newer to Weiss Schwarz, the primer below covers the core rules, with the full mechanics glossary linked at the end.

## The set at a glance

Attack on Titan spans 3 tracked releases, from the original set (31 July 2015) through Final Season in September 2022, totalling 525 cards.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## Four Colours, Four Factions

Attack on Titan's card pool is confirmed to use all four colours, with Levi and the elite Special Operations Squad in green, Eren and Armin as the main yellow presence, Mikasa and fellow recruits in red, and the Titans themselves in blue. Each colour genuinely represents a different part of the source material rather than existing purely as a mechanical distinction. For deck builders, colour choice is also a character choice, a green and yellow build leans into Levi's squad alongside Eren and Armin, while a red-focused build centres more on Mikasa and the recruits. Most competitive decks settle into two of the four colours rather than spreading across all four, and the card pool's Eldian and Corps trait synergy rewards committing to specific character groupings within that choice.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Why does Attack on Titan use all four colours instead of one or two?**
Each colour maps to a different faction, green for Levi's Special Operations Squad, yellow for Eren and Armin, red for Mikasa and the recruits, and blue for the Titans, giving the property a genuinely wide colour spread.

**Should I build across all four colours or focus on two?**
Most competitive decks settle into two of the four colours rather than spreading evenly, matching how the Eldian and Corps trait synergy rewards committing to specific character groupings.

**Is a 3 set catalogue enough to represent all four colours properly?**
Yes, each of the four colours has a real character base to draw from across the 3 sets, the smaller set count affects overall depth more than colour variety.

**Do older Attack on Titan sets remain legal in tournament play?**
Weiss Schwarz does not use a rotating standard format, so older cards generally remain playable, though always check a specific event's rules before entering.

**What do the Eldian and Corps traits actually do?**
They are character trait types that certain cards check for when triggering abilities, building around cards that share one of these traits is a genuine synergy path in the card pool.

**How does the four copy limit affect a property with four colours of characters to choose from?**
It reinforces committing to one or two colours and their associated characters rather than trying to spread across the whole four colour cast, since you only have four copies of any single card to build around.
