## What Is The Brothers' War and Why Does It Matter for Australian Players?

The Brothers' War is set on Dominaria thousands of years before most MTG storylines, during the war between Urza and his brother Mishra over the Thran Mightstone and Weakstone. The set introduces Prototype (an alternative casting cost for artifact creatures that creates a smaller version) and Unearth (exile from graveyard to temporarily reanimate). The Retro Artifact bonus sheet appears in every pack — 63 artifact reprints in the classic old-border frame. The Play Booster box contains 36 packs.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the Retro Artifact bonus sheet?**
The Retro Artifact is a 63-card bonus set of powerful artifact reprints printed in the classic pre-8th Edition old-border card frame — the visual style used by MTG from 1993 to 2003. Every Brothers' War pack contains one Retro Artifact card. Values range from commons worth AU$0.50 to Wurmcoil Engine worth AU$8-20+ and Blightsteel Colossus worth AU$10-25+ in the retro treatment.

**Is The Brothers' War worth opening in Australia?**
At AU$160 for 36 packs, The Brothers' War offers moderate EV with the Retro Artifact bonus sheet providing collector value alongside regular set cards. The lower price point versus current sets makes it reasonable. Use the C3 EV Calculator for a current personalised assessment.

**Who are Urza and Mishra and why are they significant?**
Urza and Mishra are brothers whose ancient war over magical power stones shaped the entire MTG universe. Their conflict created the Phyrexian threat, established the first planeswalkers, and caused the catastrophic events that scarred Dominaria for millennia. They are the most historically significant characters in MTG lore and their appearance as legendary creatures in this set was a long-requested fan moment.

**How does The Brothers' War compare to Kamigawa: Neon Dynasty for EV?**
Kamigawa: Neon Dynasty significantly outperforms The Brothers' War in EV terms. Channel lands, The Wandering Emperor, and Fable of the Mirror-Breaker create a far stronger main set card pool. Brothers' War at AU$160 is more accessible but the per-card value averages lower than Kamigawa at AU$180.
