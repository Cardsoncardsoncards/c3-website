# Monogatari in Weiss Schwarz: Hitagi, Araragi and a Blue and Red Foundation

Monogatari brings Bakemonogatari and its sequel Nisemonogatari into Weiss Schwarz as a two-set property built on a confirmed blue and red trial deck identity, centred on Hitagi Senjougahara alongside Suruga Kanbaru, Mayoi Hachikuji, and the Araragi sisters. If you already play and want the character breakdown, jump ahead. If you are newer to Weiss Schwarz, the primer below covers the core rules, with the full mechanics glossary linked at the end.

## The set at a glance

Monogatari spans 2 tracked releases, BAKEMONOGATARI (14 February 2014) and NISEMONOGATARI (February 2016), totalling 274 cards.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## Two Confirmed Releases, One Continuous Series

The confirmed Bakemonogatari trial deck is blue and red, built around Hitagi Senjougahara's card line as a central presence, documented across multiple named versions including "Battle Situation" and "Weightless Girl" variants, alongside Suruga Kanbaru, Mayoi Hachikuji, and Karen and Tsukihi Araragi. Monogatari's Weiss Schwarz presence spans exactly two releases, the original Bakemonogatari and its direct sequel Nisemonogatari, both part of the same ongoing NisiOisiN light novel series. This is a more contained structure than properties spanning several distinct sub-series, the two confirmed sets represent a continuous storyline rather than separate, unrelated products, a useful contrast against a property like Fate, where multiple sub-series genuinely do require careful separation.
