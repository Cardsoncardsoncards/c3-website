# Bofuri Weiss Schwarz Card Guide (Australia)

Bofuri, full title BOFURI: I Don't Want to Get Hurt, so I'll Max Out My defence, was one of the standout isekai releases of its era, and its Weiss Schwarz set has quietly become one of the more consistently traded one-set properties in the English catalogue. The anime follows Maple, a new player in a VRMMO who dumps every stat point into defence to avoid pain, then accidentally becomes overpowered because of it. That premise gave the show a strong hook, and it gave the card set a genuinely distinctive main character to build a deck around.

## The set at a glance

Bofuri is a single stand-alone set in English, released 21 May 2021, with a total of 191 cards across the booster pack and trial deck. There has been no second English wave for this property, so unlike some longer-running properties on the platform, everything you need for a complete Bofuri collection sits in this one release.

The set code is BFR/S78, and it followed the standard Weiss Schwarz English release pattern of the time, a Trial Deck aimed at new players plus a Booster Pack release for the wider card pool.

## Building around Maple and the defence archetype

The card pool centres on Maple, whose in-game persona of stacking defence to an absurd degree translates into a genuinely different playstyle compared to most Weiss Schwarz decks, which typically reward aggression and tempo. Maple-focused builds lean into survivability and grinding value over time rather than closing games out quickly, which makes Bofuri an interesting pickup if you already enjoy slower, more attrition-based decks in other card games.

Sally, Maple's close friend and the more conventional agile fighter of the pair, provides the faster, more standard counterpart within the same card pool. Most competitive Bofuri builds blend support for both characters rather than committing entirely to one playstyle, since the set was designed with that balance in mind from the start.

## Chase cards and signature pulls

As with most English Weiss Schwarz releases from this period, Bofuri packs carried a chance of hot-stamped signed cards from the voice cast, and these signature versions of Maple and Sally in particular remain the cards most collectors are chasing rather than the standard playset commons and rares. PSA-graded copies of these signed cards, along with high rarity SP and SSP versions of Maple, have shown up regularly in graded card sales at a real premium over raw singles.

Because Bofuri is now a few years past its original release, supply on the secondary market has settled into a more predictable pattern than it had in the first year after launch. That makes it a reasonably approachable set to price-check and buy into with confidence, since you are not dealing with the volatility of a brand new release still working out where prices should sit.

## Why Australian collectors still buy into Bofuri

Isekai as a genre has stayed popular well past its initial anime boom, and Bofuri specifically benefits from being one of the more distinctive entries in that genre rather than a generic power fantasy. That distinctiveness has kept fan interest steady years after release, which in turn has kept demand for the card set from dropping off the way some one-off licensed releases do once the anime's initial popularity fades.

For Australian buyers, Bofuri is also a useful example of a set where patience pays off. Because it launched a few years ago rather than being a brand new release, pricing has had time to settle, so you can generally trust a listed price is close to fair market value rather than being inflated by launch hype or scalping.

## Trial Deck versus Booster Pack cards

New collectors sometimes assume Trial Deck cards are lesser versions of the cards found in boosters, but that is not accurate for Bofuri or for Weiss Schwarz generally. Trial Deck cards are a separate print run intended to give new players a functional deck out of the box, and several Trial Deck exclusive cards for Bofuri are genuinely useful in a competitive build, not just filler. If you are assembling a full Maple or Sally deck, do not overlook Trial Deck singles just because they came from the cheaper starter product rather than random boosters.

This is worth flagging because it affects how you should shop for this set. A complete competitive Bofuri deck typically needs cards pulled from both the Trial Deck and the Booster Pack, so budgeting for both product types, or buying the specific singles you need from each, will usually get you a better result than buying only booster singles and assuming that covers everything.

## What to check before buying

1. Confirm whether a listing is base rarity or a signed voice cast version. Photos should clearly show the hot stamp, not just claim it in the title.
2. Check the exact card number against the BFR/S78 set code before buying, since Bofuri shares character names with other isekai properties and it is easy to mix up a listing from the wrong game entirely.
3. Compare condition carefully on the higher rarity Maple and Sally cards. As an older release, some circulating stock has genuine wear, so factor that into what you are prepared to pay.
