We sourced four booster boxes of Riftbound Unleashed from Dice Arcade Miranda and opened every pack to build the singles inventory at [Cards on Cards on Cards](https://www.ebay.com.au/str/cardsoncardsoncards). Before listing anything, we tracked the data.

This post is the raw result. Not estimates, not averages from other people's box openings. Our four boxes, counted card by card.

---

## The Setup

**Boxes opened:** 4
**Packs opened:** 96 (24 packs per box)
**Expected cards at 14 per pack:** 1,344

---

## What We Actually Got

**Total cards observed:** 1,344 (the expected figure was correct, the packs were complete)

**Cards listed as eBay singles:** 1,184

**Gap (non-listed cards):** 160

The 160-card gap is art cards, puzzle cards, tokens, and other insert materials that we do not list as individual singles on eBay. These are cards that come in every box as part of the pack structure but are not collectible singles in the traditional sense. This gap is expected and consistent with the Riftbound pack structure.

---

## Foil Breakdown

**Total foil cards:** 287

**Total regular cards:** 897

**Foil rate across listed singles:** 24.2%

One in four cards being foil is a high rate compared to games like Lorcana or Pokemon. Riftbound's foil structure is part of its design identity, with foil versions of most cards available alongside regular prints.

## Overnumbered Cards

The stated rate for overnumbered cards (cards numbered above 219 in this 219-card set) is approximately 1 in every 3 boxes. We pulled 3 overnumbered cards from 4 boxes, which is slightly above the average expectation but within normal variance.

The overnumbered cards:

| Card | Number |
|---|---|
| Jhin - Virtuoso | 226 |
| Lillia - Bashful Bloom | 230 |
| Ivern - Green Father | 233 |

Three from four boxes. The stated rate would put expected pulls at 1.3 across four boxes. We got 3. That is good luck on our part.

## Signed Cards

The signed card rate is approximately 1 in 30 boxes. We pulled zero signed cards from four boxes, which is the expected outcome. You would need to open roughly 30 boxes before expecting to see one.

## The Honest Verdict

Four boxes of Riftbound Unleashed gave us exactly what the stated pack structure says you should get. The alternate art guarantee was met exactly. The overnumbered rate was slightly above expectation. The foil rate is high relative to other TCGs and that makes Riftbound a visually interesting game to open.

The 160-card gap from expected to listed is entirely art cards and inserts. We counted them out. They exist but they are not singles-worthy.

If you are considering opening Riftbound for the singles market: four boxes gives you comprehensive coverage of the base set with strong foil counts. The ROI depends entirely on what the Riftbound market does in Australia over the next six to twelve months.
