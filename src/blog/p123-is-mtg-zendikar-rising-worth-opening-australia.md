MTG Zendikar Rising released September 2020 and introduced Modal Double-Faced Cards (MDFCs) — a new card type where one side is a land and the other is a spell. This single design innovation changed how MTG players think about deckbuilding. A land that is also a counterspell, a land that is also a creature, a land that is also a board wipe — MDFCs eliminated the drawback of drawing too many lands in many deck archetypes.

The MDFC mythics from Zendikar Rising are still played in competitive formats five years after release, demonstrating the lasting impact of this design choice on the game.

## What Is Zendikar Rising and Why Does It Matter for Australian Players?

Zendikar Rising returns to the adventure plane of Zendikar for the third time following original Zendikar (2009) and Battle for Zendikar (2015). The set introduces Party (a mechanic rewarding having Clerics, Rogues, Warriors, and Wizards in play simultaneously) and the Modal Double-Faced Card cycle. The Draft Booster box contains 36 packs. Zendikar Expeditions — extremely rare alternate art basic lands — appear as bonus cards at very low rates (approximately one per case of six boxes).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What are Modal Double-Faced Cards and why do they matter?**
Modal Double-Faced Cards (MDFCs) are cards with a land on one side and a spell on the other. You choose which side to play — land for mana, spell for its effect. This eliminates the disadvantage of drawing too many lands because even land cards in your hand can be played as spells when you have enough mana. The MDFC mythics from Zendikar Rising (Agadeem the Undercrypt, Sea Gate Restoration, etc.) are still played in competitive formats today.

**Is Zendikar Rising worth opening in Australia?**
At AU$155 for 36 packs, Zendikar Rising offers above-average EV for a 2020 set due to the MDFC mythics being format staples. The lower price point improves the ratio. Use the C3 EV Calculator for a current personalised assessment.

**What are Zendikar Expeditions?**
Zendikar Expeditions are extremely rare alternate art full-art land cards that appear as bonus cards in Zendikar Rising packs at approximately one per case of six boxes. They are visually stunning and worth AU$30-150+ depending on the specific land. Finding one in a box is an exceptional pull but the odds are very low — do not factor them into a standard EV calculation.

**How does Zendikar Rising compare to Throne of Eldraine for EV?**
Zendikar Rising has above-average EV for its era due to the MDFC mythics. Throne of Eldraine (2019) was arguably the most powerful standard set in recent history with Oko Thief of Crowns, Once Upon a Time, and Emry all requiring bans. Eldraine likely had higher peak EV at release but has had more cards banned, which affects long-term secondary market values differently.
