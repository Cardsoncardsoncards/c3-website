# Rascal Does Not Dream in Weiss Schwarz: Adolescence Syndrome on the Stage

Rascal Does Not Dream brings its Adolescence trait system and a genuinely deep Mai Sakurajima card line into Weiss Schwarz, built from the original Bunny Girl Senpai release through to more recent follow-up material. ## The set at a glance

Rascal Does Not Dream spans 3 tracked releases, Bunny Girl Senpai (15 November 2019), Dreaming Girl (October 2022), and a further release simply tracked as the Rascal Does Not Dream Series (March 2025), totalling 557 cards. This most recent 2025 release is not covered in detail below beyond confirming its existence and card count, check the property page for its specific current listings.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## Mai's Combo Depth and the Adolescence Trait

Rascal Does Not Dream's confirmed card text builds around the 《Adolescence》 character trait, with Sakuta Azusagawa and Mai Sakurajima forming the core of the cast alongside Rio Futaba, Kaede Azusagawa, Shoko Makinohara, and Nodoka Toyohama. Confirmed via Bushiroad's own deck recipe material, Mai's climax combos include effects that retrieve key cards for future turns and boost her level to avoid enemy effects, with documented level 0, level 2, and level 3 versions of her card line built to combo together. Rio Futaba's climax combo is separately documented as a search engine that helps thin the deck for valuable cards. With this cast to choose from, a deck usually centres on Mai's card line supported by one or two other characters, rather than trying to represent the whole cast evenly.
