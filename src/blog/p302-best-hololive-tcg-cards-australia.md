## Quick Answer

Hololive TCG card values are driven entirely by talent popularity, not competitive stats. The most valuable cards feature the most subscribed and most beloved Hololive talents, particularly from the EN and JP rosters. Gura, Pekora, Koyori, Fubuki, and Calliope Mori cards in their highest rarity variants carry the strongest prices. This is a fan product market first and a competitive market second.

## How Talent Popularity Drives Value

Unlike most TCGs where competitive relevance is the primary value driver, the Hololive TCG's secondary market maps almost directly to talent subscriber counts and fan devotion. A talent with 5 million subscribers whose card is competitively marginal will outsell a mechanically powerful card featuring a lesser-known talent.

This is actually a more predictable market than most TCGs, because talent popularity changes slowly compared to shifting competitive metas. The biggest Hololive names have been dominant for years and their cards hold value based on that sustained fandom.

## Most Valuable Card Categories in Australia

**Gawr Gura (EN)** cards in the highest rarity variants are among the most consistently valuable in the Australian market. Gura's fanbase in Australia is exceptionally large and demand for her rarest cards reflects that. SR and SSR variants trade at AU$25 to AU$80.

**Pekora (JP)** is the most subscribed JP talent and her cards carry the broadest appeal within the JP fanbase. Foil and alternate art Pekora cards trade at AU$20 to AU$70.

**Calliope Mori (EN)** maintains strong secondary market interest from both Hololive fans and music-adjacent communities who discovered Hololive through her content. Expect AU$15 to AU$60 for high-rarity variants.

**Fubuki (JP)** as one of Hololive's original prominent members has deep nostalgic appeal within the JP fanbase. Her highest rarity cards trade at AU$15 to AU$50.

**Generation milestone cards** that feature entire generations of talents together (like gen-celebration releases) have broader appeal than single-talent cards and trade well with collectors who want multiple talents represented in one piece.

**Signed cards or autograph variants**, where these exist in Hololive TCG products, are the highest-value items in the entire game. Any card with a genuine talent signature commands several hundred dollars and more in the collector market.

## What to Look For in Booster Sets

Each Hololive TCG set release centres on specific talent groupings. The sets with the highest secondary market value at launch are those focusing on the most subscribed talents. Sets focusing primarily on less mainstream talents within the roster produce lower opening values.

Before buying a sealed box, check which talents are featured and cross-reference with the current talent subscriber rankings. This is genuinely a useful exercise for this specific game in a way that does not apply to other TCGs.

## The C3 Take

Hololive TCG has the most readable secondary market on this list for anyone who follows the VTuber scene. If you know who the most popular talents are, you know which cards are most valuable. The market logic is talent popularity, full stop.

For collectors who follow specific talents, buying high-rarity cards featuring those talents is a reasonable way to combine fandom with card collecting. For anyone who does not follow the VTuber scene, the secondary market will feel opaque because the usual competitive TCG logic does not apply.
