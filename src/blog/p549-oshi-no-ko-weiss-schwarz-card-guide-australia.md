# Oshi no Ko in Weiss Schwarz: Ai's Legacy and a Combined Launch Card Pool

Oshi no Ko brings Ai, Aqua, and Ruby into Weiss Schwarz, with the original release confirmed as a rare same-day trial deck and booster pack launch built for neo-standard construction from day one. If you already play and want the release structure detail, jump ahead. If you are newer to Weiss Schwarz, the primer below covers the core rules, with the full mechanics glossary linked at the end.

## The set at a glance

Oshi no Ko spans 2 tracked releases, the original set (20 September 2024) and Vol.2 (March 2026), totalling 490 cards.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## A Genuine Same-Day Combined Launch

Oshi no Ko's confirmed core cast spans Ai as the idol mother figure, her children Aqua and Ruby, and Kana Arima, MEMcho, and Akane Kurokawa in support. What makes this property distinctive, confirmed directly via official product listings, is that the original trial deck and Booster Pack were released on the exact same day and are explicitly built to combine under neo-standard deck construction from launch. This is a more integrated release structure than properties where trial deck and booster material arrive separately, a new player is expected to build from both releases together immediately. The confirmed Vol.2 follow-up is a genuine second wave rather than a correction or reprint, sharing the OSK card number prefix and extending the combined card pool further, mirroring what Blue Archive did with its own trial deck and booster release.
