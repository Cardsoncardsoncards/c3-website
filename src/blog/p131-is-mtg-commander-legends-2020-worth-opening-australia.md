MTG Commander Legends released November 2020 as the first-ever booster set designed specifically for Commander Draft — a format where players draft packs to build Commander decks on the fly. The set introduced a radical structural choice: every single pack guarantees two legendary creatures, ensuring every drafter always has Commander options from every pick.

The set is best remembered for introducing Jewelled Lotus — a card modelled directly on the legendary Black Lotus but restricted to Commander use. It was immediately one of the most impactful Commander cards ever printed and drove enormous initial demand.

## What Is Commander Legends and Why Does It Matter for Australian Players?

Commander Legends contains over 70 legendary creatures — the highest count of any single MTG set at the time of release. Every pack contains two legendary creatures guaranteed, providing an exceptional density of Commander options. The Draft Booster box contains 24 packs. Foil Etched treatment versions of legendary creatures (with a distinctive textured foil finish on the card's etched name and frame elements) are the premium collector versions. The set includes powerful reprints alongside new cards designed specifically for the Commander format.

## Chase Cards and What to Look For

Jewelled Lotus is the headline mythic — a zero-mana artifact that taps for three mana of any single colour, but only to cast your Commander. It was worth AU$60-100+ at release and remains AU$30-60+ after Commander Masters reprinted it. Opposition Agent prevents opponents from searching libraries. Hullbreacher (since banned in Commander) still holds collector value. Tevita Timeless Navigator, Galea Kindler of Hope, and Kamahl Heart of Krosa are key Commander mythics. Foil Etched versions of popular legendary creatures command significant AU premiums.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is Jewelled Lotus and is it worth buying for Commander?**
Jewelled Lotus is a zero-mana artifact that taps to add three mana of any one colour, usable only to cast your Commander. It accelerates your Commander into play one turn earlier consistently, which in Commander is a massive tempo advantage. It is legal in Commander (and Commander variants) only. At current AU prices of AU$30-60+ it is a significant investment but one that improves any Commander deck that can afford it.

**Is Commander Legends 2020 worth opening in Australia?**
At AU$155 for 24 packs with two legends guaranteed per pack, Commander Legends offers strong EV for Commander players. Jewelled Lotus as the headline mythic and the high legend density throughout make it a worthwhile Commander product. Use the C3 EV Calculator for a current personalised assessment.

**What is Foil Etched treatment?**
Foil Etched is a treatment where the foiling is applied specifically to the etched elements of the card frame — the name box, text box border, and frame lines — rather than the entire card. The result is a subtle, textured foil effect that catches light differently from traditional foiling. Commander Legends introduced this treatment for legendary creatures and it has appeared in several sets since.

**How does Commander Legends 2020 compare to Commander Legends Baldur's Gate?**
Both sets use the same two-legend-per-pack format and Commander Draft structure. Baldur's Gate (2022) is the DnD crossover sequel with a different card pool. Commander Legends 2020 has Jewelled Lotus as its standout card. Baldur's Gate introduced the Initiative mechanic and has Bhaal and Baba Lysaga as headline mythics. Both are worth opening for Commander players at similar price points.
