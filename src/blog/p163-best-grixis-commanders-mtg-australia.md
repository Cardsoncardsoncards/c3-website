The weakness is no green ramp and no white removal. but the colour combination is deep enough that many tables consider Grixis to be among the strongest.

## What Grixis Does Best

Drawing cards through multiple vectors simultaneously. Wheels (Wheel of Fortune, Windfall, Narset's Reversal) refuel hands. Black tutors find combo pieces. Blue counters protect them. Red provides the speed to execute before opponents stabilise.

## Top Grixis Commanders

### Inalla, Archmage Ritualist

Wizard tribal with a powerful trigger: whenever you cast a Wizard, you may pay 1 mana to create a hasty token copy of it until end of turn. In a deck full of powerful Wizards, this generates double the ETB triggers every turn.

**Current AU price**: approximately AU$3 to AU$8.

### Nekusar, the Mindrazer

Every player draws an extra card each turn. Every player takes 1 damage for each card drawn. In a deck full of wheel effects, Nekusar rapidly deals lethal damage to the entire table.

**Why it works**: punishes card draw (which everyone does), scales with wheel density.
**Current AU price**: approximately AU$4 to AU$10.

### Jeleva, Nephalia's Scourge

Exiles cards from the top of each player's library when she enters the battlefield, and allows you to cast opponent's instants and sorceries for free when she attacks.

**Current AU price**: approximately AU$3 to AU$8.

### Malcolm, Keen-Eyed Navigator / Breeches, Brazen Plunder (partner pair)

A popular partner pairing for pirate tribal. Malcolm generates Treasure tokens when Pirates deal combat damage. Breeches lets you cast cards exiled from opponents' libraries. Together they create a fast, theft-based strategy.

**Combined current AU price**: approximately AU$4 to AU$10 total.

### Yuriko, the Tiger's Shadow

The strongest Grixis commander For raw power ceiling. Yuriko uses Ninjutsu (sneak past blockers) to enter unblocked, and deals damage equal to the mana value of the top card of your library to all opponents when she does. With high-mana-value cards at the top, this deals enormous damage to the entire table simultaneously.

**Current AU price**: approximately AU$15 to AU$30.

## More Grixis Commanders Worth Considering

### Jeleva, Nephalia's Scourge

When Jeleva enters or attacks, exile the top X cards of each player's library, where X is Jeleva's power. You may cast one instant or sorcery exiled this way for free. A spell-slinging commander that steals your opponents' best instants and sorceries from their own libraries.

**Current AU price**: approximately AU$3 to AU$8.

### Yuriko, the Tiger's Shadow

Commander Ninjutsu lets Yuriko circumvent Commander tax by returning an unblocked attacker to hand. When Yuriko deals combat damage, reveal the top card of each player's library: each opponent loses life equal to that card's mana value and you draw those cards. Works with high-mana-value instants and sorceries for massive damage.

**Current AU price**: approximately AU$12 to AU$25.

### Marchesa, the Black Rose

Whenever another creature you control with a +1/+1 counter on it dies, return it to the battlefield under your control at the beginning of the next end step. Creates near-unlimited recursion for your own creatures while dethrone (gaining counters when attacking the player with the most life) protects your position.

**Current AU price**: approximately AU$5 to AU$12.

## Grixis Upgrade Path

**Priority 1. Card draw**: Blue and black together have the best card draw in Commander. Rhystic Study, Phyrexian Arena (AU$8 to AU$15), Necropotence (AU$10 to AU$25), and Treasure Cruise (AU$1 to AU$3) cover different mana costs and playstyles.

**Priority 2. Interaction**: Counterspell, Force of Will (if budget allows, AU$60 to AU$100+), Lightning Bolt, Dreadbore (AU$2 to AU$5), Go for the Throat (AU$2 to AU$5). Grixis has the best interaction package in any colour combination.

**Priority 3. Ramp**: No green means no creature-based ramp. Artifact ramp is essential: Sol Ring, Arcane Signet, Dimir Signet (AU$2 to AU$5), Izzet Signet (AU$2 to AU$5), Rakdos Signet (AU$2 to AU$5). Three signets are the bare minimum.

**Priority 4. Win condition**: Grixis wins through combat damage, combo, or control. Niv-Mizzet, Parun as a combo engine (AU$4 to AU$10), Thassa's Oracle for lab-man style wins (AU$12 to AU$20), or Kiki-Jiki recursion loops at higher power levels.

### Can I use a Commander precon in tournament play?
Commander preconstructed decks are legal for casual Commander play and official Commander events. The individual cards are legal in Commander, Legacy, and Vintage. The precon as a whole is not competitive at high-level play but works fine for regular Commander nights.
