# Macross Delta Weiss Schwarz Card Guide (Australia)

Macross is one of the longest running and most influential mecha and idol crossover franchises in anime history, and its Weiss Schwarz release focuses specifically on Macross Delta, the entry centred on the idol group Walküre and their music powered battles against the mysterious Var syndrome. The set draws not just from the television anime but from both accompanying films, giving genuine coverage across the full Delta storyline.

## The set at a glance

Macross is represented on this site by a single tracked release, the MACROSS Delta Premium Booster, released 13 December 2024, with a total of 154 cards. The set code is MDE, and as a Premium Booster it follows a more concentrated format than a standard Booster Pack and Trial Deck combination. Each box contains 6 packs, with 3 cards per pack, and every pack guarantees at least one shiny hot stamped card.

The card pool covers 60 base card types split between Normal and DCR, Deculture Rare, rarities, plus 60 foil pressed parallel versions and a further 20 SP special rarity cards. Illustrations were newly drawn specifically for this release by Macross Delta's character designer and chief animation director Masayuki Yoshihara, rather than reusing existing anime stills.

## Deck building around Walküre

Because Macross Delta's central hook is Walküre's music powered combat, the card pool concentrates heavily on the five members of the idol group, giving the set a naturally focused build structure compared to properties with a much wider ensemble cast. This makes Macross approachable for new players, since the core deck building decision is largely about which combination of Walküre members' abilities to prioritise rather than navigating a sprawling cast of unrelated characters.

## Chase cards and signature pulls

This is one of the more distinctive sets on the platform in terms of hit rate. Every single pack guarantees a shiny hot stamped card, and the SP special rarity cards specifically carry a genuine hot stamped signature from the Walküre voice cast members. That guaranteed hit structure makes Macross Delta a strong option if you are looking for a Premium Booster with a reliably high rate of notable pulls, rather than a standard booster where most packs contain only common cards.

Because the set draws on both the anime and its two accompanying films, chase cards span a wider range of iconic scenes and character moments than a release based purely on the television series would offer, giving longtime Macross fans a broader set of nostalgic artwork to chase. This film inclusion is a genuine point of difference from many single source anime sets, which typically draw exclusively from a television run without incorporating cinematic material.

## Why Australian collectors are interested in this set

Macross has one of the most enduring fanbases in mecha anime, with the franchise having run in various forms since the early 1980s. Macross Delta specifically brought a fresh generation of fans into the franchise through its idol and music focused approach, broadening the collector base beyond longtime mecha fans to include idol genre enthusiasts as well.

For Australian buyers, that dual appeal, mecha battles combined with idol group dynamics, gives Macross Delta a genuinely wider potential audience than a set built around just one of those elements alone. Demand has reflected that crossover appeal since release, and it remains one of the more approachable entries in the wider Macross franchise for anyone starting a collection today.

## Why franchise longevity matters here

Macross has been running in one form or another for well over four decades, which is an unusually long span for any anime franchise to maintain relevance across. That longevity is worth understanding as context for this specific release. Delta is a relatively recent entry in a franchise with a very deep back catalogue, and its Weiss Schwarz set benefits from being able to draw on decades of accumulated franchise goodwill even though the card pool itself covers only the Delta storyline specifically.

For collectors newer to Macross as a franchise, this is a useful entry point precisely because it does not require any familiarity with the earlier decades of Macross material. Delta was designed as a largely self contained entry point for new viewers, and its Weiss Schwarz set mirrors that accessibility, letting you collect and build around Walküre without needing prior knowledge of the original 1980s series or its many sequels.

## What to check before buying

1. Confirm whether a listing is the standard foil pressed parallel or the rarer SP signed version, since photos should clearly show the hot stamp signature rather than just describing it in the title.
2. Because this is a Premium Booster with a smaller total card pool than a standard set, compare multiple listings before buying, since fewer total prints can mean more price variation between sellers.
3. Check which Walküre member a card represents if you are collecting around a specific favourite, since the five members each have their own distinct card pool within the release.
