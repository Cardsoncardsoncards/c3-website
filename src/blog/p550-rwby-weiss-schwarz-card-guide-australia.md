# RWBY in Weiss Schwarz: An English Edition Original

RWBY brings Team RWBY, Ruby Rose, Weiss Schnee, Blake Belladonna, and Yang Xiao Long, into Weiss Schwarz, and it holds a genuinely unusual place in the catalogue confirmed directly by Bushiroad, RWBY was released as an English Edition Original rather than translated from an existing Japanese release. If you already play and want the release structure detail, jump ahead. If you are newer to Weiss Schwarz, the primer below covers the core rules, with the full mechanics glossary linked at the end.

## The set at a glance

RWBY spans 2 tracked releases, the original combined launch (17 December 2021) and a Premium Booster (June 2024), totalling 299 cards.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## An English Edition Original, and a Trial Deck and Booster That Do Not Overlap

RWBY's confirmed core cast centres on Team RWBY, Ruby, Weiss, Blake, and Yang, with Jaune Arc, Nora Valkyrie, and Lie Ren from Team JNPR rounding out the confirmed roster. What makes this property genuinely distinctive, confirmed directly by Bushiroad, is that it was produced as an English Edition Original, meaning the cards were created and released in English first rather than translated from an existing Japanese product. On top of that, official product listings confirm the Trial Deck and Booster Pack contain entirely separate cards, cards featured in one cannot be found in the other, making both purchases genuinely necessary for a complete initial collection. This dual confirmation, English-first production plus zero card overlap between the two launch products, is not something you will find stated this explicitly for most other properties in the catalogue, which makes RWBY a genuinely unusual entry from a production standpoint alone.
