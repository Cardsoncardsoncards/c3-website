One Piece Card Game is one of five games featured in Good Games' 2026 Championship Series, with a
share of the combined $3,000 prize pool up for grabs across 20 participating stores. The game's
weekly constructed events, running every Friday night at multiple Good Games locations, have
become a consistent fixture, and they're a fair reflection of how quickly One Piece's competitive
scene has grown in Australia since the game launched.

## A packed run of releases

OP-16, "Paramount War," released 12 June 2026 as the current main set, built around one of the
series' most iconic story arcs and introducing six new Leaders alongside fresh synergy pieces for
existing decks. The next main set, OP-17, "The World's Strongest Warriors," shifts focus to the
Elbaph arc and lands 28 August 2026, meaning the Championship Series will run almost entirely on
the Paramount War card pool before a new set arrives.

In between, six new Starter Decks (ST-31 through ST-36) release 31 July 2026, specifically aimed
at players who haven't picked up the game yet, a sensible entry point if the Championship Series
has you thinking about getting involved but you're not ready to build a full competitive deck from
scratch. There's also a notable one-off: a Weekly Shonen Jump issue released 13 July 2026 carried
an exclusive Monkey D. Luffy promo card marking the manga's 29th anniversary, with the publisher
printing 500,000 extra copies of that issue specifically to meet expected demand, an unusual move
that says something about how much crossover interest exists between the manga and the card game
right now.

Further out, a second Live Action Edition set is confirmed for November 2026, tied to the Netflix
live-action adaptation. Worth noting this one specifically features brand-new cards, not reprints,
covering the Straw Hat Crew and Baroque Works. It's a genuine example of a TV adaptation directly
generating new, purpose-built TCG product, rather than just driving interest in existing cards, a
different pattern from how most media tie-ins usually work.

## What this means if you're playing in the Championship

With OP-16 still the current competitive set and OP-17 not landing until late August, the
Championship Series is running on a fairly stable meta right now, a reasonable window to commit to
a deck without worrying about it becoming outdated mid-way through the series. If you're weighing
up buying into Paramount War staples, [C3's live One Piece market](/cards/onepiece) tracks current
AU pricing, and [Compare](/compare) lets you check multiple sellers before you commit, particularly
useful for Leader cards, which tend to hold their value differently from regular Character cards
given how central they are to a deck's identity.

## A scene that keeps growing

The consistency of Good Games' Friday night events says a lot on its own, weekly constructed
nights only stick around when there's real, sustained demand behind them. Combined with the
Starter Deck push at the end of July, clearly timed to bring in new players around the Championship
announcement, and a promo card tied to the manga's own anniversary landing the same day tickets
went on sale, there's a real sense that One Piece's competitive community in Australia is still in
growth mode rather than settling into a steady state.

Central Sydney's weekly Friday event has run continuously for months, expanding its time window as
turnout has grown, a fairly reliable sign of a healthy, expanding local scene rather than a niche
event struggling to fill seats.

## Getting started if you're new

For anyone thinking about picking up One Piece for the first time ahead of the Championship
Series, the six new Starter Decks landing 31 July are specifically designed as a genuine entry
point rather than a watered-down product, each comes as a full 51-card constructed deck with 10
DON!! cards (the game's core resource system) and a booster pack included, enough to actually
play competitive games from the first purchase rather than needing to buy singles immediately just
to field a legal deck. Good Games' weekly Friday events are generally welcoming to newer players
turning up with a starter deck rather than a fully optimised build, worth checking with your local
store beforehand if you're unsure.

## Looking further ahead

The November Live Action Edition set is worth keeping an eye on well before it releases. New,
purpose-built cards tied directly to a Netflix show are a genuinely different kind of release from
a standard booster set, and if the pattern seen with other crossover products holds, that's exactly
the kind of release that can pull in buyers who aren't regular One Piece TCG players at all, driven
by the show rather than the game itself. It's a similar dynamic to what's played out with Magic's
own licensed crossover sets, outside interest translating directly into card demand, worth watching
closely as the release date approaches.
