## What Is Ikoria: Lair of Behemoths and Why Does It Matter for Australian Players?

Ikoria is set on a plane of enormous monsters where humans have retreated into walled cities called Lairs. The set introduces Mutate (cast a spell on top of another creature to combine their characteristics), Companion (a mechanic allowing specific creatures to be cast from outside the game if your deck meets certain conditions), and Keyword Counters (counters that grant abilities like Flying, Trample, and Deathtouch). The Draft Booster box contains 36 packs. Godzilla Series Monster cards appear at very low rates — approximately one per three retail boxes on average.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What are the Godzilla Series Monster cards?**
The Godzilla Series is a set of 19 alternate art cards featuring Godzilla franchise monsters as their artwork. They are functionally identical to their Ikoria counterparts but use licensed Toho monster artwork — Godzilla himself, Mothra, King Ghidorah, Rodan, Mechagodzilla, and others. They are printed at very low rates and are among the most collected MTG cards from the 2020 era.

**How rare are Godzilla Series cards in Ikoria boxes?**
Godzilla Series cards appear at approximately one per three retail boxes on average — roughly a 33 percent chance of finding one per box. Individual results vary significantly. Some boxes contain none, some contain two or more. Do not purchase Ikoria boxes expecting a guaranteed Godzilla Series pull.

**Is Lurrus of the Dream-Den banned everywhere?**
Lurrus was banned in Modern, Legacy, and Vintage — one of the fastest and most widespread ban sequences for a Companion. It remains legal in Commander and Eternal formats that have not banned it. The bans reduced its competitive price but it retains value as a Commander card and collector piece due to the banning notoriety.

**Are Godzilla Series cards legal in competitive play?**
Yes. Godzilla Series cards are tournament legal and treated as the Ikoria card they correspond to. You can use Godzilla King of the Monsters in any format where Zilortha Strength Incarnate is legal. However, opponents may ask you to clarify which card a Godzilla Series card represents if the card name differs from the Ikoria name.
