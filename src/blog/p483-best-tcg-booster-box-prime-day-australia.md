## How to Judge Whether a Booster Box Deal Is Real

> **The quick EV check:** Look up the top 10 most valuable cards in the set using our [card price tracker](/cards). Add those values together and divide by the total number of booster boxes needed to pull one of each (roughly 3 to 4 boxes for set rates). If the Prime Day box price is below that number, the deal is financially sound. If not, you are buying for the experience, which is fine, but know what you are paying for.

## What to Avoid on Prime Day

- **New releases (within 4 weeks):** These will not be discounted. MTG Marvel (26 June), Chaos Origins (2 July), DBS Masters B31 (3 July) are all too fresh.
- **Pre-release products:** Star Wars Ashes of the Empire prerelease on 10 July is a launch product, not a sale item.
- **Sets with declining community:** If a game has lost its local player base, even a discounted booster box is not worth buying if the singles cannot be moved.
- **Unknown seller pricing:** During Prime Day, third-party sellers sometimes inflate prices before offering a "discount" that just returns to normal RRP. Check prices in the week before Prime Day and note what products are actually at RRP so you can spot real deals.
