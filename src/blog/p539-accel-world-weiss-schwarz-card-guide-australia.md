# Accel World in Weiss Schwarz: Avatars, the Net, and the ACCELERATE Keyword

Accel World brings Haruyuki's Silver Crow and Kuroyukihime's Black Lotus into Weiss Schwarz, built around confirmed 《Avatar》 and 《Net》 character traits and a genuine keyword ability, ACCELERATE, that ties directly into the source material's premise. If you already play and want the mechanics detail, jump ahead. If you are newer to Weiss Schwarz, the primer below covers the core rules, with the full mechanics glossary linked at the end.

## The set at a glance

Accel World spans 2 tracked releases, the original set (19 May 2017) and the Infinite Burst booster follow-up (November 2017), totalling 273 cards.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## Avatar and Net Traits, Plus the ACCELERATE Keyword

Accel World's trial deck material is documented as a yellow and red build, with community deck guides also noting a green splash is viable if needed. The confirmed card text builds synergy around the 《Avatar》 and 《Net》 character traits, with cards able to search the deck specifically for characters carrying one or both traits, on top of a confirmed ACCELERATE keyword ability, where paying a cost by putting the top card of your deck into your clock grants a power and soul boost for the turn. A deck that can consistently trigger ACCELERATE while also running enough Avatar and Net characters to make trait searches worthwhile genuinely gets more value from both systems working together. This dual mechanical focus is part of what makes Accel World feel distinct even within a fairly compact catalogue, rewarding players who understand the underlying systems rather than simply picking the highest-power cards available.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What colours does Accel World use?**
The trial deck material is documented as yellow and red, with a green splash noted as viable by community deck guides if your build calls for it.

**What does the ACCELERATE keyword actually do?**
It is a confirmed keyword ability that lets you pay a cost, putting the top card of your deck into your clock, to gain a power and soul boost for the turn, directly reflecting the source material's premise.

**Is a 2 set catalogue enough to build a complete, functional deck?**
Yes, both the original trial deck and the Infinite Burst booster support full deck construction within the confirmed colour identity and trait system.

**Do older Accel World sets remain legal in tournament play?**
Weiss Schwarz does not use a rotating standard format, so older cards generally remain playable, though always check a specific event's rules before entering.

**What are the Avatar and Net traits for?**
They are confirmed character traits that certain cards search the deck for directly, building around characters that share these traits is a genuine synergy path in this card pool.

**How does the four copy limit affect building around the Avatar and Net traits?**
It reinforces committing to a focused group of characters that share these traits and support the ACCELERATE mechanic, rather than trying to include every named character regardless of how well they combo together in an actual match.
