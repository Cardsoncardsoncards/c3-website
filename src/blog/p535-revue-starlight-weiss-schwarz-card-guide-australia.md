# Revue Starlight in Weiss Schwarz: Stage Girls and the Revue Trait

Revue Starlight brings its Stage Girls into Weiss Schwarz across the original anime release, its -Re LIVE- game adaptation, and Revue Starlight The Movie, all connected by the confirmed 《Revue》 character trait that runs through the card pool's ability text. If you already play and want the trait and release breakdown, jump ahead. If you are newer to Weiss Schwarz, the primer below covers the core rules, with the full mechanics glossary linked at the end.

## The set at a glance

Revue Starlight spans 3 tracked releases, the original anime set (14 June 2019), -Re LIVE- (July 2023), and The Movie (September 2023), totalling 533 cards.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## The Revue Trait as the Mechanical Core

Revue Starlight's confirmed card text builds around the 《Revue》 character trait, with cards regularly checking for other 《Revue》 characters on the board to power up or trigger additional effects. Cards check for other Revue characters to gain power boosts, trigger climax combos, or search the deck, meaning a deck that commits to running multiple Revue characters together genuinely outperforms one that does not lean into the trait. Building around Karen Aijo or another favourite Stage Girl still benefits from surrounding her with other confirmed Revue trait characters rather than picking cards purely on individual strength, placing this property closer to Bang Dream than to a single-character focused build. That trait-first design also means a collection built purely on chasing one favourite character's cards is likely to underperform at the table compared to one built with the wider Revue trait cast in mind from the start.
