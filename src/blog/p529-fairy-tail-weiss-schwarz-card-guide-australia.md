# Fairy Tail in Weiss Schwarz: A Red and Green Guild of Mages

Fairy Tail's Weiss Schwarz card pool is confirmed to lean mainly red and green, built around the guild's core cast of mages. That two colour identity, paired with a genuinely large named cast, gives Fairy Tail a clear starting point for new deck builders while still offering real depth to chase for collectors. If you already play and want the cast breakdown, jump ahead. If you are newer to Weiss Schwarz, the primer below covers the core rules, with the full mechanics glossary linked at the end.

## The set at a glance

Fairy Tail spans 3 tracked releases, ver.E (19 December 2014), 100 Years Quest (13 March 2026), and a Chronicle Set, together totalling 607 cards. Note that our database does not currently record a release date for the Chronicle Set, so we cannot state exactly when it launched, only that it exists as a confirmed, separately tracked release.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## A Genuinely Large Named Cast

Fairy Tail's trial deck material is confirmed to be mainly red and green, giving new players a clear two colour starting point built around guild members like Natsu Dragneel, Lucy Heartfilia, and Erza Scarlet. What stands out is just how many named guild members have their own confirmed card lines beyond that core trio, Gray Fullbuster, Mirajane, Elfman, Wendy Marvell, and Juvia Lockser, giving this property one of the larger named rosters among similarly sized 3 set catalogues. That breadth means Fairy Tail supports genuinely different character-focused builds within its red and green identity, a Natsu and Gray focused build plays differently to an Erza and Mirajane focused one, even though both stay within the same core colour pairing. With this many named characters, the four copy limit is exactly why a deck usually leans on two or three guild members rather than trying to represent the whole roster.
