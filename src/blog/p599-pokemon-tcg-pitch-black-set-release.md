Pokemon TCG Pitch Black released on 17 July 2026, the fifth set in the Mega Evolution era and the
darkest one yet, both thematically and in its artwork. It's built around Mega Darkrai ex, and it
lands with 120 cards across the main set and secret rares. Prerelease events ran 4 to 12 July
ahead of the wide release.

## Six Mega Evolution Pokemon headline the set

Pitch Black features six Mega Evolution ex cards: Mega Darkrai ex, Mega Zeraora ex, Mega
Chandelure ex, Mega Excadrill ex, Mega Delphox ex and Mega Slowbro ex. The set is the English
adaptation of Japan's Abyss Eye, which released back in May. Where the previous set, Chaos Rising,
leaned into open conflict, Pitch Black is framed around a spreading darkness swallowing the game's
world.

## The chase cards to know

Mega Darkrai ex shows up twice at the top of the rarity ladder, as the set's single Mega Hyper Rare
at card 120 and as a Special Illustration Rare at card 116. The Mega Hyper Rare is the gold-tier
pull collectors are chasing hardest, with the Special Illustration Rare close behind as the set's
signature artwork. Mega Zeraora ex's Special Illustration Rare is the strongest non-Darkrai pull,
with more than 35 special illustration cards across the set in total.

## What's releasing alongside it

Launch product includes booster packs, an Elite Trainer Box with a full-art Zarude promo, and Build
and Battle Boxes. The Mega Evolution era isn't slowing down either, Storm Emeralda featuring Mega
Rayquaza ex lands in Japan on 31 July, alongside three MEGA Starter Set ex decks built around Eevee
ex, Zoroark ex and Meowscarada ex for players who want an easier way into the format. The English
version of that set, expected under the name Delta Reign, is currently pencilled in for later in
the year.

## What this means for Australian collectors

New Mega Evolution sets have followed a fairly consistent pattern this era, a strong launch week
followed by pulls cooling off within a month as more packs hit the market. If you pulled something
big, selling early has generally been the stronger play than sitting on it. If you're buying
singles rather than chasing your own pulls, waiting three to six weeks after a set drops has
usually meant paying less for the same card.

## Buying into the set in Australia

Local game stores typically carry Pitch Black alongside the existing Mega Evolution era product,
and the Elite Trainer Box is usually the more predictable purchase if you want a guaranteed slice
of the set without gambling purely on booster packs. Build and Battle Boxes suit players heading
into prerelease-style events at their local store, since they come with enough product to build a
40-card deck on the spot. If you're chasing the Mega Darkrai ex pulls specifically, singles are the
more reliable route than opening product and hoping.

## The bigger picture for the era

Pitch Black is the fifth of six confirmed Mega Evolution sets tied to Pokemon Legends: Z-A, and
2026 also marks the Pokemon TCG's 30th anniversary. A dedicated 30th Anniversary Collection is
confirmed for 16 September, separate from the mainline Mega Evolution sets, so collectors should
expect two distinct release tracks running through the back half of the year rather than one.
