## Quick Answer

Riftbound and Dragon Ball Super Fusion World both launched or relaunched in 2024-2025 in Australia, giving both games similar competitive windows. They differ in IP scale, product availability, and the collector market maturity. This guide compares both for Australian players deciding where to invest.

## IP Scale and Recognition

Dragon Ball Super draws on one of the most globally recognised anime franchises, with Dragon Ball Z remaining a defining piece of pop culture for multiple generations of Australian viewers. The character recognition is near-universal, even among people who have never played the TCG. Riftbound draws on League of Legends, which has a more specific but intensely engaged player base. If you want a game where you can show the cards to any anime fan and get immediate recognition, Dragon Ball wins. If you want a game connected to the game you already play daily, Riftbound wins.

## Product Availability in Australia

Dragon Ball Super Fusion World sealed product is available at Australian TCG stores, some Kmart and Target locations, and through Amazon AU. The distribution network for DBS is broader than Riftbound's at this stage. Riftbound product is available through local game stores and online Australian TCG retailers but does not yet have mass retail distribution. If convenience of purchase matters, DBS has the edge currently.

## Competitive Scene

Dragon Ball Super Fusion World has an established competitive structure with store-level Championship Series events and national-level play. The game is backed by Bandai, which has strong organised play infrastructure from its other games (One Piece, Digimon). Riftbound's competitive scene is smaller but growing with Riot Games direct support. Both are genuine competitive games; DBS has more established infrastructure, Riftbound has a more invested publisher in terms of prize support and event funding.

DBS Fusion World booster box EV follows standard patterns for the game, with chase cards in the Leader alternate and Secret Rare tiers. Check [C3 Riftbound card prices](/cards/riftbound). Both games have comparable box EV structures at current retail pricing.

## Long-Term Outlook

Dragon Ball Super has decades of IP content to draw on and a publisher with a proven record of supporting TCGs long-term (Bandai). Riftbound has an IP with arguably the largest potential collector base in gaming through League of Legends and a publisher who has committed significant marketing spend to the game. Both have strong long-term cases. DBS is the safer bet based on track record; Riftbound is the higher-potential bet if the game captures its potential audience.

## Recommendation

Choose Dragon Ball Super if you are an existing Dragon Ball fan, want broader product availability, or want a more established competitive circuit. Choose Riftbound if you play League of Legends, want early-collector opportunity, or want a game still building its meta where skilled players can gain ground faster.
