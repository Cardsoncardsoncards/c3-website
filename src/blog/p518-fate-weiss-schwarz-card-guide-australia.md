# Fate in Weiss Schwarz: Sub-Series, Colours and the Rin/Archer Build

Fate is one of the more layered properties in Weiss Schwarz, not because it is complicated to play, but because it is actually several distinct sub-series sharing the Fate name rather than one continuous storyline. The card pool draws on Fate/Zero, Fate/kaleid liner Prisma Illya, and other corners of the wider Fate universe, and keeping those sub-series straight matters more here than in most properties. If you already play and just want the archetype detail, jump to the Rin/Archer section. If you are newer to the game, the primer below covers the core rules, and the full mechanics breakdown lives in our [Weiss Schwarz rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## The set at a glance

Fate has a solid but contained footprint in Weiss Schwarz, with 10 tracked releases spanning 16 August 2013 through to the Fate/Zero Chronicle Set in July 2024, totalling roughly 1,450 cards across the full run. That reflects its structure as several distinct sub-series rather than one continuously expanding storyline.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. ## The Rin/Archer Build and Why Sub-Series Matter

The mono-red Rin/Archer build is the clearest documented archetype in Fate's card pool, built around the character pairing from the original Fate/stay night storyline and its Fate/Zero prequel material. This build rewards committing fully to Rin and Archer's card lines rather than splashing in characters from other sub-series, since the strongest synergy comes from staying within that specific pairing's trait lines.

This is where the sub-series distinction becomes genuinely important for collectors. Fate/Zero tells a prequel story with its own cast emphasis, while Fate/kaleid liner Prisma Illya is a spin-off with a different tone and cast entirely, built around Illyasviel rather than the Fate/stay night cast. Treating these as interchangeable is a common mistake, a Prisma Illya card will not necessarily share synergy with a Fate/Zero era card even though both carry the Fate name. Decide which sub-series you actually want to build around before buying singles, since your purchases only pay off if they work together within the same pairing.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Are Fate/Zero and Fate/kaleid liner Prisma Illya the same cards, just under a different set name?**
No. They are distinct sub-series with different casts and tones, Fate/Zero is a prequel to Fate/stay night, while Prisma Illya is a spin-off built around Illyasviel. Do not assume cards from one will synergise with the other.

**What is the Rin/Archer build and do I need specific promo cards to play it?**
It is a mono-red archetype built around Rin Tohsaka and Archer's card lines. You do not need special promos, just a deck committed to that character pairing's trait lines.

**Which Fate sub-series is the best starting point for a new collector?**
If you want the original storyline and the Rin/Archer build, start with Fate/stay night and Fate/Zero era sets. If the spin-off's different cast and tone is the draw, Fate/kaleid liner Prisma Illya is its own separate entry point.

**Do older Fate sets remain legal in tournament play?**
Weiss Schwarz does not use a rotating standard format, so older Fate cards generally remain playable, though it is worth checking a specific event's rules before entering.

## What to check before buying

1. Confirm which Fate sub-series a listing belongs to before buying, since Fate/Zero, Prisma Illya, and Fate/stay night cards share the Fate name but do not necessarily share synergy.
2. Check whether a card is base rarity or a signed variant before paying a premium, since rarity structures differ release to release across this property's 10 sets.
3. If building the Rin/Archer mono-red archetype specifically, confirm cards are drawn from Fate/stay night or Fate/Zero material rather than a different sub-series entirely.
