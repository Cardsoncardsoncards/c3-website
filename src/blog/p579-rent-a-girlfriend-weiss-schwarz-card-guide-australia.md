# Rent-A-Girlfriend Weiss Schwarz Card Guide (Australia)

Rent-A-Girlfriend follows Kazuya Kinoshita, who after a difficult breakup turns to a rental girlfriend service and finds himself tangled in an increasingly complicated web of relationships with Chizuru Mizuhara and the wider cast of women in his life. The series became known for its dramatic, often chaotic romantic entanglements, and its Weiss Schwarz set brings that same reckless energy to the card table.

## The set at a glance

Rent-A-Girlfriend is a single stand-alone set in English, released 18 February 2022, with a total of 171 cards. It has not received a second English wave, so this remains the full available collection for the property. The set code is KNK, and the release featured 100 base card types plus 36 parallel versions, packed 9 cards per pack, 16 packs per display, 18 displays per carton.

## Deck building around Chizuru and the cast

The card pool centres on Chizuru Mizuhara, the rental girlfriend at the heart of Kazuya's story, alongside Mami Nanami, his ex-girlfriend and one of the story's central sources of romantic tension, and Ruka Sarashina and Sumi Sakurasawa, both of whom add further complexity to the show's ensemble of romantic entanglements. Because the story revolves entirely around these overlapping relationships, the card pool rewards building decks that reflect specific character pairings and rivalries from the source material. Kazuya himself, while the story's central protagonist, plays a comparatively smaller role in the card pool than the heroines around him, consistent with how the anime itself frames him more as the connective thread between the women's storylines than as an active driver of the plot.

## Chase cards and signature pulls

Every pack carries a chance of a hot stamped autograph card from the voice cast, including Sora Amamiya as Chizuru Mizuhara, Aoi Yuki as Mami Nanami, Nao Toyama as Ruka Sarashina, and Rie Takahashi as Sumi Sakurasawa. Display boxes included one of four exclusive PR cards, and first edition cartons carried an English Edition exclusive stitched playmat.

Given the show's popularity and the strength of its central cast, particularly Chizuru, signed cards featuring the lead heroines have maintained solid demand on the secondary market since release.

## Why Australian collectors are interested in this set

Rent-A-Girlfriend built a large audience through its unusually dramatic, soap opera style approach to romantic comedy, standing apart from gentler, more conventional entries in the genre. That distinctiveness, combined with a large cast of genuinely popular heroines, has given the card set broad appeal among collectors who enjoy chasing multiple characters rather than concentrating purely on one lead.

For Australian buyers, the show's continued popularity across multiple anime seasons has helped sustain interest in this card set well past its initial release window, in contrast to some single season romantic comedy tie ins that fade quickly once the anime concludes. That ongoing production pipeline is a genuine point in its favour for anyone weighing up long term collecting value in this specific genre.

## Deck building around rivalry and romantic tension

Unlike many harem style romantic comedies where the female cast largely coexists without direct conflict, Rent-A-Girlfriend leans heavily into genuine rivalry and tension between its main heroines, particularly the complicated dynamic between Chizuru and Mami. That narrative tension translates into a card pool where certain character combinations create genuinely interesting synergy and counterplay rather than simply stacking generic support effects.

This gives collectors and players a reason to think carefully about which characters to pair in a deck, since the strongest builds often reflect the same complicated relationships that drive the anime's plot forward. It is a more thoughtful design approach than simply grouping all characters from a show together without regard for their actual relationships to one another.

## Why the show's popularity has extended this set's relevance

Rent-A-Girlfriend has continued producing new anime seasons well beyond its initial run, and each new season brings renewed attention back to the franchise and, by extension, to the Weiss Schwarz set tied to it. This ongoing content pipeline is a meaningful factor in why interest in the card set has not faded the way it might for a show that concluded after a single season with no further instalments.

For collectors weighing this set against other single season romantic comedy tie ins, that ongoing production schedule is worth factoring into your assessment of the property's likely long term demand.

## What to check before buying

1. Confirm whether a listing is base rarity or a signed voice cast version before paying a premium, since photos should clearly show the hot stamp rather than just describing it in the title.
2. Decide which heroine or character pairing you want to build or collect around before buying broadly, given how central the show's romantic rivalries are to the overall card pool structure.
3. Check condition carefully on circulating stock, since the set is now a few years old and some copies show genuine handling wear.
