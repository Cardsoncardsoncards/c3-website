## What Binder Do You Actually Need?

Binders are one of those purchases that looks simple until you realise the 4-pocket vs 9-pocket decision affects whether your entire Pokemon binder is organised correctly or constantly annoying to flip through. This guide cuts through the options.

## 4-Pocket Binders

4 cards per page. Best for displaying large collections where you want each card clearly visible. Popular for full-art cards, V/VMAX Pokemon, and MTG showcase treatments.

**Capacity:** A standard 20-page binder holds 160 cards. Large 40-page versions hold 320.

## 12-Pocket Binders

Highest density. Best for bulk commons and uncommons where display quality matters less than storage efficiency.

## Which to Buy

- **Display collection:** 4-pocket.
- **General collection:** 9-pocket.
- **Bulk storage:** 12-pocket or monster binders.

One important note: always buy side-loading pocket binders, not top-loading. Cards fall out of top-loading binders when you open the pages horizontally. Side-loading solves this completely.
