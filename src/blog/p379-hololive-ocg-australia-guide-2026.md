## What Is the Hololive OCG?

The Hololive Official Card Game (OCG) is Bushiroad's TCG built around Cover Corp's VTuber agency Hololive. Players build decks featuring their favourite Hololive members and compete using those characters' abilities.

The game is designed with accessibility and fan service as primary goals. The rules are relatively approachable, the characters are directly recognisable to anyone who follows Hololive content, and the card artwork features character designs from the agency's official character sheets alongside specially commissioned illustrations.

Unlike most TCGs where you might play a game without knowing the specific characters, the Hololive OCG's entire appeal is built on fan connection to the specific VTubers depicted. If you watch Hololive content, the card game is an extension of that fandom. If you do not, the game loses much of its appeal.

## Most Valuable Hololive OCG Cards

**Limited distribution cards** from Hololive fan events, charity events, and special production runs are the highest-value Hololive cards in Australia. These are not pulled from standard booster product and represent genuine scarcity among the collector community.

**Signed cards (autographed versions)** distributed at select events or through limited promotional channels are the absolute ceiling of the Hololive collector market. Signed cards from popular VTubers trade at extraordinary premiums relative to standard versions.

**SP (Special Parallel) and SR (Super Rare) versions** of popular VTubers command premiums from fans who want premium versions of their favourite characters. The specific VTubers depicted matter enormously: cards featuring Hololive's most subscribed and beloved members (Gura, Korone, Pekora, among others) trade at higher prices than equivalent rarity versions of members with smaller audiences.

**Gen-specific demand** is a notable feature. Hololive is organised into generations (Gen 0, Gen 1, Gen 2, Gamers, HoloEN, HoloID, etc.) and fans often have strong preferences for specific gens. Cards from popular gens with dedicated fan communities hold premiums independent of general demand.

## Entry Cost and Starting the Game

**Trial Decks** for specific Hololive generations or groups cost roughly AU$20 to AU$30 and provide a complete playable deck for that group.

**Booster boxes** run AU$80 to AU$120. Value is concentrated in SR and SP cards of popular members.

**Special and limited products** released in conjunction with Hololive events and anniversaries are targeted at the collector segment and price accordingly.

## Who Hololive OCG Is Best Suited For

**Hololive fans who want a physical connection to their favourite VTubers.** This is the primary audience and the game excels at serving them. The card artwork, character abilities, and lore connections are all designed for people who follow the content.

**Collectors seeking limited-run anime-adjacent collectables.** The Hololive OCG's most valuable cards are genuine limited-distribution items with collector value beyond their game function.

**Casual players looking for an accessible TCG with familiar characters.** The rules are approachable and the characters are beloved, making this a good social play option for friend groups who share Hololive fandom.

## Honest Consideration

The Hololive OCG is not designed to compete with Vanguard, Digimon, or One Piece at the competitive TCG level. Its strength is fan connection, not strategic depth. If you are looking for your primary competitive TCG, this is not that game. If you are looking for a fun experience with characters you already love, it delivers exactly what it promises.

## The C3 Take

The Hololive OCG is one of the most honest TCG products in the market. It makes no pretense of competing with strategic depth games. It is a card game for Hololive fans and it executes that mission well. The card art is excellent, the characters are beloved by their audience, and the collector appeal of limited-distribution cards is real. If you follow Hololive, this game is worth exploring. If you do not follow Hololive, there is no meaningful reason to start with this game over other options with broader community access and deeper competitive play.
