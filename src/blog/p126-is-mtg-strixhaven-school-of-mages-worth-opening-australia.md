MTG Strixhaven: School of Mages released April 2021 and set the template for bonus sheet mechanics that now appear regularly in MTG sets. The Mystical Archive — 63 iconic spell reprints in special alternate art appearing in every single pack — was the first and arguably most impactful bonus sheet implementation in MTG history.

This is the 2021 original Strixhaven set, entirely separate from the 2026 Secrets of Strixhaven release. Both are set on the same plane (the magical university of Strixhaven) but are different sets with different card pools. The Mystical Archive only appears in the 2021 version.

## What Is Strixhaven: School of Mages and Why Does It Matter for Australian Players?

Strixhaven: School of Mages is set at Strixhaven University, divided into five colleges each representing a two-colour pair opposite on the colour wheel: Silverquill (White-Black), Prismari (Blue-Red), Witherbloom (Black-Green), Lorehold (Red-White), and Quandrix (Green-Blue). The Set Booster box contains 30 packs. Every pack contains one Mystical Archive card — a reprint of an iconic instant or sorcery from MTG history in special alternate art. The 63-card Mystical Archive includes Force of Will, Demonic Tutor, Swords to Plowshares, Ancestral Recall (for display only, not tournament legal), and dozens of format staples.

## Chase Cards and What to Look For

The Mystical Archive is the EV driver. Top targets: Force of Will (AU$25-50+ in Mystical Archive treatment), Demonic Tutor (AU$15-30+), Swords to Plowshares (AU$8-18+), Lightning Bolt (AU$5-12+), and Counterspell (AU$5-10+). Japanese alternate art versions of Mystical Archive cards command significant premiums — Japanese Force of Will can be worth AU$80-150+. From the main set: Galazeth Prismari and Velomachus Lorehold are Commander mythics with ongoing demand.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the Mystical Archive?**
The Mystical Archive is a 63-card bonus set of iconic instant and sorcery reprints in special alternate art that appears in every Strixhaven: School of Mages pack. Every single pack contains one Mystical Archive card — commons are worth AU$0.50, format staples like Force of Will are worth AU$25-50+. The wide range creates high EV variance from pack to pack and box to box.

**Is Strixhaven 2021 worth opening in Australia?**
At AU$155, Strixhaven 2021 offers reasonable EV with high variance. The Mystical Archive creates significant upside if high-value cards appear. The floor is low but the ceiling is meaningful. Use the C3 EV Calculator for a current assessment and factor in the high variance when deciding.

**What is different about Strixhaven 2021 versus Secrets of Strixhaven 2026?**
These are entirely different sets set on the same plane. Strixhaven: School of Mages (2021) features the five college rivalry story and introduced the Mystical Archive. Secrets of Strixhaven (2026) is a new set returning to the plane with different cards and a different storyline. The Mystical Archive is exclusive to the 2021 set. Cards from one set are not interchangeable with the other.

**Why are Japanese Mystical Archive cards worth more?**
Japanese-language Strixhaven sets include exclusive alternate art versions of Mystical Archive cards not available in English products. Japanese Force of Will, Demonic Tutor, and other popular Archive cards use entirely different artwork from the English versions and command significant premiums from collectors who prefer the Japanese art. These are only available in Japanese-language booster boxes.
