Magic: The Gathering is one of five games featured in Good Games' 2026 Championship Series,
running across 20 stores nationally with a share of the combined $3,000 prize pool. It's also the
most established tournament game in the lineup, with decades of competitive infrastructure behind
it, and Good Games runs a deep weekly calendar for it outside the Championship too, casual
Commander nights, Modern locals, and constructed formats across multiple locations.

## A busy year for Magic

2026 has been one of the more eventful years for Magic in a while. Secrets of Strixhaven landed
in April as a Standard-legal set, returning players to the world of Strixhaven's five colleges
with fresh mechanics built around student factions. But the bigger story has been the run of
Universes Beyond crossovers. June brought both a Marvel Super Heroes crossover set, flagged across
the hobby press as the single biggest crossover release of the year, and The Hobbit, a
Middle-earth themed set continuing Wizards of the Coast's push into licensed IP alongside original
Magic content.

That pattern, licensed crossover sets landing alongside original-setting releases, has become a
defining feature of Magic's release calendar over the past couple of years. It's also directly
relevant to anyone tracking card values, because crossover sets tend to pull in buyers who
wouldn't otherwise be shopping for Magic cards at all, fans of the source material first, Magic
players second. A Marvel fan who's never played a game of Magic might still want a specific
character card for their collection, and that kind of demand behaves differently from a
traditional competitive-set release.

## The evidence is already in C3's own numbers

This isn't just a theory. Looking back across two years of C3's own eBay sales history, the
Final Fantasy crossover set is the single highest-revenue set C3 has sold, more than 1,000
individual orders and over $7,000 in revenue from that one set alone, well ahead of any purely
original-setting Magic release over the same period. That's a real, direct example of a licensed
crossover driving genuine demand rather than just hype, actual completed sales, from C3's own
records.

With Marvel now added to the crossover list, and more Universes Beyond content clearly part of
Wizards' ongoing strategy, this is a pattern that's likely to keep repeating rather than fade out.
Whether the Marvel set matches Final Fantasy's numbers remains to be seen, but the underlying
mechanism, outside IP pulling in buyers beyond the existing player base, has already proven itself
once in C3's own data.

## What this means if you're playing in the Championship

If you're prepping for a Good Games Championship event, or one of the smaller regional Battles,
the practical question is usually whether to crack open fresh product or buy the specific singles
you need. That's not always an obvious call. Sealed product gives you a shot at bigger value but
no guarantees, while singles get you exactly what your deck needs at a known price.

C3's [Booster Box EV Calculator](/tools) is built for exactly this decision, working out the
expected value of opening a box against current market pricing, so you can see which path
actually makes more sense before you commit. Once you know what you're building toward, the
[live MTG market](/cards/mtg) shows current AU pricing across recent sets, and [Compare](/compare)
lets you check prices across multiple sellers before you buy, rather than settling on the first
listing you find.

## Checking your existing collection

Tournament season is also a natural prompt to look through what you've already got. If you've
picked up staples from older sets that have since rotated out of Standard, or you're holding
Commander pieces you're not using, check [current market movers](/market) to see whether now's a
sensible time to sell before the next big set shifts demand again. Prices on rotated cards can
move in either direction depending on whether they've found a home in Commander or other eternal
formats, so it's not a simple "sell everything old" rule, worth actually checking each card rather
than assuming.

## Beyond the Championship

Outside the five-game Championship structure, Good Games' regular Magic calendar runs Commander
nights as a casual, social format, and Modern as a more competitive weekly option, both good ways
to stay sharp between bigger events. If you're newer to competitive Magic and want a lower-
pressure entry point before a Championship event, a weekly locals night is a reasonable place to
start, and staff at most stores are happy to walk new players through the basics.

With Marvel and Hobbit content still fresh, and more Universes Beyond releases likely through the
rest of 2026, Magic's release calendar shows no sign of slowing down. Whether the current run of
crossovers translates into more moments like Final Fantasy's run at the top of C3's own sales
data remains an open question, but it's exactly the kind of pattern this site will keep tracking
as more data comes in.
