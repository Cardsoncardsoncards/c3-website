# The iDOLM@STER Cinderella Girls Weiss Schwarz Card Guide (Australia)

The iDOLM@STER Cinderella Girls follows a producer tasked with training young idols on their way to stardom, based on the mobile game and anime series from Bandai Namco. It was one of the earliest idol properties to join Weiss Schwarz, and it remains one of the more approachable entry points into the wider idol genre on the platform.

## The set at a glance

Note that the iDOLM@STER franchise has produced several distinct spin off properties over the years, including THE IDOLM@STER Million Live and THE IDOLM@STER SHINY colours, each with their own separate Weiss Schwarz releases. This guide covers only the confirmed set currently tracked in our database, the iDOLM@STER Cinderella Girls, released 18 March 2016 with a total of 266 cards. If you are specifically looking for Million Live or Shiny colours cards, be aware those are separate sets not covered here.

The set code is IMC, and the release followed a distinctive product structure built around the mobile game's colour based idol grouping. It launched as three separate themed Trial Decks, Smile, Heart, and Power, each containing 50 fixed cards built entirely from a single colour, alongside the main Booster Pack and a Meister Set collector's release.

## Deck building around Smile, Heart, and Power

Because the set launched with colour specific Trial Decks rather than a single generic starter, deck building here has a clean, structured entry point. The Smile deck runs red cards and features chase cards signed by Uzuki Shimamura's voice actress Ayaka Ohashi. The Heart deck runs blue cards, and the Power deck completes the trio with its own distinct colour identity and chase cards.

This structure makes iDOLM@STER Cinderella Girls one of the more beginner friendly ways to start collecting and playing within the idol genre specifically, since you can pick a colour based on your favourite idol group within the game and get a coherent, functional deck straight out of the box.

## Chase cards and signature pulls

Each Trial Deck included randomly inserted RRR foil cards, with two varieties per deck, plus a single SP gold foil hot stamp card per deck signed by the relevant Japanese voice actress. The Smile deck's signed Uzuki Shimamura card and the equivalent signed chase cards from the Heart and Power decks remain the primary items collectors chase from this release, alongside further signed pulls available through the main Booster Pack.

The separate Meister Set release added a further premium collector's product on top of the standard Trial Deck and Booster Pack structure, giving dedicated collectors an additional avenue to chase harder to find cards beyond the base release. A full starter kit combining all three Trial Decks, the Meister Set, and ten Booster Packs was also offered at launch, giving new collectors a genuine one stop option to build a broad initial collection across all three colour identities at once.

## Why this property has lasting appeal

The iDOLM@STER franchise as a whole has one of the longest running, most dedicated fan communities in the idol genre, spanning games, anime, and numerous card and merchandise lines over more than a decade. Cinderella Girls specifically carries that same durability, with an established roster of idols that collectors have followed since well before this Weiss Schwarz release existed.

For Australian collectors, that long running fanbase has kept demand for key idol cards, particularly the more popular characters like Uzuki Shimamura and Rin Shibuya, reasonably steady over the years since release, rather than the set fading into obscurity the way some shorter lived licensed properties do. It is a genuinely stable pick within the wider idol collecting category.

## Understanding the wider iDOLM@STER family on this platform

If you are new to collecting iDOLM@STER cards, it is worth understanding upfront that the franchise is genuinely split across multiple, entirely separate Weiss Schwarz releases rather than one continuous card pool. Cinderella Girls, Million Live, and Shiny colours each represent different mobile game and anime spin offs within the wider iDOLM@STER brand, each with its own idols, its own card mechanics, and its own separate secondary market.

This matters practically because a search for "iDOLM@STER" across marketplaces will often return listings from all of these different sub-properties mixed together, and it is easy to accidentally buy into the wrong one if you are specifically chasing Cinderella Girls idols. Always check the set code on a listing, IMC for Cinderella Girls, before assuming a card belongs to the release covered in this guide.

## What to check before buying

1. Confirm you are buying from the Cinderella Girls set (IMC/W41) specifically, since other iDOLM@STER spin offs like Million Live and Shiny colours have entirely separate card pools and could be confused in a quick listing scan.
2. Check whether a listing is base rarity, an RRR foil, or the signed SP gold foil version before paying a premium.
3. Decide which colour, Smile, Heart, or Power, you want to build around before buying broadly, since the three Trial Decks are built as genuinely separate colour identities rather than variations on the same deck.
