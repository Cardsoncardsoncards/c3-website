## What Is Innistrad: Crimson Vow and Why Does It Matter for Australian Players?

Innistrad: Crimson Vow is set during the vampire wedding of Olivia Voldaren, who has forced Sigarda (an angel) into the ceremony. The set introduces Cleave (an alternative cost that removes part of a card's text) and Exploit (sacrifice a creature for a bonus). The Set Booster box contains 30 packs. The Fang Frame showcase treatment applies a gothic vampire aesthetic with ornate decorative borders, blood motifs, and stylised typography. The set pairs with Midnight Hunt as companion sets covering opposite sides of the Innistrad horror theme.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Innistrad: Crimson Vow worth opening in Australia?**
As a settled 2021 set at AU$160, Crimson Vow offers moderate and predictable EV. The Fang Frame showcases and Toxrill as a Commander staple provide the main value. The lower price point makes it more accessible than current standard sets. Use the C3 EV Calculator for a current assessment.

**What is the Fang Frame showcase treatment?**
The Fang Frame is a special card treatment exclusive to Innistrad: Crimson Vow that applies a gothic vampire aesthetic with ornate decorative borders, blood red motifs, and stylised horror typography. Fang Frame versions of popular mythics and commanders command collector premiums above regular card prices in the AU secondary market.

**What makes Toxrill the Corrosive popular for Commander?**
Toxrill is a seven-mana 7/7 that distributes one Slime counter to each creature your opponents control at the beginning of each end step. Creatures with Slime counters get -1/-1. When a creature with a Slime counter dies, you create a 1/1 Slug token and can draw a card. This creates a persistent board state advantage that generates value over multiple turns — exactly the kind of effect Commander players value in a top-end creature.

**Should I buy Midnight Hunt or Crimson Vow?**
Both sets are comparable in EV at similar price points. The choice depends on your Commander preferences. If you play werewolf or zombie tribal, Midnight Hunt has more relevant cards. If you play vampire tribal, Crimson Vow is the better purchase. If you are purely chasing EV and secondary market value, Midnight Hunt has a slight edge due to Teferi Who Slows the Sunset as a broader-appeal staple.
