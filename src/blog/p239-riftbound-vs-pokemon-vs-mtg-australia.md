Every year there are more TCG options. Every year the question gets harder: which one is actually worth your time and money? Here is a direct comparison of the four most actively played TCGs in Australia right now, Pokemon, MTG, One Piece, and Riftbound, across the metrics that actually matter.

## Cost to Start Playing Competitively

- Pokemon: Starter option AU$25-35, competitive deck (singles) AU$150-400+
- MTG: Starter option AU$20-35 (precon commander), competitive deck AU$200-800+ (format dependent)
- One Piece: Starter option AU$20-30 (starter deck), competitive deck AU$80-200
- Riftbound: Starter option AU$25-35 (champion deck), competitive deck AU$80-180

One Piece and Riftbound have the lowest barrier to competitive entry right now. MTG is the most expensive competitive game in Australia, particularly in formats like Legacy and Vintage.

## Collection Value and Investment Potential

**Pokemon:** The established king of TCG investment in Australia. Vintage Base Set cards, sealed product from 2000-2010, and key modern chase cards have shown consistent appreciation. Downside: the market is crowded and most opportunities are already priced in.

**MTG:** Has a Reserved List (cards that will never be reprinted) which creates a genuine long-term collector's market. Power 9 cards are the ultimate store of value in TCG collecting. Modern cards are heavily reprinted, which compresses singles prices.

**One Piece:** Strong collector interest in Manga Rare (SP and SEC) cards. The game is recent enough that early sets still have upside if the game sustains. OP-01 Romance Dawn sealed product has already appreciated meaningfully from original retail.

**Riftbound:** The highest risk and highest potential upside. The game is 7 months old in English. If it grows the way Pokemon did in 2020-2021, driven by an enormous existing IP, early collectors will benefit significantly.

## Game Depth and Competitive Scene

**Pokemon:** Large competitive scene with structured Play! Pokemon events running across Australia. Accessible but deep at the elite level. Standard format rotates annually.

**MTG:** The deepest competitive game in TCG history. Multiple formats each with distinct communities. Australian GP events run multiple times per year.

**One Piece:** Growing competitive scene. Bandai runs Regionals across Australia. Well-balanced and accessible. Simultaneous global releases in 2026 mean Australian players compete with the same card pool as everyone else.

**Riftbound:** Newest competitive infrastructure. RQ Sydney (May 15-17) is the first major Australian event. The competitive scene is forming now, which means you can become a known player before the field expands.

## Casual Playability

**Pokemon:** Excellent for casual play. Kid-friendly, widely understood.

**MTG Commander:** The most popular casual format on earth. 100-card singleton decks, multiplayer, enormous card pool.

**One Piece:** Fast games (15-25 minutes) make it excellent for casual play. Easy to learn, thematically engaging for anime fans.

**Riftbound:** Specifically designed for 1v1, 2v2, and 4-player free-for-all. Games run 20-30 minutes.

## The One You Should Start With

Start with Pokemon if you want the safest investment in terms of established collector market and the widest community in Australia.

Start with MTG if you want the deepest game with the most long-term competitive and casual content, and you are willing to invest more upfront.

Start with One Piece if you are an anime fan, want fast competitive games, and want a lower cost of entry with a growing global scene.

Start with Riftbound if you play League of Legends, want to be early to something with serious upside potential, and are excited by the fact that the Australian competitive scene is literally forming right now in May 2026.
