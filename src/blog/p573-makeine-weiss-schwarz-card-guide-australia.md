# Makeine Too Many Losing Heroines Weiss Schwarz Card Guide (Australia)

Makeine, full title Too Many Losing Heroines, follows a group of girls at Tsuwabuki High School who each lost their romantic pursuits, only to find themselves drawn together and into a new story of friendship and self discovery. The anime carved out a distinctive niche by subverting the typical romantic comedy structure, focusing on the aftermath of failed romance rather than the pursuit itself, and its Weiss Schwarz release captures that same distinctive tone.

## The set at a glance

Makeine is a single stand-alone set in English, released 13 February 2026, with a total of 279 cards. As a very recent release, it has not yet had a second wave, so this one set currently represents the complete Makeine collection available to Australian collectors. The set code is MKI, and the release featured 100 base card types plus 122 parallel versions, packed 8 cards per pack, 12 packs per display, 24 displays per carton.

The Booster Pack features 14 newly drawn illustrations spread across the main cast, with the three central Losing Heroines, Anna Yanami, Lemon Yakishio, and Chika Komari, each receiving three unique illustrations, while supporting characters including Kaju Nukumizu, Koto Tsukinoki, Karen Himemiya, Chihaya Asagumo, and Yumeko Shikiya each receive one.

## Deck building around the Losing Heroines

Because the three central heroines each carry a proportionally larger share of the set's newly drawn illustrations, they naturally anchor the strongest and most build around able card options. Anna Yanami, Lemon Yakishio, and Chika Komari each bring distinct personalities and support structures to the card pool, giving players a genuine choice of three lead characters to build a deck around, backed by a wider supporting cast from Tsuwabuki High School.

This structure suits both dedicated fans of a specific heroine and more casual collectors who want variety, since the set does not concentrate all its strongest cards around a single lead the way some more narrowly focused properties do. Supporting cast members like Kaju Nukumizu also carry genuine narrative weight in the story despite a smaller card allocation, making them worthwhile pickups for collectors following the full ensemble rather than just the three central heroines.

## Chase cards and signature pulls

Every pack carries a chance of a shiny foiled or hot-stamped card from the voice cast, including Hikaru Tono as Anna Yanami, Shion Wakayama as Lemon Yakishio, Momoka Terasawa as Chika Komari, and Minami Tanaka as Kaju Nukumizu. The set includes both SP and rarer SEC secret rare cards carrying these hot-stamped signatures, giving a genuine tiered chase structure across the release. Display boxes also included one of six exclusive Going First and Going Second cards as a box topper.

## Understanding the show's genre subversion

Most romantic comedy anime build toward a resolution, will the protagonist end up with their chosen love interest. Makeine deliberately starts from the opposite premise, with its main characters already having lost, and builds its story from there instead. That structural choice matters for understanding what the card set is trying to capture, since the artwork and character framing throughout leans into resilience, friendship, and moving forward rather than the typical romantic tension and payoff structure other high school romance sets are built around.

For collectors specifically drawn to distinctive, non formulaic anime, this makes Makeine a genuinely different addition to a collection focused on slice of life or romantic comedy adjacent properties, standing apart from the more conventional will they won't they structure that dominates the genre.
