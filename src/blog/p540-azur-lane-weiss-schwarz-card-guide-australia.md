# Azur Lane in Weiss Schwarz: Faction-Based Mono Colour Fleets

Azur Lane brings personified warships into Weiss Schwarz through the confirmed 《KAN-SEN》 character trait, with trial deck material released by faction, Royal, Sakura Empire, Iron Blood, and Union, each as its own mono colour build. If you already play and want the faction breakdown, jump ahead. If you are newer to Weiss Schwarz, the primer below covers the core rules, with the full mechanics glossary linked at the end.

## The set at a glance

Azur Lane spans 2 tracked releases, the original set (27 October 2023) and Vol.2 (October 2025), totalling 850 cards.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## Four Factions, Four Mono Colour Identities

Azur Lane's confirmed trial deck structure is genuinely distinctive, each faction gets its own dedicated mono colour trial deck rather than sharing a single colour identity across the property. The confirmed Royal faction trial deck, featuring named ships like Illustrious, Warspite, Javelin, and Dido, is entirely yellow, while Iron Blood, featuring named ships like Bismarck and Prinz Eugen, has its own separate colour identity. This means choosing a faction is effectively choosing a colour, a genuinely different starting decision to properties built around one shared colour pairing. This is a useful comparison against Kancolle, which also centres on personified warships but settles into a single red and green trial deck identity rather than splitting into separate mono colour factions.

## Chase cards and signature pulls

Confirmed card text shows climax combos built directly around the KAN-SEN trait, for example effects that search the deck for another KAN-SEN character or deal damage based on your level when specific conditions are met. ## Why Azur Lane's collecting angle matters

With 2 sets released, this property's faction-based structure means the practical catalogue is larger in character variety than the raw set count suggests, since multiple factions are represented across those releases. Card numbers are confirmed to share the AZL prefix, meaning trial deck and booster material combine under standard construction. ## Frequently Asked Questions

**Why does Azur Lane have multiple mono colour trial decks instead of one shared colour identity?**
Each faction gets its own dedicated trial deck and colour, confirmed with Royal specifically documented as entirely yellow, reflecting the faction-based structure of the source game.

**What is the KAN-SEN trait and why does it matter?**
It is a confirmed character trait shared across all factions that card abilities check for directly, cards built around it can search for other KAN-SEN characters or scale effects based on how many you have on board.

**Which faction is the best starting point for a new collector?**
That depends on which faction's ships and colour identity appeal most, there is no single correct starting faction given the deliberately separate mono colour design.

**Do older Azur Lane sets remain legal in tournament play?**
Weiss Schwarz does not use a rotating standard format, so older cards generally remain playable, though always check a specific event's rules before entering.

**Should I build a deck mixing ships from different factions?**
Most decks commit to one faction as their core, since KAN-SEN trait synergy and each faction's mono colour identity both reward staying within a single faction rather than mixing across all four.

**How does the four copy limit affect building around a specific faction?**
It reinforces committing fully to one faction's KAN-SEN cast and mono colour identity, since spreading four-of choices across multiple factions would work against both the colour structure and the trait synergy this property is built around.
