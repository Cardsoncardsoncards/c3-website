## What Is Adventures in the Forgotten Realms and Why Does It Matter for Australian Players?

Adventures in the Forgotten Realms is a standard-legal set replacing the annual Core Set slot for 2021. The Draft Booster box contains 36 packs. The set introduces the Dungeon mechanic — a persistent journey through one of three Dungeons (Dungeon of the Mad Mage, Lost Mine of Phandelver, Tomb of Annihilation) that provides bonuses at each room. The Venture into the Dungeon mechanic triggers progression. Classic Module Art showcase cards present key creatures and spells in the art style of original 1970s-1980s DnD module covers — black and white ink illustrations with colour highlights.

**What are Classic Module Art showcase cards?**
Classic Module Art showcases are special versions of key cards that use the art style of original Dungeons and Dragons adventure modules from the 1970s and 1980s — black ink illustrations with selective colour, in the style of early DnD publications. They are collector favourites for both MTG players and DnD collectors who recognise the tribute to the original game's visual history.

**Is the DnD crossover theme popular in Australia?**
Yes. The DnD and tabletop gaming community in Australia is substantial and the Forgotten Realms IP is well-recognised. The crossover attracted buyers who had never purchased MTG products before specifically because of the DnD connection. This extended buyer pool has sustained demand for the set's thematic cards beyond the regular MTG player audience.

**How does Acererak the Archlich work as a combo piece?**
Acererak enters the battlefield and bounces itself to your hand unless you have completed a Dungeon. In Legacy, this is exploited with Acererak plus creatures that allow multiple enter-the-battlefield triggers — Acererak bounces repeatedly, venturing into Dungeons multiple times until completing the Tomb of Annihilation and dealing lethal damage to opponents. It is banned in multiple formats but remains legal in Legacy where the combo is the foundation of dedicated decks.
