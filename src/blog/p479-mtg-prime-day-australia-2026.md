## What MTG Products Are Worth Targeting on Prime Day?

Prime Day is best for MTG product that is 3 to 12 months old and sitting at or near RRP on Amazon AU. Here is where the value tends to be:

- **Play Booster Boxes from sets released in 2025:** Amazon AU often moves these at a discount during Prime Day. Formats like Pioneer and Modern use cards from multiple sets, so older sealed product still has player demand even after rotation.
- **Commander Precon decks:** These age well in price terms and Amazon AU stocks many. A Prime Day deal on a Commander precon that retails at AU$60 to $80 is worth taking if you have been sitting on one.
- **Bundle products:** MTG Bundles (30 packs plus promo card) are regularly discounted during Prime Day. Good value for players who want packs without committing to a full booster box.
- **Collector Boosters from older sets:** These rarely go below RRP outside of sale events. Prime Day is one of the few times Amazon AU will move them at a discount.

## MTG Commander: Is Prime Day Worth It for EDH Players?

Commander is the most played format in Australia and the best format for Prime Day buying. Here is why:

Commander players typically want specific cards from multiple sets rather than cracking a single set for the experience. That makes singles buying preferable. But Commander players also love Commander precon decks, which are predictably priced, frequently discounted during sales, and make excellent gifts. If you are an EDH player or buying for one, Commander precon decks on Prime Day are a reliable value pick.

The other good Commander buy on Prime Day is older booster boxes from sets with strong Commander staples. If a set from 2024 or 2025 has 5 to 6 cards with sustained Commander demand, the booster box often represents better value than buying the singles individually, especially if you plan to open it regardless.
