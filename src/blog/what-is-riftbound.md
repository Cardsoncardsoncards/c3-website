## What Is Riftbound?

Riftbound is the official League of Legends Trading Card Game developed by Riot Games. It is built around the champions, items, and regions from the League of Legends universe — one of the most played PC games in the world with a player base that dwarfs any existing TCG's player count.

This guide explains what the game is, how it plays, and what Australians need to know before buying product.

## Why It Matters

League of Legends has an estimated 150 million registered accounts globally. Even a small conversion rate into the TCG represents enormous potential market size. For comparison, Pokemon has roughly 35 to 40 million active players across its TCG.

The question for Australian buyers is whether Riot can execute on distribution and organised play locally.

## How It Plays

Riftbound uses a champion-centric system where each player builds a roster of champions with abilities tied to the LoL game mechanics. The combat system is designed to feel intuitive for existing LoL players — attacks, abilities, and spell interactions map to familiar game concepts.

Turn structure: draw, summon, attack, resolve abilities. Closer to Lorcana in complexity than MTG.

## What to Buy First

At launch, the core set Starter Kit is the right entry point. It contains two pre-built decks designed to teach the game to new players. Booster boxes arrive with the first expansion.

## Should You Buy at Launch?

If you play League of Legends or collect: yes. Launch product for a major IP-backed TCG with this level of publisher backing historically holds or appreciates in value within 12 months.

If you want to play competitively: wait for the organised play structure to be confirmed in Australia before investing heavily.
