## Quick Answer

The [C3 Collection Tracker at /tracker](/tracker) is a free downloadable Google Sheets template that tracks your TCG collection across eight games with automatic value calculations. This guide explains how to set it up, log your first cards, and use it to make smarter buying and selling decisions.


## Downloading and Setting Up

Go to [C3 Collection Tracker](/tracker) and download the free tracker. In Google Sheets, go to File > Make a Copy to save it to your own Google Drive account. Making a copy ensures you have your own editable version that does not overwrite the template. The tracker covers MTG, Pokemon, Lorcana, One Piece, Yu-Gi-Oh, Dragon Ball Super, Star Wars Unlimited, and Riftbound across separate tabs.

## Logging Your First Cards

In each game's tab, enter card name, set, quantity, condition (NM/LP/MP), and your purchase price. The tracker has a current value column that you update periodically from [C3 MTG card prices](/cards/mtg) or the relevant game hub. The profit or loss column calculates automatically from your purchase price versus current value. Start with your 20 most valuable cards across all games to establish the baseline.

## Updating Card Prices

Update current prices monthly for most collectors and weekly for active buyers and sellers. For each price update, check the relevant game hub at [C3 MTG card prices](/cards/mtg) or the corresponding hub for your game, then search each card name and enter the current AU market price. The tracker's summary tab shows total collection value, total investment, and total profit or loss after each update. Tracking this over time shows whether your collection is growing or shrinking in value.

## Using the Wishlist Tab

The tracker includes a wishlist section where you can log cards you want with priority ranking and budget. When you have cash available to buy new cards, sort the wishlist by priority and budget to identify the highest-value purchase for your current spending limit. Cross-reference wishlist items with current prices at the game hubs to confirm you are paying a fair price before buying.

## Before Selling a Card

Before listing any card for sale, update its current price in the tracker and check the profit or loss. This tells you exactly how the sale compares to your original investment and whether now is the right time to sell or whether holding makes more sense. Cards with significant appreciation are worth checking against the [Market page at /market](/market) to see if the current price is near a peak or still rising.

## Sharing and Backing Up

The Google Sheets format means your collection is automatically backed up in Google Drive. Share the tracker with a family member or fellow collector by clicking Share in Google Drive and entering their email. Sharing is useful for couples who build a collection together or for players who want a second set of eyes on their collection valuation.
