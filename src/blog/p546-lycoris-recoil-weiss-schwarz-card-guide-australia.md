# Lycoris Recoil in Weiss Schwarz: Chisato, Takina and Café LycoReco

Lycoris Recoil brings Chisato Nishikigi and Takina Inoue's partnership into Weiss Schwarz, built around confirmed 《LycoReco》 and 《Weapon》 character traits that directly reward running the pair, and the wider Café LycoReco cast, together on the board. If you already play and want the partnership mechanics, jump ahead. If you are newer to Weiss Schwarz, the primer below covers the core rules, with the full mechanics glossary linked at the end.

## The set at a glance

Lycoris Recoil spans 2 tracked releases, the original Booster Pack (22 March 2024) and a Premium Booster (June 2025), totalling 323 cards.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## The Chisato and Takina Partnership as the Mechanical Core

Lycoris Recoil's confirmed core cast centres on Chisato and Takina, with Mizuki, Kurumi, Majima, Mika, and Shinji rounding out the confirmed roster from the Café LycoReco setting. What defines this card pool is how directly its strongest cards are built around the Chisato and Takina partnership rather than either character working independently. Confirmed card text shows the "Time for Work♪ Chisato & Takina" level 3 combo card and its supporting climax both checking for the same condition, 2 or more other LycoReco or Weapon characters present, meaning the deck's power ceiling is tied to how well you support the partnership with the wider cast rather than how many copies of Chisato or Takina alone you run. This makes the supporting cast functionally necessary rather than optional flavour, a genuinely different design philosophy to properties where one or two star characters can carry a deck largely on their own, and a useful contrast to the trait-based synergy seen in properties like Bang Dream or Konosuba.
