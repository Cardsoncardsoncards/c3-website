# Dandadan Weiss Schwarz Card Guide (Australia)

Dandadan is one of the freshest additions to the English Weiss Schwarz catalogue, and one of the most anticipated. Based on the manga that has sold over 3.2 million copies, and the anime adaptation that quickly became one of the most talked about shows of its season, the set covers the occult and youth story of Momo Ayase, Okarun, Aira Shiratori, and the alarming Turbo Granny.

## The set at a glance

Dandadan is a single stand-alone set in English, released 30 January 2026, with a total of 312 cards. As one of the newest properties on the platform, it has not yet had a second wave, so this one release currently represents the complete Dandadan collection available to Australian collectors. The set code is DDD, based on the first season of the anime, with 12 newly drawn illustrations featuring Momo, Okarun, Aira, and Turbo Granny.

The Booster Pack contains 104 base card types plus 141 parallel versions, packed 8 cards per pack, 12 packs per display, 24 displays per carton. A Trial Deck was also released alongside it, giving new players a 50 card starter option with its own set of shiny and hot-stamped chase cards.

## Deck building around Momo and Okarun

The card pool is built around the core cast's occult battles, with Momo's psychic abilities and Okarun's alien powered transformation forming the mechanical backbone of most decks. Aira Shiratori, one of the breakout characters of the anime's first season, also has a strong presence in the set, and Turbo Granny features as one of the more visually striking and mechanically distinctive cards for collectors chasing scene accurate artwork.

Because this set is based specifically on the first season of the anime, the card pool stays tightly focused on that opening arc rather than spreading across the wider manga, which keeps the available archetypes and synergies coherent for a new set.

## Chase cards and signature pulls

Every Dandadan pack carries a chance of a shiny foiled or hot-stamped card, including signed cards by voice cast member Ayane Sakura in her role as Aira Shiratori. Display boxes also included one of eight exclusive Going First and Going Second cards as a box topper, and cartons carried an English Edition exclusive stitched playmat.

As a genuinely new release, Dandadan pricing is still settling. Early secondary market activity has shown strong demand for the Aira signature cards in particular, reflecting her popularity from the anime's first season, but as with any brand new set, expect prices on chase cards to move more than they would on an older, more established release.

## Why Australian collectors are watching this set closely

Dandadan arrived with a genuinely large existing fanbase from the manga, and the anime adaptation broadened that further, pulling in viewers who had never read the source material. That combination of an established readership plus a strong new anime audience is exactly the kind of setup that has historically produced strong, sustained demand for a Weiss Schwarz property, rather than a short burst of hype that fades once the initial release window passes.

For Australian buyers, this is a set worth watching in its first year rather than assuming today's prices are the final word. New releases with strong source material popularity often see continued growth in demand as later seasons of the anime air, so early movers who buy in confidently on solid research tend to do better than those who wait for prices to fully settle.

## Comparing Dandadan to other recent shonen and seinen releases

Dandadan sits in a crowded field of recent Weiss Schwarz releases based on breakout modern manga and anime, alongside properties like Chainsaw Man and Frieren. What sets it apart is the genre blend at the heart of the source material, part horror, part comedy, part romance, which has given the anime a wider crossover audience than a straight shonen battle series typically reaches. That broader appeal is worth factoring in if you are deciding how much of your collecting budget to put toward a brand new release versus a more established property.

The trade off with any set this new is volatility. Prices on Dandadan singles, particularly the Aira Shiratori signature cards, have moved quickly since release and could continue to shift as more of the anime airs and the print run circulates more widely. If you are buying to hold rather than to play, treat early pricing as a starting point rather than a settled market, and revisit your valuation as the set matures over its first year.

## What to check before buying

1. Confirm whether a listing is base rarity, a numbered parallel, or the signed Aira Shiratori version, since Dandadan has a wide 141 card parallel spread and pricing between tiers can vary significantly.
2. Because this is a very recent release, check seller feedback and photos carefully. Freshly opened stock should be genuinely near mint, and any wear should be reflected clearly in the asking price.
3. Watch for a second wave or expansion announcement. If Dandadan follows the pattern of other hit anime properties, a follow up set covering later story arcs is a real possibility, which could affect demand for first season cards.
