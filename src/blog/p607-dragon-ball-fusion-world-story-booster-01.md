Dragon Ball Super Card Game Fusion World is trying something new with Story Booster 01 [ST01],
releasing in Japan on 8 August 2026 and in English on 21 August. Rather than a standard numbered
expansion, it's built around a "story" concept, recreating iconic anime moments through all-foil
cards with anime-style backgrounds rather than the usual card frame.

## What makes Story Booster different

Every card in ST01 is foil, a departure from the mix of foil and non-foil most Fusion World sets
use, and the illustrations are built to look like scenes lifted directly from the anime rather than
standard card art. The set includes a new Leader card with the ability to place a Field at the start
of the game, a mechanic Bandai hasn't detailed in full yet beyond confirming it exists. At 85 total
card types, it's a smaller release than a full numbered booster, split across 5 Leaders, 36
Commons, 20 Rares, 10 Super Rares, 2 Secret Rares and 12 Energy Markers.

## Collector focused, not just a player set

The all-foil, anime-scene approach positions Story Booster 01 closer to a collector product than a
pure competitive release, similar in spirit to how other games use premium or anniversary boosters.
That doesn't mean it's irrelevant competitively, the new Field-placing Leader is a genuine new
mechanic, but expect the set's chase appeal to lean heavily on the alternate art and foil treatment
rather than raw power level alone.

## Where this sits in Fusion World's busy August

Story Booster 01 lands in the middle of a genuinely active month for the Core Dragon Ball game.
Store Championship events run through 31 July, an Ultimate Battle promotion runs through 16 August,
and Fusion World has a presence at Gen Con from 30 July to 2 August. If you're playing competitively
rather than just collecting, that's a lot of in-store activity to work Story Booster 01's release
around.

## What this means for Australian players

The five to six week wait between the Japanese and English release is a good window to watch how
the new Field-placing Leader performs in Japan before deciding whether to prioritise this set once
it's available locally. If you're mainly collecting rather than competing, the foil-only,
scene-based art style is the real drawcard here, and that appeal doesn't depend on the meta at all.

## How this compares to a normal numbered booster

Fusion World's main line runs through numbered FB sets, with Cross Force (FB10) as the most recent
mainline release in June 2026. Story Booster products sit alongside that main line rather than
replacing it, similar to how other TCGs run premium or anniversary boosters as a side release. If
you're building a competitive deck, keep following the numbered FB sets as your primary source of
new staples, and treat Story Booster 01 as a supplementary pickup for the specific Leader and any
Field-related tech it introduces.

## The wider anniversary trend across TCGs

Story Booster 01's all-foil, scene-based approach fits a pattern seen across other anime TCGs this
year, where established games mark milestones with premium, collector-leaning products rather than
simply numbering another mainline set. Weiss Schwarz's BanG Dream! 10th Anniversary Premium Booster
follows the same logic. For Fusion World specifically, this is a signal the game is mature enough
now, more than two years into its run, to support a side release built purely around presentation
rather than needing every product to expand the competitive card pool.
