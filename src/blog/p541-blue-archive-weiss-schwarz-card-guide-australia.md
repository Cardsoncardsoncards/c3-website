# Blue Archive in Weiss Schwarz: Kivotos on Two Fronts

Blue Archive brings the mobile game's cast into Weiss Schwarz, built around the confirmed 《Kivotos》 character trait named after the setting itself. If you already play and want the mechanics detail, jump ahead. If you are newer to Weiss Schwarz, the primer below covers the core rules, with the full mechanics glossary linked at the end.

## The set at a glance

Blue Archive currently has one released set, the original game-based release (21 February 2025), totalling 358 cards. A second release, Blue Archive The Animation, is announced for 31 July 2026 and had not released yet as of this writing, so it is not covered by this guide until it is out.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## Trial Deck and Booster Pack Designed to Combine

Blue Archive's confirmed core cast includes Shiroko, Hoshino, Ayane, Serika, Nonomi, and Arona, all documented via the trial deck's own voice cast credits. A genuinely useful confirmed detail is that the original trial deck was released to combine directly with a Booster Pack launching the same day, both sharing the BAV card number prefix, meaning a new player is expected to build from both releases together from day one rather than starting with the trial deck alone. For deck builders, this also means the confirmed Kivotos trait synergy is designed to be supported from the very first purchase rather than something you grow into later.

## Chase cards and signature pulls

Confirmed card text shows a climax combo, "et omnia vanitas," that grants a chosen ability to a character with 2 or more other Kivotos characters present, either battle protection or a card retrieval effect. Beyond this documented combo, signature and rarity structures vary within the release, so check the listings on the property page below for current detail.

## Why Blue Archive's collecting angle matters

With one confirmed set released, Blue Archive is currently one of the more approachable properties to collect in full compared to franchises that have accumulated many more sets over a longer publishing history. Starting with the trial deck and its paired Booster Pack gives the most complete card pool currently available, and since both were designed to launch together, there is no partial-release gap to work around the way there sometimes is with properties where booster material trails the trial deck by months. ## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the Kivotos trait and where does the name come from?**
Kivotos is the name of the setting in Blue Archive, confirmed as a character trait that card abilities check for directly, rewarding decks that run multiple Kivotos characters together.

**Do I need to buy both the trial deck and the Booster Pack to build a proper deck?**
The trial deck is confirmed to be designed for combination with the same-day Booster Pack release, so buying both gives the most complete starting card pool.

**Is Blue Archive The Animation available to buy yet?**
Not at the time of writing. It is announced for 31 July 2026 but had not been released, so it is not covered by this guide until confirmed data is available.

**What does the "et omnia vanitas" climax combo do?**
It is a confirmed climax combo that grants a chosen character either battle protection or a card retrieval ability, provided you have 2 or more other Kivotos characters present.

**Do older Blue Archive sets remain legal in tournament play?**
Weiss Schwarz does not use a rotating standard format, so older cards generally remain playable, though always check a specific event's rules before entering.

**How does the four copy limit affect a deck built from the trial deck and booster material?**
It applies across your full combined pool from both releases, meaning careful choices about which four-of characters to prioritise matter as much here as in any single-release property.
