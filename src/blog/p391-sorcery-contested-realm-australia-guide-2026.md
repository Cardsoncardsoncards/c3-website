## What Is Sorcery: Contested Realm?

Sorcery: Contested Realm is an independent TCG published by Erik Olofsson and the Sorcery team. It is notable for several reasons that distinguish it sharply from other TCGs in the market:

**The art direction** is unlike any other TCG. Sorcery commissioned and curated artwork deliberately evoking the classic fantasy illustration tradition, featuring landscapes, creatures, and characters rendered in a painterly style that references the aesthetic of 1970s and 1980s fantasy art. The cards look like paintings.

**The gameplay** is a strategic deep game where players build sites (land-like cards with permanence) and summon Avatars and minions to contest control of those sites. The territory control mechanic creates games with genuine positional strategy.

**The card stock and production quality** are premium. Like Grand Archive, Sorcery is produced in limited quantities with premium materials that make the physical cards genuinely excellent objects.

**The thematic design**, the world of Sorcery is original and deeply constructed, with faction identities (Earth, Air, Water, Fire) that have real internal logic and aesthetic coherence.

## Most Valuable Sorcery: Contested Realm Cards in Australia

**Unique rarity cards** are the ceiling of the Sorcery secondary market. Unique cards pull at extremely low rates (approximately one per case or less) and feature the most powerful and visually premium cards in the game. Near Mint Unique cards of flagship Avatars trade at AU$100 to AU$300 or more in Australia.

**Exceptional rarity cards** are the high-rarity tier below Unique. These appear more frequently than Uniques but are still genuinely scarce from standard product. Exceptional versions of competitively relevant Avatars and Sites trade at AU$40 to AU$150.

**Alpha print run cards** from the game's initial Kickstarter and first print run have collector scarcity premiums. Like Magic: The Gathering's Alpha and Beta prints, the first edition of a premium limited-print TCG holds special collector status.

**Site cards** are unique to Sorcery among modern TCGs and some high-power Site cards in Exceptional rarity trade at meaningful prices because of their universal gameplay relevance across many deck types.

## Entry Cost and Starting Sorcery

**Starter Decks** are available for each of the four elements at roughly AU$20 to AU$30. These provide a complete deck for learning the game.

**Booster boxes** are more expensive than most TCGs at AU$150 to AU$200 due to the premium production costs and limited print runs. Value is concentrated in Exceptional and Unique cards.

**Singles** are the most efficient purchasing path. The Sorcery community actively trades through Discord and eBay AU.

## The Australian Sorcery Community

Sorcery has a small but deeply engaged Australian community. The game attracts a different type of player than most TCGs, often adults with experience in multiple TCGs who are specifically seeking premium game quality, serious strategy, and beautiful card art.

Events run in major Australian cities through community organisation rather than publisher-direct infrastructure. The Sorcery Discord is the primary community hub and Australian players are active there.

## Who Sorcery Is Best Suited For

**Players who want the best card art in any TCG.** If visual quality of the physical objects matters to you, Sorcery is the benchmark.

**Strategic TCG players who want deep territory control gameplay.** The Site-based positional system creates games different from any other mainstream TCG.

**Premium collectors.** The limited print runs and genuine production quality make Sorcery cards collectible objects beyond their game function.

**Adults looking for a serious TCG experience.** Sorcery's community skews older and more experienced. The conversations about game design, strategy, and collection at Sorcery events tend to be at a high level.

## Honest Consideration

Sorcery's community in Australia is small and requires active seeking. The game's premium price point (both for product and for singles) is higher than most extended TCGs. And the original IP means you are investing in a world and characters with no prior cultural recognition.
