# Girls Band Cry Weiss Schwarz Card Guide (Australia)

Girls Band Cry follows Nina Iseri and the members of the bands Togenashi Togeari and Diamond Dust as they navigate the messy, emotional reality of trying to make it as a band. The anime stood out for its distinctive full CG animation style and its unusually raw, unfiltered take on the music anime genre, and its Weiss Schwarz release reflects that with a more specialised product format than most licensed anime sets.

## The set at a glance

Girls Band Cry is represented on this site by a single tracked release, the Girls Band Cry Premium Booster, released 23 January 2026, with a total of 208 cards. Unlike most Weiss Schwarz sets, this was released as a Premium Booster rather than a standard Booster Pack and Trial Deck combination. Each display contains 6 packs, with 4 cards per pack, and every pack guarantees at least one foil-stamped card, a notably higher hit rate than a typical booster release.

The set code is GCR, and the release featured 28 newly drawn illustrations across a total of 69 base card types plus 79 to 80 parallel and foil-stamped versions, depending on the specific print count referenced by different retailers.

## Deck building around Togenashi Togeari and Diamond Dust

The card pool covers both of the anime's central bands, giving collectors and players a choice between building around Togenashi Togeari, Nina Iseri's group, or Diamond Dust, their rival and eventual collaborator group. Because the whole release is presented as a single Premium Booster rather than being split across multiple products, the full competitive card pool for this property sits within this one release, similar to other one-set properties on the platform.

## Chase cards and signature pulls

This is one of the more distinctive sets on the platform in terms of signature card structure. The SP cards feature genuine character autograph style signatures from the voice cast members of both Togenashi Togeari and Diamond Dust, while a separate SEC+ card carries hot-stamped signatures from the series creators themselves, including series director Kazuo Sakai and character designer nari Teshima. Having creator signatures on a card, rather than only cast signatures, is unusual for a Weiss Schwarz release and adds a genuine collector angle beyond the standard voice actor autograph chase.

First edition boxes also included a bonus MyGO!!!!!! PR card as part of a cross campaign with that property, a detail worth checking for if you are specifically chasing first edition exclusive extras rather than just the core Girls Band Cry cards.

## Why Australian collectors are interested in this set

Girls Band Cry built a passionate niche audience thanks to its unusual visual style and its willingness to portray band life with genuine friction and emotional messiness rather than a polished, idealised version of the music industry. That distinctiveness has translated into strong collector interest in the card set specifically, particularly around the creator signature SEC+ card, which is a genuinely rare kind of chase item on this platform.

For Australian buyers, the Premium Booster format itself is worth understanding before buying in. Because every pack guarantees a foil hit, the entry cost to pull something notable is lower than with a standard booster pack, but the overall card pool is also smaller, which can affect long term price stability compared to a full sized set. That trade off suits collectors who want a concentrated, high hit rate product over one that spreads value across a much larger number of common cards.

## Premium Booster sets versus standard releases

Weiss Schwarz occasionally uses the Premium Booster format for properties where a smaller, more concentrated release makes sense, typically music or idol focused shows with a compact main cast rather than a sprawling ensemble. Girls Band Cry fits that pattern well, since the story centres tightly on two bands rather than a wide cast of supporting characters spread across a larger narrative.

For collectors, the practical difference is that a Premium Booster release tends to sell out its print run more predictably than a standard set, since the smaller total card count and guaranteed foil rate make it an easier product for a retailer to gauge demand for ahead of time. That can mean less ongoing restocking once the initial run sells through, so if you are planning to build a complete set rather than just picking up a handful of favourite characters, it is worth acting within the first year or so of release rather than assuming supply will remain freely available indefinitely.

## What to check before buying

1. Confirm whether a listing is the standard SP character signature version or the rarer SEC+ creator signature card, since the two carry very different value despite both being described as signed.
2. Check whether a listing is first edition with the MyGO!!!!!! cross campaign PR card included, if that bonus matters to your collection.
3. Because this is a Premium Booster with a smaller total card pool than a standard set, compare multiple listings before buying, since fewer total prints can mean more price variation between sellers.
