# To Loveru Darkness Weiss Schwarz Card Guide (Australia)

To Loveru Darkness follows Rito Yuki and the alien princess Lala Satalin Deviluke, along with an ever expanding cast of extraterrestrial and human love interests, in one of the longest running romantic comedy harem franchises in anime and manga history. Its Weiss Schwarz set brought that colourful cast of characters directly onto the card table.

## The set at a glance

To Loveru Darkness is currently represented on this site by the To Loveru Darkness 2nd release, released 13 January 2017, with a total of 157 cards. The set code is TL, and the release featured 100 base card types plus 18 parallel versions, packed 8 cards per pack, 20 packs per display, 16 displays per carton. In English, this property received a Trial Deck and this Booster Pack, for two total releases. Note that a further Japanese only Booster Pack Vol.2 exists for this same Darkness 2nd storyline, but it was never released in English and is not currently tracked in our database. This guide covers the confirmed English release only.

## Deck building around Rito, Lala, and the cast

The card pool draws on the wide ensemble that defines the To Loveru Darkness franchise, with Lala Satalin Deviluke, Yami, Mikan Yuki, Momo Deviluke, Nana Deviluke, Yui Kotegawa, and Mea Kurosaki among the characters featured across the set. Because the source material is fundamentally a harem comedy built around a large cast of love interests, the card pool rewards building decks around specific character groupings that reflect the show's own complex web of relationships, rather than concentrating power around a single lead.

## Chase cards and signature pulls

Every pack carries a chance of a foil stamped card from the Japanese voice cast, giving collectors a genuine chase structure spread across the show's large ensemble rather than concentrated on just one or two central figures. Because To Loveru Darkness is an older release now nearly a decade past its original print run, secondary market pricing has settled into a fairly predictable pattern, making it a reasonably approachable set to price check and buy into with confidence.

## Why this property still resonates with collectors

To Loveru as a franchise has one of the longest continuous runs in the harem romantic comedy genre, spanning multiple manga series, anime adaptations, and spin offs over more than a decade. That longevity has built a genuinely dedicated, long standing fanbase that has continued collecting merchandise and cards tied to the franchise well past the initial excitement of any single release.

For Australian collectors, To Loveru Darkness sits alongside other established, older harem and romantic comedy properties as a steadier, nostalgia driven pick rather than a fast moving speculative set. Demand has remained reasonably consistent over the years since release, reflecting the franchise's enduring niche appeal rather than a short lived burst of hype.

## Understanding a franchise with a complex release history

To Loveru's Weiss Schwarz history is a good example of why checking set codes carefully matters. The Darkness 2nd storyline alone spans three separate Japanese releases, a Trial Deck and two Booster Packs, but only two of those, the Trial Deck and the first Booster Pack, ever received an English localisation. This is a common pattern with older, long running franchises on the platform, where the Japanese release calendar moves faster and further than what eventually gets brought to English speaking markets.

For collectors specifically chasing a complete To Loveru collection, this means being clear about which market and which specific release you are targeting. English collectors are working from a smaller confirmed pool than Japanese collectors, and treating listings from the untracked Japanese only Vol.2 as interchangeable with this English release would be a mistake, since the card pools and pricing between the two are genuinely separate.

## A long running franchise with steady niche demand

Unlike shorter lived seasonal harem comedies that rise and fade with a single anime season, To Loveru has sustained a dedicated audience across manga serialisations and multiple anime seasons stretching back well over a decade. That sustained fan investment has given this particular Weiss Schwarz release a kind of durability that newer, single season tie ins have not yet had the chance to prove.

## What to check before buying

1. Confirm whether a listing is base rarity or a signed voice cast version before paying a premium, since photos should clearly show the foil stamp rather than just describing it in the title.
2. Check the TL set code against the card number on any listing, since the wider To Loveru franchise has multiple releases across its history and it is worth confirming a card belongs to this specific Darkness 2nd set.
3. Check condition carefully on circulating stock, since this is now an older release and some copies show genuine handling wear from nearly a decade in circulation.
