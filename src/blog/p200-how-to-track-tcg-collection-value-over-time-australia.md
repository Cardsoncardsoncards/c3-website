Most TCG collectors know approximately how much they've spent on cards. Very few know what their collection is currently worth. The gap between those two numbers is often significant. and knowing it changes how you think about buying, selling, and holding.

This guide covers how to track TCG collection value over time using free tools available in Australia.

## Why Tracking Matters

**Know when to sell**: a card you bought for AU$15 may now be worth AU$40. Without tracking, you don't know until you happen to look it up.

**Understand your actual spending**: the AU$50 you spent on a booster box looks different if the cards are now worth AU$35 or AU$65.

**Make smarter buying decisions**: if your collection is already valued at AU$2,000 and you're considering adding another AU$200 booster box, tracking gives you the context to evaluate that decision.

**Tax record keeping**: if you sell cards at significant volume, records of purchase and sale prices are relevant for tax purposes.

## The C3 Free Collection Tracker

The [C3 free collection tracker](/tracker.html) is a Google Sheets template covering 7 TCGs. Download it, make a copy to your own Google Drive, and you have a permanent tracking system.

**What it tracks:**

- Current market value (you update this periodically from price sources)
- Profit/loss per card
- Total collection value
- Wishlist with priority ranking

**How to use it for value tracking over time:**

Log your cards once. Update the "current value" column monthly (spot-check 5 to 10 cards against eBay AU sold listings). The profit/loss column shows movement automatically.

This approach takes approximately 20 minutes per month to maintain for a collection of 100 to 300 cards.

For the most valuable cards in your collection (say, cards worth AU$20+), update prices monthly. For bulk cards under AU$5, update quarterly.

## Manabox (MTG-specific app)

Manabox is a free MTG collection tracking app with camera scanning functionality. Point your phone at a card and it identifies it and logs it. Prices are USD (US market) rather than AUD, so you'll need to convert: but for fast cataloguing of a large collection, it's faster than manual entry.

After cataloguing with Manabox, cross-reference the top-value cards against eBay AU sold for accurate AUD prices.

## TCGdex (Pokemon and Other TCGs)

TCGdex is a free database API covering Pokemon TCG and other games. Not a collection tracker per se, but provides card data for building your own tracking system.

## Building a Simple Google Sheets Tracker Yourself

If you prefer to build your own, a basic collection tracker needs:

| Column | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Card Name | Identifier |
| Set | Distinguish printings |
| Quantity | How many copies |
| Condition | NM/LP/MP/HP |
| Purchase Price | What you paid |
| Current Price | Updated periodically |
| Profit/Loss | Formula: Current - Purchase |
| Notes | Foil, promo, etc. |

The "Current Price" column is updated manually from eBay AU sold data or the C3 Card Vault. The Profit/Loss column is a simple subtraction formula.

## How Often to Update

**Weekly**: if you're actively buying and selling. Keep it current with each transaction.

**Monthly**: if you're a casual collector. Spot-check your 10 most valuable cards.

**Quarterly**: if you're primarily holding and rarely transacting. Full update every 3 months gives you a broad picture.

**Before selling**: always update the price of any card you're about to list or take to a buylist. Selling based on outdated prices is the most common collection management mistake.
