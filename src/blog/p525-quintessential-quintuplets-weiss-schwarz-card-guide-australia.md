The Quintessential Quintuplets brings a genuinely distinctive design idea into Weiss Schwarz, confirmed via Bushiroad's own official deck recipe article, each of the five Nakano sisters has a distinct playstyle rather than sharing one generic sister archetype. ## The set at a glance

The Quintessential Quintuplets spans 4 tracked releases, from the original set (19 November 2021) through the Specials Premium Booster in January 2025, totalling 871 cards.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## Five Sisters, Five Distinct Playstyles

What makes this property stand out is that Bushiroad's own official deck recipe material treats each of the five Nakano sisters as a genuinely separate build path rather than variations on one shared archetype. Ichika's documented "Surprise Approach" climax combo is the clearest named example, built around timing a climax card play for maximum effect. Choosing a sister is the first real decision here, closer to picking a character-focused build in a property like Fate than to picking a shared trait line the way you might in Bang Dream. With five sisters each carrying a distinct playstyle, the four copy limit reinforces committing to one sister's cards rather than trying to build a deck that represents all five equally.
