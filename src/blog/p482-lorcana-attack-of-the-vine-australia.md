## What Is Attack of the Vine?

Attack of the Vine is the next expansion set in the Disney Lorcana Trading Card Game. Lorcana launched in 2023 and has maintained consistent retail presence in Australia, with product widely available at JB Hi-Fi, EB Games, Target, and Amazon AU. It is one of the few TCGs with genuine mass-retail support in Australia, which affects both availability and pricing.

Lorcana sets typically include booster packs, booster boxes, starter decks, and gift sets. The game uses an ink system rather than colours or mana, with each card belonging to one of six ink types. Competitive Lorcana is growing in Australia with regular Organised Play events at participating stores.

## Prime Day and Attack of the Vine: Can You Do Both?

Yes, and this is important to understand. Prime Day closes 13 July. Attack of the Vine prerelease is 17 July. These are four days apart. You can spend during Prime Day on older Lorcana product (sets from 2024 or early 2025) and then separately buy Attack of the Vine at launch on 17 or 24 July. There is no conflict.

What does not make sense is waiting for a Prime Day deal on Attack of the Vine, because it will not exist. Prime Day will have passed before the set releases.

## Lorcana Card Prices: What to Watch

Lorcana singles prices tend to spike in the first week after a set release as the community discovers which cards have competitive value. The best strategy for a competitive player is to hold off on singles purchases for 7 to 10 days after launch, then buy once the market has settled.

For collectors, enchanted rarity cards are the main chase in Lorcana sets. These are alternate-art versions of regular cards with foil treatment. They are pulled at low rates from booster packs and often hold or increase in value after release. If collecting enchanteds is your goal, sealed product at launch is the main path.
