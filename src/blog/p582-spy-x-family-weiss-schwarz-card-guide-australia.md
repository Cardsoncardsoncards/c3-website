# Spy x Family Weiss Schwarz Card Guide (Australia)

Spy x Family follows Loid Forger, an elite spy, Yor Forger, an assassin, and Anya Forger, a telepathic child, as they form a fake family to complete a covert mission, each hiding their true identity from the others while genuine familial bonds slowly form. The series became one of the biggest anime hits in years following its 2022 debut, and its Weiss Schwarz set has been one of the most consistently popular releases in the current English catalogue.

## The set at a glance

Spy x Family is a single stand-alone set in English, released 26 January 2024, with a total of 218 cards. It has not received a second English wave, so this remains the complete Spy x Family collection currently available. The set code is SPY, and the release featured 100 base card types plus 53 parallel versions, packed 9 cards per pack, 16 packs per display, 18 displays per carton.

The Booster Pack features 9 newly drawn illustrations across the core Forger family and supporting cast, with Loid Forger, Anya Forger, and Yor Forger each receiving 2 unique illustrations, alongside single illustrations for Sylvia Sherwood, Becky Blackbell, and Fiona Frost.

## Deck building around the Forger family

The card pool centres on the three central Forgers, with Loid's spy craft, Yor's combat prowess as an assassin, and Anya's telepathic abilities each providing genuinely distinct mechanical identities within the deck. This gives collectors and players a real choice of which family member to build around, or how to weight support across all three, reflecting the show's own balance between its three very different lead characters.

Supporting cast members including Sylvia Sherwood, Becky Blackbell, and Fiona Frost round out the wider deck building options, giving the set solid depth beyond just the three central Forgers.

## Chase cards and signature pulls

Every pack carries a chance of a sign card from the voice cast, including Takuya Eguchi as Loid Forger, Atsumi Tanezaki as Anya Forger, Saori Hayami as Yor Forger, and further signatures across Sylvia Sherwood, Becky Blackbell, and Fiona Frost. Notably, this release includes SPYR, a special rarity created specifically for this set alongside the usual SEC and SP rarities, a genuinely unique addition not shared with other Weiss Schwarz properties. Display boxes included one of six exclusive PR cards, and first edition cartons carried an English Edition exclusive stitched playmat.

Tournament Store Assist PR Packs were also made available, each containing one of six types of shiny PR cards, redeemable with the purchase of a display, giving competitive players an additional way to chase exclusive cards beyond standard retail purchases.

## Why this property continues to draw collectors

Spy x Family became a genuine cultural phenomenon following its debut, spawning a second anime season and a theatrical film in quick succession, a release pace that reflects just how much ongoing demand the franchise has generated. That rapid, sustained content output has kept the card set firmly in the spotlight since its release, in contrast to properties that rely purely on nostalgia to maintain interest.

For Australian collectors, that ongoing content pipeline, plus the crossover appeal of a story that blends spy thriller, family comedy, and genuine heart, has made Spy x Family one of the more actively traded sets in the current catalogue. It is a set worth following closely rather than treating as a settled, static market.

## A property specific rarity worth understanding

Weiss Schwarz very rarely creates a rarity tier exclusive to a single property, which makes the SPYR rarity found in this set a genuine point of distinction. Most properties on the platform work within the standard rarity structure shared across the whole game, so a set receiving its own bespoke rarity tier signals just how much commercial confidence the publisher had in Spy x Family at the time of this release.

For collectors, this means SPYR cards carry a kind of exclusivity that goes beyond the usual SP or SEC chase structure found elsewhere in the catalogue. If you are building a complete high rarity collection for this set specifically, factor in that SPYR cards are a distinct category worth researching separately rather than assuming they slot into the same pricing tier as a standard SEC card from another property.

## What to check before buying

1. Confirm whether a listing is standard SP, the property specific SPYR rarity, or the rarer SEC secret rare, since these carry meaningfully different value despite superficially similar descriptions.
2. Check which Forger family member a card represents if you are building around a specific character, since Loid, Yor, and Anya each carry genuinely distinct mechanical identities.
3. Given the franchise's continued strong momentum with new seasons and films, monitor pricing trends rather than assuming a snapshot price is stable, since renewed anime attention has historically driven fresh demand spikes.
