# Overlord in Weiss Schwarz: Nazarick's Floor Guardians on the Card

Overlord's Weiss Schwarz catalogue is built around Nazarick, the tomb turned kingdom at the centre of the series, and its cast of Floor Guardians led by Ainz Ooal Gown. This post sticks closely to what live card data actually shows rather than assuming details that are not confirmed. If you already play and want the character detail, jump ahead. If you are newer to Weiss Schwarz, the primer below covers the core rules, with the full mechanics glossary linked at the end.

## The set at a glance

Overlord's confirmed catalogue centres on two core sets, Nazarick: Tomb of the Undead (25 September 2020) and its Vol.2 follow-up (1 September 2023), together totalling 351 cards. There are also two Vol.3 releases, an Extra Booster and a Premium Booster, both dated 15 May 2026, but our data currently shows only a handful of sealed-product listings for each rather than individual character singles, so we are treating those as sealed product releases for now rather than a confirmed third set of singles.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## Deck building around Ainz and the Floor Guardians

With a genuine roster of named Floor Guardians to choose from, the four copy limit is exactly why an Overlord deck is built by picking which guardians you want to commit to rather than trying to include the whole cast at four copies each. Ainz Ooal Gown anchors the card pool as the series' central figure, with his line providing the clearest deck building starting point.

One thing worth being upfront about: this property's specific colour identity and named archetype have not been confirmed in the data available to us, unlike properties such as Bang Dream or Konosuba where a documented trait archetype exists. If you are deck building competitively, check the specific colour and level printed on each card directly rather than assuming a fixed colour pairing.

## Chase cards and signature pulls

Live card data confirms a real and varied cast at the centre of this catalogue, led by Ainz Ooal Gown. Alongside him, the confirmed pool includes Albedo, Shalltear, Cocytus, Aura, Demiurge, and Lupusregina, all Floor Guardians drawn directly from the series' central cast, plus supporting characters including Nabe, Brain, Climb, and Crusch. Several of these, including Ainz, Albedo, Shalltear, Aura, and Cocytus, appear across multiple rarity levels within the confirmed sets, giving collectors real depth to chase rather than a shallow single-print run.
