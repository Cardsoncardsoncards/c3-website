## Why Australian Release Dates Matter

TCG products do not always release in Australia on the same day as the US or Japan. Several factors affect Australian availability:

**Shipping and distribution lead times**: Physical product must be shipped to Australian distributors and then to retailers. For major releases, Australian game stores typically receive stock within days of the US release. Smaller or limited releases may take longer.

**Regional distribution agreements**: Some products are distributed through different channels in Australia than in the US. Amazon AU may have product available before or after local game stores depending on the distributor.

**Prerelease events**: Most TCGs run prerelease or launch events at local game stores one week before the mass retail release date. These events let players crack packs early and play sealed games. The local game store date and the mass retail date are different for most major sets.

**Amazon AU availability**: Amazon AU does not always list products at the same time as Australian game stores receive them. For in-demand products, Amazon AU often sells out quickly and may not restock for weeks.

## Understanding Prerelease vs Mass Retail

Most major TCG sets follow a two-stage release:

**Prerelease (local game store only)**: One week before the main release. Players purchase prerelease kits and open packs at store events. Product is only available at participating local game stores, not online or at mass retail.

**Mass retail release**: The official set release date when product becomes available at game stores, department stores (Target, Big W), and online (Amazon AU, eBay).

If you want to play with the new cards as early as possible, find a local game store running prerelease events. If you just want product and don't care about day-one play, the mass retail date is the relevant date.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How far in advance are Australian release dates confirmed?
For major games like MTG and Pokemon, release dates are typically announced 2 to 3 months in advance. Riftbound dates have been confirmed 4 to 6 weeks ahead. Smaller games may announce with less lead time.

### Are Australian release dates always the same as US release dates?
Not always. For major MTG and Pokemon sets, the Australian mass retail date matches the US date. For some special products or newer games, there can be delays of days to weeks.

### What is the difference between a prerelease pack and a booster box?
A prerelease pack contains a fixed number of booster packs (typically 4 to 6) plus promo cards and event materials. It is designed for use at prerelease events. A booster box contains a larger number of packs (usually 24 to 36) and is the standard sealed product purchase.

### Does Amazon AU always have new releases in stock on launch day?
Not always. For high-demand releases, Amazon AU stock can sell out on launch day or even during the pre-order period. Set up a pre-order early for any release you know you want sealed product from.
