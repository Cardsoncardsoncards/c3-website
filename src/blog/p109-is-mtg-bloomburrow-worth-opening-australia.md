## What Is Bloomburrow and Why Does It Matter for Australian Players?

Bloomburrow is set on a plane of small animal communities living in a world scaled to their size — a mushroom is a landmark, a pond is a lake. The set introduces Gift and Offspring as new mechanics. Creature type synergies across the various animal races drive strong Commander demand for tribal deck builders. The Bloomburrow showcase treatment uses a distinctive nature-art style that has attracted collector interest. The Play Booster box contains 36 packs with 14 cards each.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Bloomburrow worth opening in Australia?**
At AU$180, Bloomburrow offers reasonable EV for a 2024 standard set. The Commander demand for tribal synergies and the collector interest in showcase treatments sustain prices above average for its release window. Use the C3 EV Calculator for a current personalised assessment.

**Why does Bloomburrow have no humans?**
Bloomburrow is set on a plane where the dominant intelligent species are small animals — mice, frogs, rabbits, birds, and otters — rather than humanoid races. The design choice was intentional to create a distinct plane identity. It is the first MTG set without any human creature cards.

**What tribal decks does Bloomburrow support?**
Mouse, Frog, Rabbit, Bird, Otter, and Bat tribal archetypes all have dedicated payoffs in Bloomburrow. Mouse and Frog are the strongest competitively. All six archetypes have Commander support making the set broadly useful for Commander players building or upgrading tribal decks.

**How do Bloomburrow showcase treatments compare to other sets?**
The Bloomburrow showcase uses a nature illustration style inspired by children's book art. It is distinctive and has attracted collector interest from people who are not regular MTG players. The showcase mythics command premiums of AU$5-20+ above the regular card price in the AU secondary market.
