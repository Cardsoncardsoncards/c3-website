Weiss Schwarz had a genuinely busy week. Two English sets released on 17 July 2026, Umamusume:
Pretty Derby and the BanG Dream! 10th Anniversary Premium Booster, and two more properties are
close behind, Blue Archive The Animation and an Extra Booster for Nazarick: Tomb of the Undead
both land on 31 July.

## Why Weiss Schwarz works this way

Unlike most TCGs, Weiss Schwarz isn't one card pool with expansions, it's dozens of separate
licensed properties, each running its own booster releases on its own schedule. Bushiroad currently
supports over 69 distinct properties across the English and Japanese lines, from long-running
anime staples to newer additions each season. That means "what's new in Weiss Schwarz" is really a
question about which specific properties are getting fresh support, not one single release
calendar.

## Umamusume: Pretty Derby joins the English line

Umamusume: Pretty Derby is one of the higher profile new titles to join Weiss Schwarz English this
year, following the same pattern the property took in Bushiroad's Shadowverse back in 2023. The
horse-girl racing franchise has a large existing fanbase, and its first Weiss Schwarz booster gives
English collectors a way into the property without needing to import Japanese product.

## BanG Dream! hits ten years

The BanG Dream! 10th Anniversary Premium Booster is a milestone release for one of Weiss Schwarz's
longest-running and most consistently supported properties. Premium Boosters typically carry a
higher hit rate and more premium foil treatments than a standard booster, which fits a set built to
celebrate a decade of the franchise's support in the game.

## Blue Archive and Nazarick land 31 July

Two more releases are just eleven days out. Blue Archive The Animation brings the popular mobile
game's anime adaptation into Weiss Schwarz for the first time, while the Nazarick: Tomb of the
Undead Extra Booster adds a third volume of support for the Overlord franchise. Both are worth
watching if you collect either property, since a first English booster for a new anime adaptation
tends to draw stronger early demand than a routine follow-up set.

## How the English catalogue keeps growing

Bushiroad runs regular English Conference announcements, most recently the Winter 2026 conference
in February, to confirm which new properties are joining the English Weiss Schwarz line each
season. Umamusume and the Blue Archive debut both trace back to announcements made at these
conferences months before the actual booster hits shelves, so keeping an eye on conference
announcements is generally the earliest signal of what's coming to a property you collect.

## What this means for Australian collectors

If you collect a specific Weiss Schwarz property rather than the game broadly, the practical move
is checking which of your properties has a release landing this month rather than trying to track
the whole game at once. C3 covers Weiss Schwarz property by property, including a dedicated
[Weiss Schwarz hub](/cards/weissschwarz) plus individual property pages, so you can follow just the
franchises you actually collect.

Pricing on brand new property debuts like Umamusume and Blue Archive tends to be volatile in the
first few weeks as the market works out what's actually in demand, so it's worth checking before
committing to a big purchase either way.

## Should you import or wait for English product

Once a property has confirmed English support, the local English booster is usually the simpler
and cheaper option. Import shipping and exchange rates on Japanese product tend to erase any price
advantage, and English boosters generally arrive within weeks of the Japanese release for actively
supported franchises like Umamusume and BanG Dream. Importing still makes sense for properties that
haven't been announced for English release, but check the conference announcements first.
