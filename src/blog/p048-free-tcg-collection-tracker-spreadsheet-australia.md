## How to Get the Most Out of It

### Enter Cards When You Acquire Them

The tracker is most useful when it's current. The discipline that makes a collection tracker valuable is simple: when you get new cards (booster box opening, trades, purchases), enter them before they go into storage. Two minutes at the time of acquisition is much easier than reconstructing what you pulled from a box three weeks later.

### Keep Purchase Prices Accurate

The profit/loss calculation only works if your purchase prices are accurate. If you bought a box for AU$150 and pulled a card worth AU$80, log the card's purchase price as your cost allocation from that box — some people use a proportional allocation; others simply log what they'd pay for the card as a single today. Either approach is consistent as long as you apply it uniformly.

### Update Values Monthly for High-Value Cards

Card values shift. A monthly value update for your highest-value cards (AU$20+) takes 15–20 minutes and keeps your total collection value accurate. For bulk cards, quarterly is sufficient. Update immediately when you hear about a specific card spiking or crashing.

### Use the Wishlist Actively

The wishlist is most useful when you consult it before buying. Before opening eBay or attending a game store, check your wishlist. Buying a card that's on your wishlist at the right price is more satisfying and more financially sound than impulse purchasing cards you happen to come across.

Mark wishlist priorities honestly — a card you "might use someday" has a different priority than a card you need for a deck you're actively building. This distinction prevents the wishlist from becoming a hoarding list.
