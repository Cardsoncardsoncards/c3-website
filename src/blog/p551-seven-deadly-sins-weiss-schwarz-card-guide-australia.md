# The Seven Deadly Sins in Weiss Schwarz: Meliodas and the Britannia Trait

The Seven Deadly Sins brings Meliodas, Elizabeth, and the founding cast into Weiss Schwarz, built around a confirmed 《Britannia》 character trait that ties the whole card pool together. If you already play and want the character and combo breakdown, jump ahead. If you are newer to Weiss Schwarz, the primer below covers the core rules, with the full mechanics glossary linked at the end.

## The set at a glance

The Seven Deadly Sins spans 2 tracked releases, the original set (6 August 2021) and Revival of the Commandments (December 2022), totalling 349 cards.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## The Britannia Trait as the Mechanical Foundation

The confirmed original trial deck features Meliodas, Elizabeth, Hawk, Diane, and Gilthunder, the characters who appear early in the source material. What ties the property together is the confirmed 《Britannia》 character trait, with cards scaling their power based on how many other Britannia characters you have in play and searching the deck specifically for Britannia characters when placed on the stage. This gives a genuine mechanical throughline across both confirmed sets, meaning characters introduced later in Revival of the Commandments, like Escanor and Merlin, still combo with the original trial deck's Britannia trait cards. With confirmed named characters spanning Meliodas, Elizabeth, Hawk, Diane, Gilthunder, and later additions like Escanor and Merlin, the four copy limit is exactly why a deck typically commits to Meliodas as the central figure supported by a smaller group of allies, rather than trying to represent the whole confirmed cast evenly. This is a genuinely collector-friendly structure, since it means an early purchase decision does not lock you out of later expanding into the same deck's core mechanics.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the Britannia trait and why does it matter?**
It is a confirmed character trait that card abilities check for directly, cards scale their power based on how many other Britannia characters are in play and can search the deck for more of them.

**Which characters are in the original trial deck?**
Meliodas, Elizabeth, Hawk, Diane, and Gilthunder are confirmed as the characters featured in the original trial deck.

**Do characters from Revival of the Commandments combo with the original trial deck?**
Yes, since both releases share the confirmed Britannia trait, later characters like Escanor and Merlin combo with original trial deck material rather than existing in a separate mechanical system.

**Do older Seven Deadly Sins sets remain legal in tournament play?**
Weiss Schwarz does not use a rotating standard format, so older cards generally remain playable, though always check a specific event's rules before entering.

**Should I build purely around Meliodas or include the wider Britannia cast?**
Meliodas is the natural central figure given his documented card depth, but the confirmed Britannia trait rewards running a supporting group of other Britannia characters alongside him rather than a Meliodas-only approach.

**How does the four copy limit affect building around the Britannia trait?**
It reinforces choosing a focused group of Britannia characters to run at four copies each, since the trait's searching and scaling effects work best with a deliberate spread of characters rather than a single dominant card repeated with no support.
