# Avatar: The Last Airbender in Weiss Schwarz: Four Elements, Four Colours

Avatar: The Last Airbender brings Aang, Katara, and Team Avatar into Weiss Schwarz as an English Edition Original, and Bushiroad's own deck recipe material confirms one of the most direct colour-to-theme mappings in the whole catalogue, water, earth, fire, and air line up exactly with the four Weiss Schwarz colours. If you already play and want the mechanics detail, jump ahead. If you are newer to Weiss Schwarz, the primer below covers the core rules, with the full mechanics glossary linked at the end.

## The set at a glance

Avatar: The Last Airbender is a single stand-alone set, released 16 June 2023, with a total of 203 cards. It has not received a second release since, so this remains the complete Avatar collection currently available.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## A Genuine Four-Colour Payoff Card

Avatar's confirmed card pool builds around the 《World of Avatar》 character trait, with cards checking for other World of Avatar characters to trigger power boosts, searches, and card draw, alongside a genuine one-to-one mapping between the four classical elements and the four in-game colours. Confirmed via Bushiroad's own deck recipe material, "Aang: Master of All Elements" carries a climax combo that gets stronger for each of the four elements you have mastered, meaning this card's power scales with genuine colour diversity rather than staying within one or two colours. This is a genuinely unusual design choice, most properties never build a payoff around spreading across all four colours at once, and it means Avatar supports two distinct approaches, a conventional one or two colour build leaning into Katara's documented power growth, or a genuine four colour build chasing Aang's element-scaling combo.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do the four elements map onto Weiss Schwarz's colours?**
Confirmed via Bushiroad's own deck recipe material, water, earth, fire, and air each correspond directly to one of the four Weiss Schwarz colours, and Aang's climax combo tracks how many of these you have represented.

**Is Avatar another English Edition Original like RWBY?**
Yes, confirmed via official product listings, Avatar: The Last Airbender was released as an English Edition Original rather than translated from an existing Japanese product.

**Should I build a focused two colour deck or chase the four colour Aang payoff?**
Both are supported, a conventional build leaning into Katara's power growth works within one or two colours, while the four colour approach specifically rewards spreading across all four to maximise Aang's climax combo.

**Do older Avatar sets remain legal in tournament play?**
Weiss Schwarz does not use a rotating standard format, so cards generally remain playable, though always check a specific event's rules before entering.

**What does the World of Avatar trait actually do?**
It is a confirmed character trait that cards check for directly, triggering power boosts, searches, or card draw when you have other World of Avatar characters in play.

**How does the four copy limit affect a deck chasing the four colour Aang payoff?**
It still applies per card as normal, but building toward Aang's element-scaling combo means your four-of choices need to be spread more deliberately across colours than a typical focused deck would require.
