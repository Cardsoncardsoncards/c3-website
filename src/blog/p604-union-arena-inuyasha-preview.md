Union Arena's InuYasha booster (UE23BT) is confirmed for 14 August 2026, and it's already being
called one of the most anticipated English releases the game has had this year. It draws from both
the original Inuyasha anime and Inuyasha: The Final Act, and it's a genuinely different kind of
release for Union Arena, since the game builds each booster around a single anime franchise rather
than expanding one shared card pool.

## Who's in the set

The lineup leads with Inuyasha, Kagome Higurashi, Sesshomaru and Naraku, recreating the journey to
collect the shards of the Shikon Jewel. Bandai has confirmed 11 card types with illustrations made
specifically for Union Arena, meaning these aren't reused anime stills but new artwork exclusive to
the card game.

## The chase card format is unusual

The set's SR★★ and SR★★★ cards carry foil-stamped autographs from the Japanese voice cast printed
directly onto the card, alongside a limited AP card with its own serial number. That's a different
chase card model to what most other TCGs use, since the value here comes from the voice actor
signature itself rather than purely from rarity or competitive strength. Collectors chasing a
specific actor's autograph should expect these to behave more like signed merchandise than a
typical secret rare.

## How Union Arena's release model works

Unlike a core game with expansions, Union Arena releases IP-specific boosters at a near-monthly
pace, more than 20 English sets since the line launched with Jujutsu Kaisen in November 2024.
InuYasha is the last confirmed main release for August, with Attack on Titan Vol. 2 following in
September and a NIKKE Precious Booster in October. Titles like BLEACH Vol. 3, Gurren Lagann and
Umamusume: Pretty Derby have also been announced for the "coming soon" list, so the release
schedule isn't slowing down.

## What this means for Australian collectors

If you already collect another Union Arena property, InuYasha is worth watching purely for how it
performs relative to other recent single-IP boosters, since early competitive results tend to set
expectations for how quickly a new franchise settles into the format. If you're an InuYasha fan
first and a card game player second, the voice-cast autograph cards are the ones worth budgeting
for specifically, they're likely to hold value differently than the rest of the set once the
initial hype settles.

## Getting the set locally

Local game stores that already carry Union Arena typically stock new IP boosters like InuYasha
alongside whatever the previous set's leftover stock is, so check with your usual store about
launch day availability rather than assuming national stock will be even. Because each Union Arena
release is tied to a single franchise rather than a shared card pool, picking up InuYasha doesn't
require owning any previous sets, it plays as a self-contained release even if you've never touched
Union Arena before.

## Why the autograph angle is worth understanding

Voice-cast autograph cards are a relatively new chase card concept even within Bandai's own TCG
lineup, and they change how a set should be valued compared to a normal rarity ladder. A card's
long-term price is likely to track the popularity of the specific voice actor and character
alongside the usual factors of print run and demand, rather than competitive strength alone. That's
worth keeping in mind if you're deciding whether to grade a pull or hold it raw, since signed
merchandise and trading card grading don't always follow the same collector logic.
