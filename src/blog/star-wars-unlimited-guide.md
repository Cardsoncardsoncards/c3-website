## Star Wars Unlimited: What Australian Buyers Need to Know

Star Wars Unlimited launched in March 2024 and is one of the few new TCGs to achieve genuine organised play traction in Australia in its first year. Fantasy Flight Games (Asmodee) backs it, distribution is solid through major AU retailers and Amazon AU, and the gameplay is genuinely well-designed.

## How It Plays

Star Wars Unlimited uses a base-centric system. You control a base that takes damage — when your base reaches zero, you lose. Leaders are your central characters and flip between a horizontal "epic action" mode and an upright deployed unit mode.

The resource system uses any card as a resource (played face-down), which means there is no concept of being "mana-screwed." Every card has a second use as a resource. This is one of the most player-friendly design decisions in any current TCG.

## The Sets

**Spark of Rebellion (SOR):** Set 1. The format-defining set. Millennium Falcon and Luke Skywalker are the headline cards. Strong resale performance since launch.

**Shadows of the Galaxy (SOG):** Set 2. Bounty hunter focus. Boba Fett, Jabba the Hutt. Strong for competitive play.

**Twilight of the Republic (TOR):** Set 3. Prequel era. Clone Wars characters. High art quality.
