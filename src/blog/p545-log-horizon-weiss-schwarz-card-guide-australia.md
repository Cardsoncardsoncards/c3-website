# Log Horizon in Weiss Schwarz: Shiroe's Blue and Red Guild Cast

Log Horizon brings Shiroe and the Log Horizon guild into Weiss Schwarz, a documented blue and red trial deck built around confirmed 《Weapon》 and 《Magic》 character traits that echo the source material's own trapped-in-an-MMO premise. If you already play and want the character and combo breakdown, jump ahead. If you are newer to Weiss Schwarz, the primer below covers the core rules, with the full mechanics glossary linked at the end.

## The set at a glance

Log Horizon spans 2 tracked releases, the original trial deck (23 October 2015) and a Power Up Set (March 2023), totalling 156 cards.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## Weapon and Magic Traits, Reflecting the Game-Within-a-Game Premise

Log Horizon's trial deck is confirmed as blue and red, built around Shiroe's card line in one colour and Akatsuki's in the other, with Naotsugu, Nyanta, Serara, Marielle, and Henrietta rounding out the confirmed cast. Confirmed card text shows the climax "Sophisticated Cooperation" granting a significant power boost to any character with "Shiroe" in its card name, a directly named-character combo that rewards running multiple Shiroe cards together. The card pool also builds around 《Weapon》 and 《Magic》 character traits, a fitting mechanical choice given the source material's premise, with cards that scale their power based on how many Weapon or Magic characters you have on board. A deck built around Shiroe's enchanter-focused Magic line plays differently to one leaning into Naotsugu or other Weapon-trait characters, even within the same blue and red identity.
