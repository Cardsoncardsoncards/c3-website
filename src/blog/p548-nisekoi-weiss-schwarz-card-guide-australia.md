# Nisekoi in Weiss Schwarz: A Genuine Multi-Colour Love Triangle

Nisekoi brings Raku Ichijou's love triangle with Chitoge Kirisaki and Kosaki Onodera into Weiss Schwarz, a confirmed yellow and blue trial deck that community deck techs document as expanding into a genuinely powerful multi-colour build once booster material is added. If you already play and want the archetype detail, jump ahead. If you are newer to Weiss Schwarz, the primer below covers the core rules, with the full mechanics glossary linked at the end.

## The set at a glance

Nisekoi spans 2 tracked releases, NISEKOI -False Love- (23 January 2015) and an Extra Booster (August 2016), totalling 229 cards.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## A Documented Multi-Colour Archetype

Nisekoi's confirmed trial deck is yellow and blue, built around Chitoge and Kosaki's card lines, with Raku Ichijou, Seishirou Tsugumi, Marika Tachibana, and Ruri Miyamoto rounding out the confirmed named cast. What sets this property apart is that its most well known competitive archetype genuinely expands beyond the trial deck identity. Community deck tech articles document a red and blue build as the property's most well known competitive archetype, built around a recurring card called Pendant of Promise and specific level 3 finishers, with green also documented as a viable splash for players wanting access to Kosaki Onodera's climax combo specifically. With confirmed named characters spanning Chitoge, Kosaki, Raku, Seishirou, Marika, and Ruri, the four copy limit is exactly why community-documented competitive decks commit to a specific colour and character focus, most commonly built around Chitoge or Kosaki as the central combo piece, rather than spreading evenly across the whole confirmed cast. This layering of trait synergy underneath a broader colour and character choice is a pattern that shows up across several Weiss Schwarz properties, but Nisekoi's confirmed shift away from its own trial deck colours into a community-favoured build is a genuinely distinctive case.
