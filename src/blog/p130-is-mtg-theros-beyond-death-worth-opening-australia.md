## What Is Theros Beyond Death and Why Does It Matter for Australian Players?

Theros Beyond Death is set primarily in the Underworld realm of Theros, ruled by the God Erebos. The set introduces Escape (cast from graveyard by exiling cards) and Constellation (triggers when enchantments enter under your control). The Draft Booster box contains 36 packs. The Constellation Showcase treatment presents cards in a star map aesthetic with gold constellation lines connecting illustrated figures. The full God cycle (Heliod, Thassa, Erebos, Purphoros, Nylea, plus demigods) provides Commander targets across all colours and two-colour combinations.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Theros Beyond Death worth opening in Australia?**
At AU$145 for 36 packs, Theros Beyond Death offers moderate EV with predictable secondary market values as a settled 2020 set. Uro and Kroxa anchor the mythic value. The lower price point versus current sets makes the per-pack ratio reasonable. Use the C3 EV Calculator for a current assessment.

**Why was Uro banned in so many formats?**
Uro generates massive value every turn it attacks or enters the battlefield — gaining 3 life, drawing a card, and putting a land into play. With Escape allowing it to return from the graveyard repeatedly for a relatively low cost, Uro created games where the controller generated insurmountable card and resource advantage. It was banned in Standard, Modern, Legacy, and Pioneer for warping gameplay around its presence.

**What is the Heliod and Walking Ballista combo?**
Heliod Sun-Crowned gives all creatures that gain life a +1/+1 counter. Walking Ballista can be activated to deal one damage by removing a +1/+1 counter. If Walking Ballista has lifelink or gains life some other way, gaining one life triggers Heliod to add a +1/+1 counter back, enabling infinite damage. This two-card infinite combo drove Pioneer tournament play and led to format discussions about banning one or both pieces.

**What are Constellation Showcase cards in Theros Beyond Death?**
The Constellation Showcase treatment presents cards in a star map aesthetic — characters are depicted as constellations against a dark sky, with gold lines connecting illuminated star points to form the illustrated figure. Gods and legendary creatures received this treatment. They command collector premiums in the AU secondary market due to their distinctive visual style.
