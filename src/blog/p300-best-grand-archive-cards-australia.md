## Quick Answer

Grand Archive's most valuable cards are Cold Foil and Rainbow Foil variants of Champions and powerful Regalia from the game's competitive sets. The game's premium card philosophy means even standard foil versions of key cards carry meaningful prices. Cold Foils in particular are among the highest-quality physical cards in any TCG and collector demand reflects that.

## Grand Archive's Foil Tiers

Grand Archive uses several foil treatments that significantly affect card value. Understanding these is essential before buying or selling.

**Cold Foil (CF)** is the game's premium tier. Cold Foils use a distinct non-reflective foil treatment that gives the card a matte, etched appearance. They are distributed at much lower rates than standard foils and are the highest-value version of any given card. A competitively relevant Champion in Cold Foil can reach AU$100 to AU$400+ for the most desirable cards.

**Rainbow Foil (RR)** is the standard full-art foil treatment, more common than Cold Foil but still significantly scarcer than non-foil versions. Rainbow Foil versions of competitive staples trade at AU$20 to AU$80.

**Extended Art variants** exist for some cards and feature artwork that extends to the card border. These command premiums over standard art versions.

**Non-foil versions** of the same cards are substantially cheaper, typically 20 to 40 percent of the foil price for the same card.

## Most Valuable Card Categories

**Cold Foil Champions** from Dawn of Ashes and subsequent sets are the flagship collector targets. Champions like Lorraine, Zander, and other class-defining characters in CF command AU$80 to AU$350 depending on competitive relevance and scarcity.

**Cold Foil Regalia** of the most-played equipment pieces in the current competitive meta sit at AU$40 to AU$150. Equipment that appears in multiple Champion builds is more valuable than equipment restricted to a single class.

**Rainbow Foil competitive staples** are the most actively traded singles in Australian Grand Archive communities. Prices run AU$15 to AU$60 for the most-played cards.

**Kickstarter exclusives** from the original campaign have the highest collector premiums. These were distributed in limited quantities to backers and are not available through retail channels. Specific Kickstarter-exclusive art variants can reach AU$200 to AU$500 for the most desirable.

## The Investment Angle

Grand Archive has made deliberate decisions about print run management that have supported card values better than most indie TCGs. First edition product is explicitly tracked, print runs are smaller than mass-market games, and the publisher has been transparent about production volumes.

Cold Foil versions of cards from early sets have appreciated from release prices in many cases. This is not guaranteed to continue but the publisher's approach creates better conditions for value retention than most comparable games.

## Where to Buy Grand Archive Cards in Australia

The Australian secondary market for Grand Archive is thin. Most trading happens through the game's Discord community and international platforms. US TCG marketplaces carry better stock of specific singles than eBay AU in most cases, with international postage adding AU$8 to AU$15.

## The C3 Take

Grand Archive has the most interesting secondary market of any indie TCG currently available in Australia. The Cold Foil tier is genuinely beautiful and the print run management creates real scarcity. If you are drawn to the game and want to invest in a few premium pieces, Cold Foil Champions from the first two sets are the safest targets based on historical price performance.

Do not expect liquidity. The market is small and selling at your target price takes time. Buy what you love and the value question becomes secondary.
