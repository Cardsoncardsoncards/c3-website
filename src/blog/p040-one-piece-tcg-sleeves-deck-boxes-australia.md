## One Piece Deck Size: What This Means for Accessories

One Piece decks are 50 cards plus one Leader card — 51 cards total. This is smaller than Pokemon (60 cards), Magic Standard (60 cards), or Lorcana (60 cards). The smaller deck size has practical implications for your accessories:

**Sleeves:** Buy 60 sleeves for a One Piece deck — the 51-card deck plus spares for replacements.

**Deck boxes:** A standard 80 or 100-card capacity deck box has plenty of room for a single-sleeved One Piece deck, plus space for your Don!! cards (10 per game), tokens, and a life tracker if you use one. Don't buy a deck box so tight it's hard to access cards.

**Double-sleeved decks:** If you double-sleeve (inner perfect fit plus outer sleeve), count your cards as slightly thicker. Most 80+ capacity deck boxes handle a 51-card double-sleeved One Piece deck with comfort.

Dragon Shield Matte are the benchmark sleeve for competitive One Piece play in Australia, as they are across the TCG landscape. Available at local game stores and Amazon AU.

**Why they work for One Piece:** The Don!! attachment mechanic means cards are picked up, placed, and moved around the table more than in some other TCGs. Dragon Shield Matte sleeves handle repeated manipulation well and their durability under regular play is well established.

### KMC Hyper Mat — Strong Alternative

KMC Hyper Mat sleeves offer reliable quality at a slightly lower price point than Dragon Shield. Available in Australia and a legitimate choice for players who want premium quality without Dragon Shield pricing.

### Dragon Shield Perfect Fit — For High-Value Cards

Secret Rare and Special Card variants from One Piece can be worth AU$50–200+. For these cards, double-sleeving with a Dragon Shield Perfect Fit inner sleeve provides meaningful additional protection against edge wear and surface damage.

The small cost of an inner sleeve on a AU$100 card is trivially justified.

### Official One Piece Card Sleeves

Bandai releases official One Piece Card Game sleeves featuring art from the game. These are collector-appealing and sold through game stores and online retailers. Quality varies by product — some official sleeves are high quality; others are more decorative than functional.

For serious competitive play, third-party branded sleeves (Dragon Shield, KMC) tend to be more reliable. Official sleeves work well for casual play and collection display.

## Storage for One Piece Collections

For the broader collection beyond your active decks, the same storage principles apply as for any TCG:

**Binders with side-loading 9-pocket pages** for organised collection display and set completion tracking. One Piece sets are typically smaller than Pokemon or Magic sets, so a single binder can hold a complete set including rarity variants.

**Cardboard storage boxes** for bulk cards from booster openings. BCW and Ultra Pro boxes in 500–1000 card counts are cost-effective for bulk storage.

**Toploaders** for individually valuable cards (Secret Rares, Special Cards) not in active deck use.

**Silica gel packets** in storage boxes if you're in a high-humidity environment — particularly relevant in Queensland, the NT, and coastal areas.

## Australian Climate Note

The standard Australian climate storage advice applies to One Piece cards as it does to all TCGs. Keep cards away from direct sunlight, avoid storage in hot garages or sheds, and manage humidity with silica gel if necessary. One Piece cards don't have specific additional climate vulnerabilities beyond standard card stock.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What size sleeves fit One Piece Card Game cards?**
Standard size sleeves — 88mm x 63mm. The same size as Magic: The Gathering, Pokemon, Lorcana, and most major TCGs.

**How many sleeves do I need for a One Piece deck?**
Buy 60 sleeves for a 51-card One Piece deck — this covers your deck plus spare sleeves for replacements when one tears during play.

**What deck box fits a One Piece deck?**
Any deck box rated for 60 or more cards. The Dragon Shield Nest 100 is particularly useful because its side compartments hold Don!! cards and accessories. Ultra Pro Satin Tower 80 fits a single-sleeved One Piece deck with room for accessories.

**Do I need to sleeve Don!! cards as well?**
In competitive play, yes — all cards in your deck area should be in matching sleeves. Don!! cards are not in your main deck but are used during play, so they should also be sleeved. Use a separate set of sleeves (or the same colour) for consistency.

**Are official One Piece Card Game sleeves worth buying?**
For casual play and aesthetic appeal, yes. For serious competitive play, third-party premium sleeves like Dragon Shield Matte offer better durability and consistency. Many competitive players use official sleeves for a deck they love aesthetically while keeping a separate functionally-prioritised sleeve for tournaments.

**Where can I buy One Piece card accessories in Australia?**
Local game stores that sell One Piece product typically stock standard TCG accessories. Amazon AU has a wide range of sleeves and deck boxes available with Prime shipping.
