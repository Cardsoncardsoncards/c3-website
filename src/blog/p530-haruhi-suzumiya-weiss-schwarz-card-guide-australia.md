# Haruhi Suzumiya in Weiss Schwarz: The SOS Brigade in Red and Blue

The Melancholy of Haruhi Suzumiya has one of the earliest confirmed trial deck colour identities documented in Weiss Schwarz, a red and blue pairing built around Haruhi herself and Yuki Nagato, with the SOS Brigade as the connecting thread. If you already play and want the colour and character breakdown, jump ahead. If you are newer to Weiss Schwarz, the primer below covers the core rules, with the full mechanics glossary linked at the end.

## The set at a glance

Haruhi Suzumiya spans 3 tracked releases, the original set (22 January 2016), an Extra Booster (May 2016), and a Power Up Set (March 2023), totalling 242 cards.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## The SOS Brigade as the Connecting Thread

Haruhi Suzumiya's confirmed trial deck identity is red and blue, with Haruhi's own card line, carrying the confirmed Brigade Leader and SOS Brigade character traits, sitting in red, and Yuki Nagato's card line in blue. The Brigade framing gives this property a genuine thematic and mechanical throughline, with the wider cast, including Mikuru Asahina, appearing across both colours in supporting roles. The red and blue pairing is not just a starting point to eventually branch away from, it is the property's own confirmed core identity, similar in spirit to how Kancolle settles into its own red and green trial deck pairing rather than spreading into a wider colour spread.
