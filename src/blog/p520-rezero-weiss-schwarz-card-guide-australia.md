# Re:Zero in Weiss Schwarz: Multi-Colour Builds and the Emilia-Centric Approach

Re:Zero brings a distinctive design choice into Weiss Schwarz, its card pool supports both a broad multi-colour build spanning red, yellow, and blue, and a tighter single-character-focused approach centred on Emilia. That gives Re:Zero collectors and players two genuinely different ways to build with the same catalogue. If you already play and want the build detail, jump ahead. If you are newer to the game, the primer below covers the core rules, with the full mechanics glossary linked at the end.

## The set at a glance

Re:Zero has one of the smaller footprints among Weiss Schwarz's licensed properties, with 4 released sets from 21 December 2018 through The Frozen Bond in February 2021, totalling around 550 cards. A fifth release, Re:ZERO -Starting Life in Another World- Vol.3, is announced for 28 August 2026 and has not been released yet, so it is not covered by this guide until it is out.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## Two Build Styles: Multi-Colour and Emilia-Centric

Re:Zero is unusual in supporting a genuine three colour spread across red, yellow, and blue as one documented build path, while also supporting a more contained single-character build focused on Emilia's card line as the other. The multi-colour build draws on the broader cast across all three colours, giving access to a wider pool of abilities and climax combos at the cost of a more carefully balanced colour base. The Emilia-centric build concentrates the deck around her specific card line, trading access to the wider cast for more consistent colour requirements.

Neither build is inherently stronger, they represent different trade-offs between flexibility and consistency, and which one suits a given collector often comes down to whether Emilia specifically is the character they are most invested in.

## Why Re:Zero's collecting angle matters

With only 4 released sets, Re:Zero is one of the more approachable full catalogues to collect compared to sprawling 10 or 12 set franchises, making a complete collection a realistic goal for many collectors. If you are deciding where to start, picking based on which build style you want to play, multi-colour or Emilia-centric, is a more useful filter than picking by release order, since the two approaches pull from different parts of the card pool.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Should I build multi-colour red/yellow/blue or go Emilia-centric as a new Re:Zero player?**
There is no single correct answer, multi-colour gives access to a wider cast at the cost of a more balanced colour base, while Emilia-centric plays more consistently with a narrower cast.

**Is Re:Zero a small enough catalogue to realistically collect in full?**
With 4 released sets, Re:Zero is one of the more approachable full catalogues in Weiss Schwarz, making a complete collection realistic for many collectors.

**Is a new Re:Zero set on the way?**
Yes, Re:ZERO -Starting Life in Another World- Vol.3 is announced for 28 August 2026. It had not released at the time of writing, so it is not covered by this guide yet.

**Do older Re:Zero sets remain legal in tournament play?**
Weiss Schwarz does not use a rotating standard format, so older Re:Zero cards generally remain playable, though always check a specific event's rules before entering.

**Can I mix the multi-colour and Emilia-centric approaches in one deck?**
You can, but the two build styles are documented as distinct approaches with different colour and consistency trade-offs, so most players commit to one rather than blending both.

## What to check before buying

1. Confirm which of the 4 released Re:Zero sets a listing is from before buying, since character names repeat across sets and it is easy to mix up an era.
2. Decide whether you are building multi-colour or Emilia-centric before buying singles, since the two styles pull from different parts of the card pool and buying without a plan risks cards that do not support either build well.
3. Once Vol.3 releases later in 2026, confirm whether a listing is from the new set or an earlier release before assuming pricing or rarity carries over.
