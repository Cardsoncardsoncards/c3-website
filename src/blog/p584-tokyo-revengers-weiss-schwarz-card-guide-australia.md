# Tokyo Revengers Weiss Schwarz Card Guide (Australia)

Tokyo Revengers follows Takemichi Hanagaki, who discovers he can travel back in time and uses that ability to try to save his ex-girlfriend Hinata Tachibana from a cruel fate by changing the past. Getting close with members of the Tokyo Manji Gang along the way, Takemichi must use what he learns to protect the people who matter most to him. The series became one of the defining delinquent gang dramas of its era, and its Weiss Schwarz set captures that gritty, time bending story directly on the card table.

## The set at a glance

Tokyo Revengers is a single stand-alone set in English, released 29 July 2022, with a total of 202 cards. It has not received a second English wave, so this remains the complete Tokyo Revengers collection available on the platform. The set code is TRV, and the release featured 100 base card types plus 52 parallel versions, packed 9 cards per pack, 16 packs per display, 18 displays per carton.

## Deck building around Takemichi and the Tokyo Manji Gang

The card pool centres on Takemichi Hanagaki, whose time travelling ability provides a genuinely distinctive mechanical hook, supported by key Tokyo Manji Gang members including Manjiro Sano, Keisuke Baji, Takashi Mitsuya, and Chifuyu Matsuno, alongside Hinata Tachibana and Emma from the story's present day cast. Because the story moves between past and present so deliberately, the card pool reflects that structure, giving collectors a choice between building around the gang's earlier delinquent era or the present day consequences of Takemichi's time travelling interventions. Each gang member carries genuine narrative weight in the story, which the card pool reflects through distinct mechanical roles rather than treating them as interchangeable supporting cast.

## Chase cards and signature pulls

This set features a genuinely distinctive chase card structure beyond the usual voice cast signatures. Alongside SSP hot stamped autographed cards from the voice cast, including Yuuki Shin as Takemichi Hanagaki, Azumi Waki as Hinata Tachibana, and Yuu Hayashi as Manjiro Sano, the set includes TRV rarity cards, hot stamped cards carrying iconic quotes directly from the anime. Building a chase structure around memorable dialogue rather than purely character portraits is a genuinely distinctive approach, and it gives fans of the show's most quotable moments a specific type of card to hunt for.

Tournament Store Assist PR Packs were also made available, each containing one of six special shiny PR cards, redeemable with the purchase of a display at tournament registered stores, giving competitive players an additional avenue to chase exclusive cards.

## Why this property continues to draw collectors

Tokyo Revengers built a large, dedicated fanbase through its blend of delinquent gang drama and time travel stakes, a combination that stood out clearly from the broader wave of school and gang focused anime released around the same period. That distinctiveness, combined with a genuinely large cast of gang members each with their own devoted following, has kept demand for this card set steady since release.

For Australian collectors, the TRV quote cards specifically are worth understanding as a point of difference from most other Weiss Schwarz releases. Collectors who value dialogue and narrative moments as much as character artwork will find this one of the more thoughtfully designed chase structures in the current one-set tier, and it rewards genuine familiarity with the source material over simply chasing whatever looks visually striking.

## Why quote based chase cards are a notable design choice

Most Weiss Schwarz chase structures revolve entirely around character artwork and voice cast autographs, treating the visual portrayal of a character as the primary collectible element. The TRV quote cards in this set take a different approach, elevating specific lines of dialogue to chase card status in their own right. For a franchise built heavily around Takemichi's internal monologue and the gang's memorable exchanges, this is a genuinely fitting design decision, since much of Tokyo Revengers' emotional impact comes from what characters say in pivotal moments rather than purely how they look.

For collectors, this means building a complete Tokyo Revengers collection involves engaging with the show's narrative more directly than simply chasing character portraits. If you are a fan of the series' writing and specific memorable lines, tracking down the TRV quote cards tied to your favourite moments adds a layer of collecting engagement that goes beyond the usual pursuit of the prettiest or rarest character art.

## What to check before buying

1. Confirm whether a listing is a standard SSP signed card or specifically a TRV quote card, since these are genuinely distinct chase items within the release despite both carrying hot stamped elements.
2. Decide which era of the story, the Tokyo Manji Gang's earlier delinquent days or the present day cast, you want to build or collect around before buying broadly.
3. Check condition carefully on circulating stock, since the set is now a few years old and some copies show genuine handling wear.
