## Getting Started with One Piece TCG in Australia

One Piece TCG launched in late 2022 and has grown into one of the strongest-selling TCGs in Australia behind Pokemon and MTG. The art is excellent, the gameplay is faster-paced than MTG, and the sealed product has shown strong resale performance since the Paramount War era.

## Where to Start

**If you want to play:** Start with a Starter Deck. They are pre-built, playable out of the box, and give you the core mechanics before you invest in booster boxes. The ST (Starter) format is well-supported and most starter decks are available on Amazon AU at AU$20 to $30.

**If you want to collect:** Booster boxes from the current OP set are the right entry point. Each box contains 24 packs. Secret Rares and Special Parallel cards are the chase cards that drive secondary market value.

## Current Sets Available on Amazon AU

The OP (One Piece) set numbering is sequential. OP-01 through OP-04 are the earlier sets. OP-05 onward represents the current meta era. Anything from OP-07 forward is the strongest for current play.

## What Makes One Piece TCG Different

The game uses a Leader card system where your leader has a fixed colour identity that determines which cards you can run. This makes deck construction approachable. There is no mana curve concept — you attach resources (called DON!!) to characters to boost their power.

Games typically finish in 30 to 45 minutes, which is faster than MTG.

## Buying Tips for Australia

- Import pricing is real. Some sets are Japan-only at launch and hit AU stores 2 to 3 months later.
- English sets from Bandai are the standard for AU play. Japanese sets are desirable for collectors but have no competitive play support here.
- Confirm the set is an official English Bandai release before buying from marketplace sellers on Amazon AU.
