# Miss Kobayashi's Dragon Maid Weiss Schwarz Card Guide (Australia)

Miss Kobayashi's Dragon Maid follows Kobayashi, an ordinary office worker whose life is upended when she takes in Tohru, a dragon who takes human form to become her maid, along with the energetic young dragon Kanna. Originally a full colour manga published on the comico platform, the series has built a strong, dedicated fanbase across its manga, anime, and now Weiss Schwarz card release.

## The set at a glance

Miss Kobayashi's Dragon Maid is a single stand-alone set in English, released 18 November 2022, with a total of 220 cards. It has not received a second English wave, so this remains the complete collection currently available for the property. The set code is KMD, and the release featured 100 base card types plus 61 parallel versions, packed 9 cards per pack, 16 packs per display, 18 displays per carton.

The card pool draws not only from the manga and anime but also incorporates illustrations from the video game Miss Kobayashi's Dragon Maid Explosion, giving the set a broader visual source than a straightforward anime adaptation would typically offer. That video game tie in also signals the franchise's reach beyond just print and animation, into interactive media as well, which reflects the kind of sustained multi format popularity that tends to support long term collector interest.

## Deck building around Kobayashi, Tohru, and Kanna

The card pool centres on the core household of Kobayashi, Tohru, and Kanna, with Tohru's dragon powers and fierce devotion to Kobayashi typically anchoring the strongest builds, supported by Kanna's more playful, mischievous card effects. Because the source material is fundamentally a slice of life comedy built around this small central household, deck building here stays focused and approachable rather than requiring navigation of a large, sprawling cast.

## Chase cards and signature pulls

This is one of the rarer sets on the platform in terms of signature structure. Rather than featuring signed cards from the voice cast, Miss Kobayashi's Dragon Maid includes hot stamped autographed cards signed by Coolkyousinnjya, the creator of the original manga. A creator signature rather than a voice actor signature is a genuinely unusual chase item, similar in spirit to the developer signed card found in the Guilty Gear Strive release, and it gives this set a distinct kind of collector prestige beyond the usual cast autograph format.

It is also worth noting that the artwork for the SP cards in the English release was modified from the original Japanese edition, so English and Japanese versions of the same numbered card can differ visually, which is a detail worth checking if you are comparing listings across both markets.

## Why Australian collectors are interested in this set

Miss Kobayashi's Dragon Maid built a loyal audience through its distinctive blend of everyday slice of life comedy with its fantastical dragon premise, and that combination has kept the franchise relevant well past its initial manga and anime run. The creator signed chase card in particular gives this set a genuine point of differentiation from the many voice cast signed releases that dominate the wider Weiss Schwarz catalogue.

For Australian buyers, that creator signature is the standout detail to understand about this release. Collectors specifically drawn to author or creator signed memorabilia, rather than voice actor autographs, will find this one of the more interesting entries in the current one-set tier, and it is worth budgeting patience into your search given how few sets on the platform feature this kind of signature at all.

## Creator signed cards versus voice cast signed cards

Across the wider Weiss Schwarz catalogue, the overwhelming majority of signature chase cards feature autographs from the anime's voice cast rather than from the people who created the original source material. Miss Kobayashi's Dragon Maid is one of a small handful of releases, alongside Guilty Gear Strive's developer signed card, that instead features a signature from the actual creator of the work.

This distinction matters for how you think about collecting this set. A voice actor signs cards as a performer associated with a character, but a creator signature connects the card directly back to the person who conceived the story and characters in the first place. For collectors who value that direct connection to the source material's origin, the Coolkyousinnjya signed card is arguably a more meaningful item than an equivalent voice cast signature would be, even though both types of signature carry a premium in the current market.

## What to check before buying

1. Confirm whether a listing is the standard SP hot stamped version or specifically the Coolkyousinnjya creator signed card, since these are genuinely distinct chase items within the release.
2. If comparing English and Japanese listings for the same card number, check the artwork carefully, since SP card illustrations were modified between the two editions.
3. Check condition carefully on circulating stock, since the set is now a few years past release and some copies show genuine handling wear.
