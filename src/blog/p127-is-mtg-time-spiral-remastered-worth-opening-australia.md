## What Is Time Spiral Remastered and Why Does It Matter for Australian Players?

Time Spiral Remastered contains reprints from Time Spiral, Planar Chaos, and Future Sight — three sets released 2006-2007 that explored MTG's past, present, and future simultaneously through different card frame and mechanic treatments. The main set uses standard modern card frames. The Timeshifted bonus slot appears in every pack as one card using the classic old-border pre-8th Edition frame. Key reprints include Tarmogoyf, Snapcaster Mage, Damnation, Lotus Bloom, and the full Pact cycle. The Draft Booster box contains 36 packs.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What are Timeshifted cards in Time Spiral Remastered?**
Timeshifted cards are reprints printed in the classic pre-8th Edition card frame — the old border style used by MTG from 1993 to 2003. Every pack in Time Spiral Remastered contains one Timeshifted card. The 121-card Timeshifted sheet includes everything from commons worth AU$0.50 to Snapcaster Mage worth AU$30-50+. Getting 36 guaranteed retro frame cards per box creates significant EV variance.

**Is Time Spiral Remastered worth opening in Australia?**
At AU$170 for 36 packs, TSR offers reasonable EV driven by the guaranteed Timeshifted slot. The range is wide — most Timeshifted cards are low value but hitting Snapcaster Mage, Tarmogoyf, or Damnation significantly boosts box value. Use the C3 EV Calculator for a current assessment.

**Why are foil retro frame cards so valuable?**
Foil retro frame cards combine two sought-after treatments: the nostalgic old-border card frame and the foil finish. Cards printed in the original MTG era were foiled rarely and the combination creates a visually distinctive product that collectors actively seek. Foil retro Snapcaster Mage and Tarmogoyf from Time Spiral Remastered are among the most valuable standard-print MTG cards of the 2021 release period.

**What is the original Time Spiral block about?**
Time Spiral (2006) was set during a period of catastrophe in the MTG multiverse — a hole in the fabric of time caused by overuse of mana over centuries. The plane of Dominaria was ravaged and time rifts were tearing reality apart. The block explored MTG's past through nostalgic card reprints and mechanics, the present through current designs, and the future through Future Sight's futureshifted cards with then-unprecedented mechanics.
