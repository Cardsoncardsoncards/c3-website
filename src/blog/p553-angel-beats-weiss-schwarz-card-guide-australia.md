# Angel Beats in Weiss Schwarz: Kanade, Yuri and the Battlefront

Angel Beats brings Kanade Tachibana and Yuri Nakamura into Weiss Schwarz through the confirmed Re:Edit release, built around the Battlefront cast and a genuinely well documented Kanade and Yuri character-focused build. If you already play and want the archetype detail, jump ahead. If you are newer to Weiss Schwarz, the primer below covers the core rules, with the full mechanics glossary linked at the end.

## The set at a glance

Angel Beats is a single stand-alone set, the Re:Edit release, released 25 July 2014, with a total of 194 cards. Note that an older release combined Angel Beats with a separate visual novel, Kud Wafter, but that combined release predates and sits outside the current confirmed hub scope entirely.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## A Documented Kanade and Yuri Core Build

Angel Beats's confirmed Re:Edit release centres on Kanade and Yuri, alongside Yui, Iwasawa, Hisako, Otonashi, and Shiina. Community deck techs confirm a well established build structure, level 1 Kanades with climax combos to search the deck and begin compression, level 2 characters serving as a transition into level 3, and Yuri's climax combos functioning as the deck's finishing sequence. A separate documented approach, informally called a "Music" deck by the community, builds around Iwasawa and the wider GirlDeMo band cast instead, giving a genuine second direction beyond the Kanade and Yuri core, a useful reminder that "one set" does not necessarily mean "one deck" in this catalogue.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the documented core build for this property?**
Community deck techs confirm a Kanade and Yuri build, using level 1 Kanade climax combos to compress the deck before transitioning to Yuri's level 3 finishing combos.

**Is there a build that does not centre on Kanade and Yuri?**
Yes, a documented "Music" deck build centres on Iwasawa and the GirlDeMo band cast instead, giving a genuine second direction within the same confirmed set.

**Was there an earlier Angel Beats release before Re:Edit?**
Yes, an older release combined Angel Beats with a separate visual novel, Kud Wafter, though that combined release predates and sits outside the current confirmed Re:Edit hub scope entirely.

**Do older Angel Beats cards remain legal in tournament play?**
Weiss Schwarz does not use a rotating standard format, so cards generally remain playable, though always check a specific event's own rules before entering any tournament with them.

**Should I build around Kanade, Yuri, or both together?**
Community-documented builds run them together as a core pairing, Kanade's climax combos support early compression while Yuri's combos provide the finishing sequence at level 3 to close the game out.

**How does the four copy limit affect choosing between the Kanade/Yuri build and the Music build?**
It reinforces committing to one documented direction, since running four copies of key cards from both the Kanade and Yuri core and the Iwasawa-focused Music build at once would spread your deck too thin to execute either reliably.

## What to check before buying

1. Confirm a listing is genuinely from the Re:Edit release rather than the older, out of scope Kud Wafter combined release.
