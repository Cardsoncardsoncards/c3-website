Dragon Ball Super Card Game (DBSCG) starter decks are pre-built, ready-to-play 50-card decks that include a Leader card. For new players in Australia, a starter deck is the correct first purchase — they're balanced, legal for tournament play, and represent good value.

This guide covers which DBSCG starter decks are currently available and worth buying in Australia.

## What DBSCG Starter Decks Include

Each DBSCG Starter Deck contains:

- 1 Leader Card (the deck's main character)
- 50 Battle Cards (the main deck)
- 1 Starting Leader Card (the reverse side of the Leader, used during Awakening)
- Quick start guide
- Some versions include Token cards

Starter Decks in DBSCG are significantly more competitive than starters in some other TCGs. They're designed to be usable at local events without major upgrades.

## How to Choose a Starter Deck

The primary factor is character preference. DBSCG Starter Decks are themed around iconic Dragon Ball characters, and the deck's playstyle reflects that character:

**Goku-themed decks**: typically aggressive. High-power attacks, consistent offense, straightforward game plans. Good choice for players who want to attack and apply pressure from turn one.

**Vegeta-themed decks**: often midrange. Balance between offense and support. Strong individual card power.

**Frieza-themed decks**: tend to be more control-oriented. Disruption, card advantage, and late-game payoff.

**Gohan-themed decks**: often combo-adjacent. Build up board state and execute specific game-ending sequences.

**Villain ensemble decks** (Cell, Broly, Beerus): varied styles depending on the character. Generally higher individual card power at the cost of consistency.

## What's Currently Available in Australia

DBSCG Starter Decks are available at local game stores and online in Australia for approximately AU$25 to AU$40.

Availability varies by location. Check your local game store or search eBay AU for current Australian stock.

## The Most Recommended Starting Approach

For most new Australian players:

**Buy two different starter decks and learn by playing them against each other.**

Two players, two decks, immediate play. This costs AU$50 to AU$80 total and gives both players a functional learning experience from the first game.

After playing 5 to 10 games and understanding which aspects of the game you enjoy most, you'll have enough information to decide which direction to upgrade.

## Upgrading Beyond Starter Decks

Starter Decks in DBSCG are a solid competitive foundation. The primary upgrades are:

**3 copies of the same Starter Deck**: DBSCG decks can include up to 4 copies of each card. Buying 3 starter decks of the same character gives you additional copies of key cards.

**Targeted singles**: specific high-impact cards from recent booster sets that synergise with your Leader.
