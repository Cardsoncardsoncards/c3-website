# Mob Psycho 100 Weiss Schwarz Card Guide (Australia)

Mob Psycho 100 follows Shigeo Mob Kageyama, an outwardly average middle schooler who happens to be one of the most powerful espers in existence, and his journey to suppress his overwhelming psychic power while trying to live a normal life. Originally created as a webcomic by ONE, the same creator behind One Punch Man, and animated by BONES, the series has built a strong critical reputation, and its Weiss Schwarz set has become a steady favourite among collectors who enjoy character driven, emotionally grounded storytelling.

## The set at a glance

Mob Psycho 100 is a single stand-alone set in English, released 18 December 2020, with a total of 169 cards. It has not received a second English wave, so this remains the complete Mob Psycho 100 collection available on the platform. The set code is MOB, and the release featured 100 base card types plus 30 parallel versions, packed 8 cards per pack, 20 packs per display, 16 displays per carton.

The set launched alongside a Trial Deck+ and a Supply Set bundling a deck box, sleeves, and boosters together, giving new players an accessible way into the format without needing to buy loose boosters straight away.

## Deck building around Mob and Reigen

The card pool centres on Mob himself, whose suppressed psychic power provides one of the more thematically interesting mechanical hooks on the platform, alongside Arataka Reigen, his self proclaimed mentor and the show's primary source of comic relief, and Dimple, the mischievous spirit who accompanies them. Because the core trio carries such distinct personalities and roles in the story, from Mob's quiet restraint to Reigen's overconfident bluster, the card pool reflects genuinely different playstyles across the main characters rather than reskinning the same mechanics with different art. Supporting cast members from Mob's school life and the wider Spirits and Such Consultation Office also feature, rounding out the deck building options beyond just the three central figures.

## Chase cards and signature pulls

Every pack carries a chance of a hot stamped autograph card from the voice cast, including Setsuo Ito as Shigeo Mob Kageyama, Takahiro Sakurai as Arataka Reigen, and Akio Otsuka as Dimple. The Trial Deck+ specifically offered a chance at either one of three RRR cards with special embossing or a single SP card signed by the voice cast per deck, giving even the beginner focused starter product a genuine chase card structure. Display boxes included one of five exclusive PR cards, and cartons carried four rubber playmats.

## Why this property has held up well with collectors

Mob Psycho 100 carries a strong critical reputation, both for its distinctive animation style and for its unusually thoughtful handling of themes like self worth, restraint, and what it means to be ordinary despite extraordinary ability. That critical standing has helped the series maintain relevance well past its original broadcast run, in a similar pattern to other critically acclaimed properties on this platform.

For Australian collectors, Mob Psycho 100 sits alongside other quality driven, less hype dependent sets as a steadier long term hold. Demand has not spiked dramatically at any point since release, but it has also proven durable, reflecting a dedicated fanbase that continues to value the series years after its anime concluded. That steadiness makes it a reasonable pick for a patient collector building a considered, quality focused collection rather than chasing short term trends.

## The ONE connection

Mob Psycho 100 shares a creator with One Punch Man, one of the most successful action comedy franchises of the past decade, and that shared authorship is worth understanding as context for this set's collector appeal. While the two series differ significantly in tone and genre, both carry ONE's distinctive approach to subverting typical shonen power fantasy tropes, with Mob's suppressed power mirroring Saitama's own understated relationship to overwhelming strength in a different register.

For collectors who came to Mob Psycho 100 through an existing interest in One Punch Man, or vice versa, this shared creative lineage adds a layer of interest beyond the show standing purely on its own. It is worth knowing this connection exists if you are building a collection around ONE's body of work specifically rather than treating each franchise as entirely separate.

Because both franchises draw from the same creative sensibility but occupy separate Weiss Schwarz releases with their own card pools, collectors sometimes treat the two as a natural pairing to build alongside each other, even though nothing in the card mechanics themselves ties the two sets together directly. It simply reflects a genuine overlap in fan interest rather than any official crossover content between the two properties.

## What to check before buying

1. Confirm whether a listing is base rarity or the signed voice cast version before paying a premium, since photos should clearly show the hot stamp rather than just describing it in the title.
2. Check condition carefully on circulating stock, since the set is now several years old and some copies show genuine handling wear from being opened and played with over time.
3. If chasing Trial Deck+ specific RRR embossed cards, confirm the listing is from that specific product rather than the main Booster Pack, since the two products have separate rarity structures for their signature and embossed chase cards.
