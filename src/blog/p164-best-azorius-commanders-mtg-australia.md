Azorius (white-blue) is the premier control combination in Commander. White provides removal and wraths; blue provides counterspells and card draw. Together they can answer almost any threat while building toward a win condition at their own pace.

## Top Azorius Commanders

### Brago, King Eternal

Brago blinks (exiles and returns) all your non-land permanents whenever he deals combat damage to a player. This resets ETB (enters the battlefield) triggers, re-activates tap abilities, and untaps any tapped permanents you control. With evasion and a blink-heavy build, Brago generates enormous value every turn.

**Current AU price**: approximately AU$3 to AU$8.

### Ephara, God of the Polis

Draws a card during each upkeep if a creature entered the battlefield under your control during the previous turn. In a deck with instant-speed creature deployment (flash creatures, bounce effects), Ephara draws consistently every single turn.

**Current AU price**: approximately AU$4 to AU$10.

### Tameshi, Reality Architect

Tameshi returns permanents from the graveyard at the cost of returning lands to your hand (which then re-enter to trigger effects). Loops with cards that care about lands entering the battlefield.

**Current AU price**: approximately AU$2 to AU$5.

### Augustin IV, Grand Arbiter

All spells cost 1 more for opponents and 1 less for you. A tax-based strategy that slows the entire table while enabling you to cast ahead of schedule.

**Current AU price**: approximately AU$5 to AU$12.

### Sephara, Sky's Blade

Seven mana, but castable for 1W with four creatures that have flying tapped. Sephara makes all your flying creatures indestructible and generates Angel tokens on end steps.

**Current AU price**: approximately AU$3 to AU$6.

## More Azorius Commanders Worth Considering

### Raff Capashen, Ship's Mage

All Historic spells (Artifacts, Legends, Sagas) can be cast at instant speed. This converts your entire deck into a reactive toolbox: playing artifacts and legendary creatures on your opponent's end step denies them information about your gameplan and keeps mana available for counterspells.

**Current AU price**: approximately AU$1 to AU$4. Exceptional budget option.

### Isperia, Supreme Judge

Draws a card whenever an opponent attacks you or a planeswalker you control. In multiplayer Commander, opponents attacking each other still triggers Isperia if they swing at you. Generates consistent card draw without relying on your creatures being in play.

**Current AU price**: approximately AU$2 to AU$5.

### Kwain, Itinerant Minstrel

Each player may draw a card and gain 3 life for 2 mana at sorcery speed. Enables group-hug strategies that slow aggressive opponents while building hand size. Deceptively political: opponents are reluctant to attack someone giving them cards.

**Current AU price**: approximately AU$2 to AU$5.

### Teferi, Temporal Archmage

A planeswalker commander with the ability to untap up to four permanents. The combination of untap effects, loyalty abilities, and his static ability (planeswalker loyalty abilities can be activated at instant speed) makes him a powerful control-combo commander.

**Current AU price**: approximately AU$8 to AU$18.

## Azorius Upgrade Path

**Priority 1. Card draw**: Rhystic Study (AU$15 to AU$40) and Smothering Tithe (AU$15 to AU$25) are the two cards that define Azorius at the Commander table. Run both if budget allows.

**Priority 2. Removal package**: Swords to Plowshares (AU$2 to AU$6), Path to Exile (AU$3 to AU$8), and Counterspell (AU$2 to AU$5) form the core interaction suite. These are cheap and essential.

**Priority 3. Board wipes**: Cyclonic Rift (AU$25 to AU$50) is the best one-sided wipe in Commander. Wrath of God (AU$5 to AU$12) and Supreme Verdict (AU$8 to AU$18) cover the symmetrical options.

**Priority 4. Mana base**: Azorius has access to excellent dual lands. Hallowed Fountain (AU$8 to AU$15), Glacial Fortress (AU$2 to AU$5), and Prairie Stream (AU$2 to AU$5) improve consistency significantly over basic lands.

## What Azorius Struggles With

Azorius has no access to green's ramp, red's direct damage, or black's unconditional removal. The primary weaknesses:

**Artifacts and enchantments**: blue and white can remove them through bounce (temporary) and targeted destruction, but neither colour has mass artifact removal. Permanents that create ongoing value can be difficult to answer completely.

**Speed**: Azorius plays reactively. A mono-red Krenko deck can win before Azorius gets its engine running. Knowing when to be proactive is the skill that separates good Azorius pilots from average ones.

**Commander damage from aggro**: blue and white don't have great blockers below 3 mana. Early aggression can chip away at you before your counterspells come online.
