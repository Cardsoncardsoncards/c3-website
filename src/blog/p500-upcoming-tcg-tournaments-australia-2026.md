## Tracking Card Prices for Events

Every event on this list creates demand for specific cards in that game. Competitive players buying cards before a major tournament push up prices on meta staples in the 2 to 4 weeks before the event date.

The most practical approach is to check prices early and buy when you identify what you need. Waiting until the week before an event to finalise your deck is expensive.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I find TCG tournaments near me in Australia?**
Check the event pages for each game's official organiser. For Bandai games, TAK Games (takgames.com.au) runs most Oceania organised play. For Bushiroad games, check en.bushiroad.com. For Magic, check locator.wizards.com and anzmtg.com.au. For Pokemon, check play.limitlesstcg.com. For Yu-Gi-Oh, check yugioh-card.com/en/events. For Riftbound, check locator.riftbound.uvsgames.com.

**Are there TCG tournaments in every Australian state?**
Most major events are concentrated in VIC, NSW, and QLD. WA and SA have events for some games but less frequently. TAS and ACT have smaller regular events. NT has minimal organised play for most TCGs.

**Where is the best place to buy tournament cards in AUD before an event?**
Check [C3](/cards) for current AUD prices first. Compare against eBay AU sold listings and your local game store. Buying 3 to 4 weeks before major events consistently gives better prices than buying the week of.

**Can I submit a local event to this list?**
Local store events and community tournaments can be submitted by contacting Cards on Cards on Cards. We aim to include confirmed events with a registration link or venue address and a confirmed date.

**Is this list updated regularly?**
This post was last updated in June 2026 and covers confirmed events through December 2026. New events are added as they are announced by official organisers.
