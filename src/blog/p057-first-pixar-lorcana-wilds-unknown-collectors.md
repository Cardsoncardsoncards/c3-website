It is a structural expansion of who cares about Lorcana cards, and that has direct implications for the value and desirability of what gets printed in this set and every Pixar-featuring set that follows.

This is not a hype article. It is a clear-eyed look at what the Pixar addition means, which cards matter most for collectors, and what the honest risks are.

## The Significance of the First Pixar Set

Lorcana launched in 2023 drawing from Disney animated films, primarily classics. The first two years covered characters from The Little Mermaid, Beauty and the Beast, The Lion King, Sleeping Beauty, Aladdin, Frozen, Moana, and many others. These are strong IP with deep nostalgia but they represent a specific era and demographic of Disney fandom.

Pixar operates differently. Toy Story, The Incredibles, Brave, Monsters, Inc., Coco, and Turning Red have their own distinct fan bases that do not always overlap with classic Disney animation audiences. A collector who would never buy a Maleficent card may genuinely want an Enchanted Woody. A parent who grew up watching Toy Story with their children sees Buzz Lightyear differently than they see Cinderella.

This is the collector-base expansion that many Lorcana observers have been waiting for since the game launched. The arrival of Pixar is not incremental. It is a new category of person who now has a reason to buy Lorcana cards.

## Which Cards Collectors Should Watch

Full card reveals for Wilds Unknown begin in April 2026. As of early April, the confirmed Pixar character slate is: Woody, Buzz Lightyear, Jessie, Elastigirl / Mrs. Incredible, Jack-Jack Parr, and Merida from Brave. More characters from each franchise will be in the 204-card main set.

Within Lorcana's rarity structure, the cards that hold long-term collector value are:

**Enchanted Rarity Cards**
Enchanted is the highest rarity in Lorcana, featuring full-card artwork with special foiling treatment and appearing approximately once in every four to six booster boxes. Wilds Unknown contains 18 Enchanted cards.

The most valuable Enchanted cards in previous sets have been characters with the broadest fan recognition. Elsa, Simba, Ariel, and Maleficent Enchanted versions have consistently commanded AU$80 to AU$400+ depending on the character and demand.

For Wilds Unknown, the Enchanted versions of Woody, Buzz Lightyear, and Elastigirl are the cards to watch. These characters have immediate mainstream recognition that extends well beyond the Lorcana community. An Enchanted Buzz Lightyear card shown in a glass case on social media will reach Toy Story fans who have never seen a Lorcana booster pack.

**Iconic Rarity Cards**
Wilds Unknown introduces 2 Iconic rarity cards. Iconic is above Enchanted in terms of rarity and visual spectacle. Previous Iconic cards from the Fabled set have been among the rarest and most sought-after cards in Lorcana's history. The two Iconic cards in Wilds Unknown are not yet revealed. If either features a Pixar character, expect significant collector attention.

**Foil Promo Cards from the Two-Player Starter Set**
The Two-Player Starter Set includes foil promo versions of Buzz Lightyear (Amber/Emerald deck) and Elastigirl (Amethyst/Ruby deck). Starter deck exclusives with foil treatments have historically become collector items in their own right because the only way to obtain them is through that specific product. If you want the foil Buzz Lightyear starter promo, you need to buy the starter set.

**Traveler Cycle Promo Cards**
The six Traveler promo cards are exclusive to prerelease packs, available only at local game stores from 8 May. One promo appears at random per prerelease pack. If any Traveler promo features a Pixar character (this has not been confirmed as of writing), that card could command a premium for collectors who missed prerelease events.

## The Honest Collector Risk Assessment

**What works in favour of Wilds Unknown as a collector set:**
The Pixar debut creates genuine new-audience demand. Toy Story in particular has multigenerational appeal. The Enchanted cards featuring these characters are genuinely scarce (approximately 1 in every 4 to 6 boxes). Lorcana's Eternal format means all cards stay legally playable indefinitely, removing the rotation risk that limits collector value in Standard-format TCGs.

**What the risks are:**
Print run is unknown. Ravensburger has significantly expanded production since the supply-tight 2023 and 2024 releases. Higher supply = lower sustained scarcity = lower collector prices. The first set of a new IP type also carries uncertainty around whether the actual cards are as visually impressive as the hype suggests. Enchanted card prices depend heavily on artwork quality, and until the full card list is revealed, you are making assumptions about what the Enchanted Buzz Lightyear actually looks like.

**The balanced position:**
If you want to attend prerelease events and open product for the experience of being first with new Pixar Lorcana cards, that is a perfectly reasonable use of AU$55 to AU$65 per prerelease pack. If you are speculating on sealed booster displays for long-term sealed collector appreciation, the risk is higher and the time horizon needs to be years, not months.

If you want specific Enchanted or Iconic cards from the set, waiting two to four weeks after the 15 May retail release and then buying singles from eBay AU at stabilised prices is the most efficient approach.

## How This Changes the Game Going Forward

Wilds Unknown is not the end of the Pixar expansion. It is the beginning.

Ravensburger has confirmed the full 2026 Lorcana calendar includes Pixar in every remaining set:
- Q3: Attack of the Vine (Monsters, Inc., Turning Red)
- Q4: Unnamed set (Coco confirmed)

For collectors building a Pixar-focused Lorcana collection, the entry point is now. The first-ever Pixar Lorcana cards from Wilds Unknown carry the same kind of milestone status that first-run set cards typically carry. Once Monsters, Inc. appears in Q3, the Toy Story and Incredibles cards from Wilds Unknown are no longer the newest but they remain the first.

This milestone status matters for long-term collector appeal. The first printing of a new character type in any TCG has historically held a premium over later printings of the same character. The first Enchanted Buzz Lightyear, whenever it is pulled, is the first one that has ever existed.

**Will there be an Enchanted Buzz Lightyear card?**
This has not been officially confirmed as of the writing of this article. Given Buzz is a face character for the set and appears in the starter deck, an Enchanted version is broadly expected by the community. This remains unconfirmed.

**What is Iconic rarity?**
Iconic is above Enchanted in rarity and visual treatment. Wilds Unknown contains 2 Iconic cards. Previous Iconic cards from the Fabled set have been among the most valuable individual Lorcana cards produced. The Iconic cards in Wilds Unknown have not been revealed yet.

**Is now a good time to buy earlier Lorcana sets?**
If you want specific characters or chase cards from older sets, eBay AU for singles is the most efficient channel. Older booster displays from popular sets have appreciated somewhat in sealed form but not at a level that makes speculative buying straightforward. Buy what you want to own, not what you hope will increase in value.

From 15 May onwards, Wilds Unknown will be at mass retail. Availability varies by store. Amazon AU is typically the most reliable online source for confirmed stock.
