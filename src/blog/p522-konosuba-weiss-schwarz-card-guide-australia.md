# Konosuba in Weiss Schwarz: The Adventurer Trait and Tri-Colour Builds

Konosuba brings a documented tri-colour approach into Weiss Schwarz, with a red/yellow/blue "Adventurer" trait archetype built around its core party. That three colour structure sets it apart from properties that stay within one or two colours. If you already play and want the archetype detail, jump ahead. If you are newer to Weiss Schwarz, the primer below covers the core rules, with the full mechanics glossary linked at the end.

## The set at a glance

Konosuba spans 4 tracked releases, from the original KonoSuba set (25 August 2017) through KONOSUBA -God's blessing on this wonderful world! Re:Edit in November 2025, totalling 576 cards.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## The Adventurer Trait Archetype

Konosuba's documented tri-colour Adventurer archetype rewards building around the shared trait line that runs across its core party rather than picking one dominant colour and treating the others as a light splash. This is a useful contrast against properties like Love Live, which tends to reward staying within a single sub-series and colour identity rather than spreading across three. A Konosuba deck builder needs to be comfortable managing a three colour mana base from the outset, in exchange for access to the party's full range of abilities across all three colours rather than being locked into one. Practically, this means Konosuba plays differently at the table to most Weiss Schwarz decks, hands need to develop across three colours rather than settling quickly into one dominant identity, which some players find a more engaging deck building puzzle and others find simply less consistent.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Why does Konosuba need three colours instead of one or two?**
The Adventurer trait archetype is built around a shared trait line running across red, yellow, and blue cards from the core party, so a competitive deck is designed to run all three from the outset.

**How does Konosuba's tri-colour approach compare to Love Live's single sub-series focus?**
They sit at opposite ends of the same design space. Love Live rewards staying within one sub-series and colour identity, while Konosuba rewards spreading across three colours through the shared Adventurer trait.

**Is a 4 set catalogue enough to fully realise the Adventurer archetype?**
Yes, the compact catalogue still supports the full tri-colour build, the smaller set count affects breadth of options more than the archetype's core viability.

**Do older Konosuba sets remain legal in tournament play?**
Weiss Schwarz does not use a rotating standard format, so older Konosuba cards generally remain playable, though always check a specific event's rules before entering.

**Is a three colour deck harder to build than a mono or dual colour one?**
It requires more careful mana base management, but the payoff is access to the full party's abilities across all three colours rather than being limited to whichever colour a single character belongs to.

**How does the four copy limit affect building the tri-colour Adventurer archetype?**
It forces deliberate choices about which characters across all three colours genuinely support each other, rather than allowing a deck builder to simply max out individually strong cards without regard to colour balance.
