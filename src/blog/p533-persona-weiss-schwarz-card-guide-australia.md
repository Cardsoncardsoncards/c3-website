# Persona in Weiss Schwarz: Three Games, One Neo-Standard Pool

Persona's Weiss Schwarz catalogue spans three different entries in the wider Persona game franchise, Persona 3 Reload, Persona 4, and Persona 5, and unlike some multi-game properties in this catalogue, two of these are confirmed to actually combine under neo-standard construction rules. If you already play and want the cross-set detail, jump ahead. If you are newer to Weiss Schwarz, the primer below covers the core rules, with the full mechanics glossary linked at the end.

## The set at a glance

Persona spans 3 tracked releases, Persona 4 ver.E (24 October 2014), Persona 5 (July 2017), and Persona 3 Reload Premium Booster (March 2025), totalling 474 cards.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## Neo-Standard Compatibility Between Persona 5 and Persona 4

Confirmed directly via official product listings, Persona 5's trial deck material is built as neo-standard with Persona 4 ver.E, meaning the two can be combined under neo-standard construction rules rather than treated as fully separate collecting lanes, similar to how Madoka Magica's Magia Record material and Date A Live's booster releases extend their own respective card pools. Persona 3 Reload, released as a Premium Booster, sits somewhat separately in the confirmed data, so if you are planning a deck around it specifically, treat the Persona 4 and Persona 5 compatibility as its own distinct combination rather than assuming Persona 3 material automatically joins in, unless you can confirm that directly.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do Persona 4 and Persona 5 cards actually combine in one deck?**
Yes, confirmed via official product listings, Persona 5's trial deck material is built as neo-standard with Persona 4 ver.E, allowing the two to combine under neo-standard construction rules.

**Does Persona 3 Reload also combine with Persona 4 and Persona 5 under neo-standard rules?**
This has not been confirmed in the data available to us. Persona 3 Reload was released as a separate Premium Booster, so treat it as its own collecting lane unless you can confirm compatibility directly.

**Which Persona game is the best starting point for a new collector?**
That depends on which game you are a fan of. If you want the added flexibility of neo-standard construction, starting with Persona 4 or Persona 5 gives you access to both through their confirmed compatibility.

**Do older Persona sets remain legal in tournament play?**
Weiss Schwarz does not use a rotating standard format, so older cards generally remain playable, though always check a specific event's rules and neo-standard construction requirements before entering.

**Can I build a deck mixing all three Persona games?**
Persona 4 and Persona 5 are confirmed to combine under neo-standard rules, but Persona 3 Reload's compatibility with the other two has not been confirmed, so check current construction rules before assuming a three-game deck is supported.

**How does the four copy limit affect a property spanning three different games?**
It reinforces committing to one game's protagonist and cast as your core rather than trying to spread four-of choices thinly across all three games and their separate casts.

## What to check before buying

1. Confirm which of the three Persona games a listing belongs to before buying, since protagonists and casts do not overlap between games.
2. Check whether a card is base rarity or a signed variant before paying a premium, since rarity structures differ release to release.
3. Before assuming a mixed Persona 3, 4, and 5 deck is supported, confirm current neo-standard construction rules, since only Persona 4 and Persona 5 compatibility is confirmed at this time.
