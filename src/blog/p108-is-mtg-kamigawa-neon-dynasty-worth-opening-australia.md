## What Is Kamigawa: Neon Dynasty and Why Does It Matter for Australian Players?

Neon Dynasty is set on the plane of Kamigawa, returning 1,200 years after the original Kamigawa block (2004) in a neon-lit technological future. The set introduces Channel lands — dual-faced cards that are either lands or powerful spells — which became immediate format staples across Standard, Modern, Legacy, and Commander. The Neon Ink treatment is one of the rarest and most valuable special treatments in modern MTG: cards printed in five different ink colours (red, blue, green, yellow, and the ultra-rare gold) with gold Neon Ink versions worth AU$200+ each.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Kamigawa: Neon Dynasty worth buying in Australia?**
Neon Dynasty has one of the strongest EV profiles of any standard set from the 2022-2024 era. Channel lands, The Wandering Emperor, Fable of the Mirror-Breaker, and Farewell are all format staples with sustained AU demand. At AU$180 it represents strong value. Use the C3 EV Calculator for a current personalised assessment.

**What are Neon Ink cards and how rare are they?**
Neon Ink cards are versions of specific Neon Dynasty mythics printed in coloured ink — red, blue, green, yellow, or gold. Gold Neon Ink is the rarest and can be worth AU$200-500+. Any Neon Ink pull is exceptional. They are found at very low rates in Set and Collector Boosters.

**What are Channel lands and why are they valuable?**
Channel lands are double-faced cards with a land on one side and a powerful spell on the other. The Channel ability lets you discard the land for a one-time effect. Cards like Boseiju Who Endures (destroys noncreature permanents) and Otawara Soaring City (bounces permanents) are staples across Modern and Legacy because they generate value even when you draw too many lands.

**How does Neon Dynasty compare to other 2022 sets for EV?**
Neon Dynasty significantly outperforms other 2022 standard sets in EV terms. Kamigawa and Double Masters 2022 are the two standout 2022 products for value. Dominaria United, Streets of New Capenna, and The Brothers War all have lower average card values by comparison.
