# Frieren Beyond Journey's End Weiss Schwarz Card Guide (Australia)

Frieren: Beyond Journey's End follows the near immortal elf mage Frieren as she reflects on her past adventures and the fleeting nature of human life after the defeat of the Demon King. The anime became one of the most critically praised releases of its year, and its Weiss Schwarz set has been one of the more consistently strong sellers in the current English catalogue as a result.

## The set at a glance

Frieren is a single stand-alone set, released 22 November 2024, with a total of 292 cards. There has been no second English wave for this property to date, so the full available collection currently sits in this one release. The set code is SFN, and the card pool features newly drawn illustrations of Frieren, Himmel, Fern, Stark, Heiter, Eisen, Sein, Aura, and Serie, giving genuine coverage across the show's core party and several key supporting characters.

The base release consisted of 111 normal type cards and 111 parallel types, packed 8 cards per pack, 12 packs per display, 24 displays per carton. A second edition booster box has also been produced to meet ongoing demand, which is itself a signal of how well this set has continued to sell after its initial release.

## Deck building around Frieren and the party

The card pool splits naturally between Frieren herself, whose long lived perspective and powerful magic anchor most competitive builds, and the rest of the original hero party, Himmel, Heiter, and Eisen, whose cards carry the emotional weight of the show's flashback structure. Fern and Stark, the younger generation travelling with Frieren in the present timeline, round out the set with their own distinct playstyles.

Because the anime moves between past and present so deliberately, the card pool reflects that structure well, giving collectors and players a genuine choice between building around the original party's nostalgia driven synergies or the newer generation's more straightforward combat focused cards.

## Chase cards and signature pulls

Every pack carries a chance of a hot-stamped signed card from the voice cast, including Atsumi Tanezaki as Frieren, Nobuhiko Okamoto as Himmel, Kana Ichinose as Fern, and Chiaki Kobayashi as Stark, along with signatures from the supporting cast. Display boxes included one of six Going First and Going Second box toppers, and cartons carried an English exclusive stitched playmat.

Given the show's critical acclaim and broad audience, demand for top rarity Frieren and Himmel cards in particular has stayed strong well past the initial release window, which is reflected in the decision to produce a second edition booster box rather than letting the set quietly sell out and disappear from shelves.

## Why Australian collectors are buying into this set

Frieren stands apart from a lot of recent isekai and fantasy adjacent anime because of its critical reception. It was not just popular, it was widely regarded as one of the best anime of its year by critics and audiences alike, and that kind of reception tends to produce longer lasting collector demand than a purely hype driven release. The show's themes around mortality, memory, and the passage of time have also resonated broadly, pulling in viewers who do not typically follow fantasy anime.

For Australian buyers, that combination of critical acclaim and broad audience reach makes Frieren one of the steadier long term holds among recent Weiss Schwarz releases, less prone to the sharp price swings you sometimes see on hype driven seasonal hits. It is a genuinely good candidate for a collector who wants exposure to a modern anime property without the volatility that comes with brand new, unproven releases.

## Why a second edition print run matters

Weiss Schwarz does not automatically reprint every set that sells well. A second edition booster box is a deliberate business decision that reflects genuinely sustained demand rather than a one time launch spike. For Frieren, this is a useful signal for collectors trying to judge whether a set's popularity is likely to hold up over the medium term. Sets that need a second print run tend to have broader, more durable fan interest than sets that sell through once and are never revisited, which is a meaningful difference if you are weighing this property against other current releases for long term collecting value.

That said, a reprint can also affect scarcity calculations for first edition specific box toppers and packaging variants. If you are specifically chasing first edition exclusive items rather than just the cards themselves, confirm which edition a listing is from before buying, since the two are not interchangeable for that purpose even though the core card pool is shared.

## What to check before buying

1. Confirm whether a listing is from the first or second edition booster box, since box topper distribution and packaging can differ slightly between print runs.
2. Check whether a card is base rarity or a signed voice cast version before paying a premium, since Frieren and Himmel signatures in particular carry a genuine price gap over standard versions.
3. Compare several listings before buying higher rarity cards from the original party members, since demand has remained steady rather than dropping off, and prices can vary between sellers.
