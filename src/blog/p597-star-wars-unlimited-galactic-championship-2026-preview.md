The Star Wars Unlimited Galactic Championship runs from 24 to 26 July 2026 at the Venetian Resort
in Las Vegas, the same venue as last year. It's the single biggest event on the Organized Play
calendar, capping off an entire season of qualifiers with the crowning of a new Galactic Champion.
More than 3,000 players, collectors and spectators are expected across the weekend.

## A new format for 2026

This year's Championship is a genuine change from the 2025 debut. Fantasy Flight has confirmed the
event will be a hybrid, combining Premier Constructed with limited play for the first time, so
competitors need to be sharp in Draft and Sealed as well as their usual deck building. The main
event itself runs 13 Swiss rounds across two days before cutting to a Top 8 single elimination
bracket.

## How players qualified

Getting an invite requires 100 Qualifier Points earned across the season's Planetary Qualifiers and
other Competitive events, with a single Planetary win worth exactly 100 points on its own. Players
who didn't reach that total during the season still get one more shot through the Last Chance
Qualifier, held on the Friday of Championship weekend. The 32 players who earned the most points
across the season also receive paid flights and hotel for the trip.

## The card pool going in

The main event uses Premier Constructed on the Sets 4 to 7 card pool, meaning Ashes of the Empire,
released 17 July, is not tournament legal for this weekend's main event. Recent Planetary
Qualifiers on that same pool have seen leaders like Lando Calrissian, Leia Organa, Mother Talzin,
Darth Vader and Obi-Wan Kenobi consistently making top standings. Expect the winning decklist to be
built on cards that have already proven themselves over months of Qualifier play rather than
anything brand new.

## Defending the title

Filip Szkudlarek won the first ever Galactic Championship in 2025, and Season One of Organized Play
has been building toward this weekend as its natural conclusion. Season Two starts once this event
wraps, so the result here effectively resets the clock for everyone chasing next year's invite.

## Beyond the main event

Away from the Top 8 chase, the Premier Open runs as a two day side tournament that doesn't require
any Qualifier Points to enter, and it still awards prize wall tickets and points toward next
season's Championship. The Eternal Open gives players a separate side event outside the current
Premier pool entirely. Between the main event, side tournaments and the usual meet ups, merchandise
and activities, the weekend is built to have something for competitors and casual attendees alike.
