## What Is Modern Horizons 3 and Why Does It Matter for Australian Players?

Modern Horizons 3 released June 2024 as the third set in the Modern Horizons series following MH1 (2019) and MH2 (2021). All three sets have produced cards that immediately became format staples and maintained long-term value. MH3 introduces new Eldrazi cards, retro frame versions of powerful staples, and the full Nadu Winged Wisdom debacle — a mythic so powerful it received emergency bans across multiple formats. The Play Booster box contains 36 packs with 14 cards each. A Collector Booster box (AU$320+) offers higher foil density and retro frame treatments.

## Chase Cards and What to Look For

The Eldrazi mythic cycle anchored by Emrakul the World Anew is the headline target. Retro frame versions of dual lands and powerful staples are the collector prize. Nadu Winged Wisdom achieved the rare distinction of being banned in Modern, Legacy, and Commander — it is still worth AU$15-30+ as a banned card due to casual play demand. The full uncommon Evoke cycle and various Modern staple rares make the floor value of this set significantly higher than standard releases.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Modern Horizons 3 worth opening in Australia?**
Modern Horizons 3 has one of the strongest EV profiles of any MTG set available on Amazon AU. The premium design targeting Modern and Legacy means floor rares have genuine format value. At AU$180 for a Play Booster box it represents strong value. Use the C3 EV Calculator for a current personalised verdict.

**What is the difference between MH3 Play Boosters and Collector Boosters?**
Play Boosters at AU$180 per box are the standard product and the basis for the EV calculation. Collector Boosters at AU$320+ per box offer higher foil frequency, retro frame cards, and exclusive treatments not available in Play Boosters. Collector Boosters have higher EV potential but also higher cost and more variance.

**Was Nadu Winged Wisdom really that powerful?**
Yes. Nadu was banned in Modern, Legacy, and Commander — one of the fastest emergency ban sequences in MTG history. Despite the bans it retains casual value around AU$15-30+ because the card is still legal in Vintage and many casual formats. It is a notable pull even from a banned card perspective.

**Should I buy MH3 singles or open packs?**
For specific Modern staples, buying singles from the C3 eBay store is more cost-efficient than opening packs. If you enjoy the opening experience or are building a complete set, a box at AU$180 offers reasonable EV for a premium product.
