# JoJo's Bizarre Adventure in Weiss Schwarz: Stand Users on the Stage

JoJo's Bizarre Adventure brings one of the most recognisable art styles in anime into Weiss Schwarz, and its card pool is built around the confirmed Stand User and Stand character traits that anchor the whole franchise. This property gives collectors and players a genuine cross-arc card pool to work with. If you already play and want the Stand mechanics detail, jump ahead. If you are newer to Weiss Schwarz, the primer below covers the core rules, with the full mechanics glossary linked at the end.

## The set at a glance

JoJo's Bizarre Adventure spans 3 tracked releases, Golden Wind (27 March 2020), and Stone Ocean and Stardust Crusaders Premium Boosters, both released together on 4 October 2024, totalling 507 cards.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## Stand Users and Stands as the Mechanical Throughline

JoJo's confirmed card text builds around 《Stand User》 and 《Stand》 character traits, meaning cards regularly check for these traits when triggering abilities, giving a genuine mechanical throughline across characters from different story arcs. Cards across Stardust Crusaders, Stone Ocean, and Golden Wind check for these shared traits, which means trait synergy is a genuine cross-arc consideration even though each arc's named cast, Jotaro and his allies in Stardust Crusaders, Jolyne in Stone Ocean, Giorno in Golden Wind, is distinct. A JoJo deck typically settles on one arc as its core, since named characters and colour identity differ between arcs, while still leaning on the shared Stand trait mechanics underneath the whole property, a similar structural pattern to Attack on Titan's Eldian and Corps trait system.

## Chase cards and signature pulls

Confirmed card text shows climax combos built around the Stand User and Stand traits directly, for example effects that search or recover a Stand User or Stand character from the waiting room when a climax combo triggers. ## Frequently Asked Questions

**What do the Stand User and Stand traits actually do in this card pool?**
They are confirmed character trait types that card abilities check for directly, cards built around these traits can search, recover, or boost other Stand User or Stand characters, giving a real mechanical throughline across different arcs.

**Which JoJo arcs are currently adapted into Weiss Schwarz?**
Stardust Crusaders, Stone Ocean, and Golden Wind are confirmed as the three releases currently in the catalogue, covering Jotaro, Jolyne, and Giorno's respective arcs.

**Is a 3 set catalogue enough to represent such a long running franchise?**
It represents the three specific arcs currently adapted rather than the franchise's full arc list, so treat it as a curated selection rather than complete franchise coverage.

**Do older JoJo sets remain legal in tournament play?**
Weiss Schwarz does not use a rotating standard format, so older cards generally remain playable, though always check a specific event's rules before entering.

**Should I build a deck mixing characters from different arcs?**
Most decks commit to one arc's named cast as the core, since colour identity and character lines differ between arcs, while still benefiting from the shared Stand trait synergy that runs underneath the whole property.

**How does the four copy limit affect building around three different arcs?**
It reinforces committing to one arc's named cast rather than mixing Jotaro, Jolyne, and Giorno's card lines into a single deck, since you only have four copies of any single card to build a specific arc's line around.
