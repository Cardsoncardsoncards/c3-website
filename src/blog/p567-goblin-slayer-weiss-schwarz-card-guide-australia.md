# Goblin Slayer Weiss Schwarz Card Guide (Australia)

Goblin Slayer follows a stoic, helmeted adventurer who has dedicated his life entirely to hunting down goblins, using cold, methodical tactics rather than heroics. Based on the light novel series that has sold more than 3 million copies, the anime aired to strong popularity in late 2018, and its Weiss Schwarz set has remained one of the steadier dark fantasy properties on the platform since release.

## The set at a glance

Goblin Slayer is a single stand-alone set in English, released 31 January 2020, with a total of 165 cards. It has not received a second English wave, so this remains the complete Goblin Slayer collection currently available. The set code is GBS, and the release featured 100 base card types plus 28 parallel versions, packed 8 cards per pack, 20 packs per display, 16 displays per carton.

The set reenacts the world of Goblin Slayer through its full card pool, covering Goblin Slayer himself alongside Priestess, High Elf Archer, Cow Girl, and Sword Maiden, the core cast of adventurers who accompany or support him through the story.

## Deck building around Goblin Slayer and the party

Because the anime's central hook is a single minded, tactically focused protagonist, the card pool reflects that with Goblin Slayer himself typically anchoring the most straightforward, methodical builds. Priestess and High Elf Archer provide support oriented cards that round out the party's mixed skill set, while Sword Maiden brings a distinct, more powerful presence drawn from her role later in the story. Cow Girl, while less central to combat mechanics, remains a fan favourite pull thanks to her recurring presence throughout the series as Goblin Slayer's grounding connection to ordinary life outside adventuring.

This gives Goblin Slayer a reasonably approachable structure for new players, since the core party is small and each character has a clearly defined role, rather than requiring you to parse a sprawling ensemble cast to figure out what synergises with what.

## Chase cards and signature pulls

Every pack carries a chance of a signed card from the voice cast, including Yuuichirou Umehara as Goblin Slayer, Yui Ogura as Priestess, Nao Toyama as High Elf Archer, Yuka Iguchi as Cow Girl, and Aya Endo as Sword Maiden. Display boxes also included one of five exclusive PR cards, and first print run cartons carried four English Edition exclusive rubber playmats.

Signed Sword Maiden and Goblin Slayer cards have shown up regularly in graded card sales at a genuine premium over standard rarities, reflecting sustained demand from the show's dedicated fanbase years after the original release. Priestess signatures, while typically less expensive than Sword Maiden, still attract steady bidding given her central role across the story's early arcs.

## Why this property has held up well

Dark fantasy as a subgenre tends to age better than lighter, more disposable seasonal comedies, and Goblin Slayer is a solid example of that pattern. Its premise, world building, and central character concept have stayed distinctive enough to keep fan interest alive well past the initial anime run, which has translated into steady rather than spiking demand for the card set.

For Australian collectors, that steadiness is the main appeal here. Goblin Slayer is not a set likely to produce sudden price spikes, but it has also not seen the kind of decline that some short lived licensed sets experience once initial hype fades, making it a reasonably safe, low volatility addition to a broader anime TCG collection. It suits a patient collector more than one chasing fast returns.

## Goblin Slayer compared to other dark fantasy licensed sets

Weiss Schwarz has released relatively few genuinely dark, mature toned fantasy properties compared to its much larger catalogue of lighter isekai and school life anime. That scarcity is worth factoring in if you are specifically drawn to darker themed collectibles, since Goblin Slayer occupies a fairly distinct niche on the platform rather than competing directly against a large group of similar releases.

That niche positioning has a practical upside for collectors. With fewer directly comparable sets competing for the same buyer base, demand for Goblin Slayer has stayed relatively consistent rather than being diluted across several similar dark fantasy options the way demand for, say, isekai comedy sets can be split across a dozen similar releases in a given year. If dark fantasy specifically is your collecting focus, this remains one of the more established entry points on the platform.

## What to check before buying

1. Confirm whether a listing is base rarity or the signed voice cast version before paying a premium, since photos should clearly show the signature rather than just describing it in the title.
2. Check condition carefully on older stock. As a 2020 release, some circulating copies show genuine wear, so factor that into what you are willing to pay.
3. Compare several listings for Sword Maiden cards specifically, since she tends to carry the strongest sustained demand of the set's five main characters.
