# Madoka Magica in Weiss Schwarz: A Green/Blue Cast and Neo-Standard Compatibility

Puella Magi Madoka Magica's Weiss Schwarz catalogue centres on a green/blue trial deck colour identity and a confirmed core cast of Homura Akemi, Madoka Kaname, Sayaka Miki, and Kyoko Sakura. What sets this property apart is a genuine cross-set deck building feature, the Magia Record spin-off material is explicitly compatible with neo-standard construction, changing how you can build with these cards compared to properties where every set stands entirely alone. If you already play and want the archetype detail, jump ahead. If you are newer to Weiss Schwarz, the primer below covers the core rules, with the full mechanics glossary linked at the end.

## The set at a glance

Madoka Magica spans 4 tracked releases, from the original set (31 May 2013) through the two Magia Record spin-off releases in 2021, totalling 615 cards.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## Neo-Standard Compatibility with Magia Record

The most distinctive feature of Madoka Magica's Weiss Schwarz presence is that its Magia Record spin-off sets are explicitly built to be compatible with neo-standard construction. Cards from the Magia Record material are not walled off from the rest of your deck the way spin-off cards sometimes are in other properties, they can be combined with core series cards within neo-standard rules, giving deck builders genuine cross-set flexibility. With a confirmed core cast of four magical girls, the four copy limit is exactly why building a deck usually means picking which of the four to lean on most heavily rather than spreading evenly across all of them.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What does neo-standard compatibility actually mean for Magia Record cards?**
It means Magia Record spin-off cards can be combined with core Madoka Magica series cards within neo-standard construction rules, rather than being treated as a separate, isolated card pool.

**Who are the confirmed core characters in Madoka Magica's catalogue?**
Homura Akemi, Madoka Kaname, Sayaka Miki, and Kyoko Sakura form the confirmed core cast across the catalogue.

**Should a new collector buy core series sets or Magia Record material first?**
The core series sets built around the confirmed four magical girls are the natural starting point, with Magia Record as a worthwhile follow-up once you understand how its neo-standard compatibility fits your deck.

**Do older Madoka Magica sets remain legal in tournament play?**
Weiss Schwarz does not use a rotating standard format, so older cards generally remain playable, though always check a specific event's rules and any neo-standard construction requirements before entering.

**Is green/blue the only viable colour pairing for a Madoka Magica deck?**
Green and blue is the trial deck colour identity and the clearest starting point for new players, giving a defined pairing rather than a wide spread of colour choices.

**How does the four copy limit affect a deck built around four confirmed core characters?**
It forces a choice, since you cannot run four copies of every one of Homura, Madoka, Sayaka, and Kyoko at once, most decks lean more heavily on one or two of the four rather than spreading evenly.

## What to check before buying

1. Confirm whether a listing is core series or Magia Record material before buying, since the two are separately printed even though they combine under neo-standard rules.
2. Check whether a card is base rarity or a signed variant before paying a premium, particularly on Homura and Madoka, the deepest characters for rarity variants.
3. If you plan to mix core series and Magia Record cards under neo-standard rules, confirm current neo-standard eligibility for both sets before finalising a competitive deck list.
