Digimon Card Game's next mainline booster, Timeless Bonds (BT-26), releases in Japan on 28 and 29
August 2026, with the English version following on 4 September. It's the third set in a row tying
into Digimon Story: Time Stranger, continuing directly from BT-24 and BT-25, and it draws heavily
on characters from Digimon Beatbreak and Digimon Savers. For Australian players who follow Digimon
primarily through English product, this is the third instalment in a row to plan around rather than
a standalone release.

## What's actually in the set

BT-26 totals 108 card types, split across 40 Commons, 26 Uncommons, 21 to 22 Rares, 12 Super Rares,
2 Secret Rares and a run of alternate art and special cards. Early previews have shown off
Jupitermon, Chronomon: Holy Mode, BloomLordmon and Ravmon, giving a solid early read on the power
level the set is aiming for even before the full card list drops.

## Part of a longer Time Stranger arc

Digimon Story: Time Stranger has now anchored three consecutive mainline boosters, which is a
longer tie-in run than most Digimon sets get to a single source game. Cards carrying the Time
Stranger trait are designed to let players recreate moments from that game directly in the card
game, so if you've been collecting pieces of that mechanic since BT-24, Timeless Bonds is where
that build-out continues rather than starting fresh.

## What comes right after it

Bandai has already previewed the set that follows, EX-13 Chivalrous XIII, due for English release 2
October and Japanese release 3 October, featuring ULforceVeedramon, Rina and Kentaurosmon in early
art reveals. That means Digimon's release cadence stays busy through the rest of the northern
autumn, worth knowing if you're budgeting across multiple sets rather than just this one.

## What this means for Australian players

A five to six week gap between the Japanese and English release is fairly standard for Digimon, so
Australian players buying English product should expect to see early competitive results and card
evaluations from Japan well before Timeless Bonds is actually available to buy locally. That's
useful information if you want to plan a deck ahead of release, just don't expect to actually build
it until early September.

## Following the Time Stranger build-out

If you've been collecting Time Stranger trait cards since BT-24, Timeless Bonds is the third
instalment in a deliberate long-term build rather than a one-off tie-in. That's a reasonable signal
Bandai intends to keep supporting the archetype for a while yet, so cards from BT-24 and BT-25 that
synergise with the Time Stranger mechanic are worth holding onto rather than trading away, even if
they haven't seen much competitive play so far. New players picking up the archetype for the first
time should expect Timeless Bonds to meaningfully deepen what's already a three-set-deep pool
rather than starting them from scratch.

## What the new preview art suggests

Jupitermon, Chronomon: Holy Mode, BloomLordmon and Ravmon are all high-tier Digimon in the anime and
games they originate from, which typically signals a set built around powerful evolution lines
rather than support pieces alone. Once the full spoiler list lands, it's worth checking whether
these headline Digimon slot into existing decks as finishers or whether they anchor an entirely new
archetype, since that changes whether Timeless Bonds is a set worth building around or simply
splashing a card or two from.
