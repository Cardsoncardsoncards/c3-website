Most Australian home contents insurance policies cover collectibles — but the coverage is often capped unless you specifically declare high-value items. A TCG collection worth AU$5,000 or more is likely uncovered or undercovered unless you've taken specific steps.

This guide covers how to document and insure a TCG collection in Australia.

## Does Home Contents Insurance Cover Cards?

**Short answer**: usually yes, with significant limitations.

Standard home contents insurance in Australia covers household items up to a policy limit. Trading cards are typically categorised as collectibles, which may have:

- A sub-limit (e.g., collectibles covered up to AU$2,500 per item or AU$10,000 total collectibles)
- An exclusion for "cards" in some policies
- A requirement to specifically schedule high-value items

**The risk**: if your home is burglarised or damaged, and you have a AU$10,000 card collection that wasn't specifically scheduled on your policy, you may receive nothing or receive a fraction of replacement cost.

**Action**: read your current policy's collectibles section. Call your insurer and ask specifically whether trading cards are covered and what the sub-limit is.

## How to Value Your Collection for Insurance Purposes

Insurance requires you to state a replacement value — what it would cost to replace the item at current market prices.

**Step 1: Use the C3 free collection tracker**

The [free C3 collection tracker](/tracker.html) is designed for exactly this. Log every card, its condition, its current market value. The tracker's profit/loss column shows total current value automatically.

Update the tracker before submitting any insurance documentation.

**Step 2: Support with eBay AU sold data**

For cards worth AU$50 or more individually, download or screenshot eBay AU sold listings showing recent sold prices for comparable condition cards. This provides independent third-party verification of your claimed value.

**Step 3: Check C3 MTG Card Vault for MTG cards**

The [C3 MTG Card Vault](/cards/mtg) shows current AUD pricing for every MTG card. Use the card pages as documentation for insurer queries about MTG card values.

## What Documentation to Prepare

For an insurance claim to succeed, you need evidence of:

**Ownership**: receipts, eBay purchase history, dated photographs of the collection

**Value at time of loss**: your tracker printout, eBay sold data, price guide references

**Condition**: dated photographs showing card condition (Near Mint, Lightly Played, etc.)

**The most common reason insurance claims fail for collections**: no documentation existed. A claim that says "I had cards worth AU$15,000" without supporting evidence is difficult to pay.

## Getting a Scheduled Item Endorsement

For cards worth AU$500 or more individually, request a "scheduled item" or "specified item" endorsement on your policy. This lists the item specifically with its value and insures it for that full amount (rather than a sub-limit).

Insurers may require:

- A professional valuation (some accept your own documentation for lower values)
- Photographs
- Proof of purchase

Some specialty insurance providers cover collectibles and trading cards specifically. These may be more appropriate if your collection exceeds AU$20,000.

## The Practical First Step

**Today**: photograph your entire collection. Date the photos. Store them in Google Photos or similar cloud storage where the date is recorded.

**This week**: update your [C3 tracker](/tracker.html) with current values for your 20 most valuable cards.

**This month**: call your insurer and ask specifically about trading card coverage.

This 3-step process takes a few hours and potentially protects thousands of dollars of collection value.
