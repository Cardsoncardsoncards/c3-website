Sword Art Online has one of the longest running card pools in Weiss Schwarz, covering the franchise's sprawling arc structure from Aincrad through to its later story lines. ## The set at a glance

SAO is one of the deepest catalogues in the whole Weiss Schwarz line up, with 12 tracked releases spanning 19 July 2013 through to the most recent SAO Alternative: Gun Gale Online 2 Extra Booster in September 2025, totalling roughly 1,750 cards across the full run.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. ## Sword Art Online's Documented Archetypes

Two build styles show up consistently across SAO's card pool, and they play quite differently from each other. SAO's own colour identity sits primarily in blue and yellow, unusual among licensed properties, most lean on one dominant colour with a light splash rather than building around two from the start.

**Sinon-focused builds** lean into Sinon's line of cards and the ranged, precision-oriented playstyle her character represents, rewarding patience and holding key pieces back until the right climax combo lines up.

**Meta Blue/Green** is the other well-documented archetype. Rather than staying purely within SAO's native blue and yellow, this build brings in a green splash to enable additional combo lines, making it one of the more flexible ways to build competitively if you are willing to run outside the property's default colour pairing.

Because SAO spans so many arcs, from the original Aincrad storyline through later expansions, players building around either archetype usually need to be deliberate about which sets they are pulling from rather than assuming every SAO card slots neatly into either build.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Why does SAO use two colours instead of one primary colour like most other properties?**
SAO's card pool was built around a blue and yellow identity from early on, so decks naturally run two colours as their core rather than one dominant colour with a small splash.

**Do I need to pick between Sinon-focused and Meta Blue/Green, or can I combine them?**
They are documented as two separate build styles with different colour requirements. Most competitive decks commit to one rather than mixing both.

**Which arc is the best starting point for a new SAO collector?**
The original Aincrad arc generally gives the clearest introduction to Kirito and Asuna's core cards before moving into later arc-specific material.

**Do older SAO sets remain legal in tournament play?**
Weiss Schwarz does not use a rotating standard format, so older SAO cards generally remain playable, though it is worth checking a specific event's rules before entering.

## What to check before buying

1. Confirm which of the 12 SAO releases a listing is from before buying, since arcs and characters repeat across sets and it is easy to mix up an era.
