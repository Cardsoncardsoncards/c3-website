Cardfight Vanguard has a genuinely busy month ahead. DZ Special Series 15, The Legendary Vanguards,
lands 31 July, DZ Booster Set 16, Parallactic Dawn, releases in Japan on 7 August, and the English
version of DZ Booster Set 15, Strike of Illusionary Shadows, arrives 28 August. For English-focused
Australian players, that last date is the one to circle.

## Strike of Illusionary Shadows is the one to watch

Strike of Illusionary Shadows already released in Japan back in June, so the 28 August English
date is catching Australian and other English-market players up to a set that's had two months of
real competitive data behind it overseas. The set includes support across five nations, Dragon
Empire, Dark States, Brandt Gate, Keter Sanctuary and Stoicheia, and features 99 serial-numbered,
gold-foiled versions of Dragonic Overlord as a standout chase card.

## Why the Japan and English dates split

Vanguard runs separate Japanese and English release calendars rather than a simultaneous model, so
a set's Japanese release date and its English release date can sit months apart. Parallactic Dawn
is a clear example, it launches in Japan on 7 August but isn't scheduled for English release until
30 October. That gap matters if you're following Japanese tournament results for deck-building
ideas well before the English cards are actually available to buy locally.

## What DZ Special Series 15 brings

The Legendary Vanguards releases 31 July as a Special Series product, typically a smaller,
more curated release than a full numbered booster set. Special Series products tend to focus on
reprinting or re-presenting popular cards in new treatments rather than introducing an entirely new
card pool, making them a common entry point for newer players or a top-up for existing decks rather
than a format-defining release.

## What this means for Australian players

If you're buying English product, Strike of Illusionary Shadows on 28 August is the set actually
worth planning around, since it's the one landing in the format you're playing. If you follow
Japanese spoilers or tournament results for early information, keep in mind that whatever performs
well in Parallactic Dawn's Japanese debut in August won't be purchasable in English until the very
end of October, so treat early Japanese results as a preview rather than something to build around
immediately.

## What to actually do with three releases stacking up

Prioritise Strike of Illusionary Shadows if you're building for local English events, since it's
the only one of the three that will actually be legal and available to buy in the format you're
playing this month. Treat Parallactic Dawn's Japanese debut as advance notice rather than something
to act on immediately, and use DZ Special Series 15 mainly as a chance to pick up reprints or
alternate treatments of cards you already know are good, rather than expecting a wave of brand new
staples from it.

## Why the nation split matters for deck building

Vanguard decks are built around specific nations rather than a single shared colour system, so a
set supporting five nations at once, as Strike of Illusionary Shadows does, spreads its impact
across the format rather than concentrating it in one archetype. Players building Dragon Empire or
Dark States decks specifically should get more direct value out of this set than players in
nations the set doesn't touch, so check the nation breakdown against your own deck before assuming
the set is a must-buy.
