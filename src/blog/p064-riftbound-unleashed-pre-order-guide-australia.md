## What Is Riftbound?

Riftbound is a competitive trading card game developed by Riot Games, released in 2024. The game uses League of Legends IP including champions, abilities, and lore. It is designed from the ground up as a competitive game with a defined tournament structure, global support from Riot, and a focused mechanical identity built around champion builds, equipment cards, and ability sequencing.

The game has three sets: Origins (the base set), Spiritforged (the first expansion), and Unleashed (this release). Each new set adds new champions, new equipment options, and new mechanics that expand the competitive format rather than replacing it.

Riftbound is distinct from other TCGs in the Australian market in two ways. It has direct corporate backing from one of the largest gaming companies in the world, which provides a level of event support and print run stability that independent TCG publishers cannot match. It also targets an existing global audience of League of Legends players, many of whom have never bought a physical TCG before.

## Should You Buy Unleashed?

### If You Already Play Riftbound

Yes. Unleashed is a competitive expansion, not a standalone product. If you are playing Riftbound in the current format with Origins and Spiritforged cards, Unleashed adds new champions, new equipment options, and new cards that will affect the competitive meta. Buy the set and update your decks accordingly. Check spoilers in the two weeks before release to identify which specific cards matter for your current champion archetype.

### If You Want to Start Riftbound

Start with Origins, not Unleashed. The Origins starter set is the correct entry point for new players. It includes a complete playable deck, teaches the core game mechanics, and gives you a foundation to build from. Unleashed is an expansion and assumes familiarity with the game's base systems.

Once you have played through the Origins starter set and understand the champion and equipment system, buying into Unleashed makes sense as your first expansion purchase.

### If You Are Curious But Not Committed

Attend a local game store launch event on or around 8 May. Riot runs structured events for Riftbound releases that are designed to be accessible to new players. Trying the game at a launch event before buying sealed product is the lowest-risk way to assess whether Riftbound suits your play style.

### If You Collect Multiple TCGs

Riftbound occupies a specific niche. It is a competitive-first game with limited casual or collector appeal compared to Pokemon, MTG, or Lorcana. If you are primarily a collector rather than a competitive player, Riftbound's secondary market is less developed and the collector card culture around the game is still establishing itself. Buy in if the League of Legends IP interests you or if you want to play competitively. Do not buy in expecting the same collector-driven value trajectory as Pokemon or MTG.
