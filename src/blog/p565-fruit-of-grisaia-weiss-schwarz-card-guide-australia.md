# The Fruit of Grisaia Weiss Schwarz Card Guide (Australia)

The Fruit of Grisaia is drawn from the visual novel and anime series that follows a group of students with troubled pasts at an isolated academy, brought together under the watch of the mysterious instructor Jb. It is one of the older properties in the current Weiss Schwarz English catalogue, and it has built a loyal, long standing collector base since its original release.

## The set at a glance

The Fruit of Grisaia is a single stand-alone set currently tracked on this site, released 19 May 2023, with a total of 189 cards. Note that a separate Japanese-only Vol. 2 release (set code GRI/S84, 202 cards) exists on the secondary market but has not yet been added to our tracked card database. This guide covers the confirmed set only, and we will update this page once Vol. 2 is fully synced into our data.

The card pool draws its cast from across the main heroines of the series, including Yumiko Sakaki, Amane Suo, Michiru Matsushima, Makina Irisu, Yuki Komine, and Ichihime Kazami, with newly illustrated artwork created specifically for the Weiss Schwarz release rather than reused visual novel art.

## Deck building around the Grisaia cast

Because the source material is a character driven visual novel rather than an action series, the card pool leans toward support and utility effects tied to each heroine's personality and backstory, rather than the more straightforward combat mechanics you find in shonen and isekai focused sets. This gives Fruit of Grisaia a genuinely different feel to build around compared to more action heavy properties, rewarding players who enjoy synergy and support based deck construction over pure aggression.

Each of the six main heroines carries her own distinct card archetype, so deck building here is less about picking a single dominant character and more about deciding which combination of heroines' support effects work best together. Newer collectors sometimes underestimate support focused sets like this one, assuming a lack of straightforward attackers means the deck is weak, when in practice a well built Grisaia deck can grind out longer games effectively against faster, more aggressive opponents.

## Chase cards and signature pulls

The Japanese release of this set featured hot-stamped signature cards from the voice cast, including Ryoko Tanaka as Yumiko Sakaki, Hiroko Taguchi as Amane Suo, Kaori Mizuhashi as Michiru Matsushima, and further cast signatures across the remaining heroines. As with most visual novel adapted sets, these signature cards remain the primary chase items for dedicated collectors of this franchise.

Because Fruit of Grisaia is now a few years past its most recent release, secondary market pricing has had time to settle into a more predictable pattern, which makes it a reasonably approachable set to price check and buy into with confidence.

## Why this property still has a dedicated following

Visual novel adaptations tend to build smaller but notably loyal fanbases compared to mainstream shonen and seinen anime, and Fruit of Grisaia is a clear example of that pattern. Its collector base has stayed engaged well past the initial release window, which has kept demand for key heroine cards reasonably steady rather than dropping off sharply the way some short lived seasonal hits do.

For Australian collectors, this makes Fruit of Grisaia a set worth considering if you already have an interest in visual novel adjacent anime, though it is unlikely to see the kind of rapid price growth that a brand new mainstream hit set might produce. Treat it as a patient, long term addition to a collection rather than a fast moving trade.

## Buying into an older, niche property versus a current mainstream release

Fruit of Grisaia is a useful example of a genuinely different kind of collecting decision compared to the newer mainstream releases covered elsewhere on this site. Where a set like Chainsaw Man or Dandadan benefits from broad, current cultural relevance, Fruit of Grisaia's value proposition rests on a smaller, dedicated fanbase that has stuck with the franchise for years rather than a wide audience that discovered it recently.

That difference matters for how you should think about this set as a collector. Broad mainstream sets tend to see faster price growth when demand spikes, but they can also correct sharply if attention moves on to the next big release. Niche, long standing properties like Fruit of Grisaia tend to move more slowly in both directions, offering a steadier, lower volatility option for collectors who already know they enjoy the franchise rather than those chasing the next speculative pick.

## What to check before buying

1. Confirm which set a listing belongs to. The original set code is GRI/S72, and the untracked Vol. 2 uses GRI/S84, so check the card number carefully rather than assuming a listing matches the set covered in this guide.
2. Check whether a card is base rarity or a signed voice cast version before paying a premium, since the hot-stamped signatures carry meaningfully higher value.
3. As an older release with a smaller ongoing print run, expect somewhat less overall stock in circulation than more recent sets, so budget extra time to compare listings rather than expecting instant availability.
