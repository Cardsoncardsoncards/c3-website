One Piece TCG's next main booster, OP-17 "The World's Strongest Warriors," lands in late August
2026 as the game's 4th anniversary set. The cards matter less than the policy behind this one,
Bandai has confirmed OP-17 is the first main One Piece booster ever released simultaneously
worldwide, closing the gap between Japan and English markets that has existed since the game
launched in 2022.

## Why the global release actually matters

Every main set before OP-17 gave Japan a head start, sometimes by weeks, before English product
arrived. That window let import prices on hot Japanese pulls run up before English collectors had
even opened a pack, and it meant early competitive results came from a Japanese metagame that
didn't always translate directly to the English format. OP-17 removes that gap entirely, English
and Japanese players get the same cards on the same day for the first time in the game's history.

## What's actually in the set

OP-17 is framed around the Impel Down and Marineford arcs, reintroducing popular older Leaders with
new, more competitive effects rather than leaning entirely on new characters. As the 4th anniversary
booster, it also introduces the Treasure Rare, a new rarity slot sitting above Secret Rare, though
Bandai hadn't confirmed which specific card gets it as of mid-July. The set totals roughly 135
unique card types across six Leaders, commons, uncommons, rares and the new premium slots.

## Reintroducing older Leaders

The nostalgic Leader angle is worth paying attention to beyond the anniversary marketing. Bringing
back older Leaders with genuinely competitive new effects gives players who built around those
characters years ago a real reason to return to them, rather than simply retiring older cards to
casual play. That's a different approach to most anniversary sets, which usually lean on brand new
characters rather than rehabilitating existing ones, and it's worth watching which older Leaders
get picked for the treatment once the full spoiler list lands.

## When it actually lands

Bandai's official announcement framed OP-17 as landing in late August, with reporting from
different regions placing the exact date anywhere from 22 to 28 August depending on whether you're
looking at the Japanese release, US prerelease events, or English wide release. Given the layered
rollout of prerelease and wide release windows even under a simultaneous policy, it's worth
checking Bandai's official channels for the confirmed date in your region rather than locking in a
single date this far out.

## What this means for Australian players and sellers

A simultaneous release is a genuinely good thing for the Australian market specifically, since AU
buyers have always been on the English side of that old Japan-first gap. Once OP-17 launches, C3's
[One Piece card pages](/cards/onepiece) will track AUD pricing as singles come online, and with no
early Japanese-market price signal to watch this time, expect the first genuine read on what OP-17
cards are worth to come from opening week itself rather than import listings beforehand.

## Getting ready for launch

If OP-16 is any guide, expect prerelease and early sealed events to be the first real look at how
OP-17's reintroduced Leaders actually play before the full singles market settles. Local game
stores typically run their own launch weekend events alongside the official release, and turning up
for those tends to be the cheapest way to see the set in action before deciding what to chase once
prices are live.
