# Bocchi the Rock Weiss Schwarz Card Guide (Australia)

Bocchi the Rock! is one of the newer additions to the Weiss Schwarz English catalogue, and one of the more popular ones with Australian collectors buying into anime TCGs for the first time. The set draws on the 2022 CloverWorks anime about Hitori Goto, a socially anxious guitarist who forms the band Kessoku Band with Nijika Ijichi, Ryo Yamada, and Ikuyo Kita. The show became one of the breakout hits of its year, and the card set has followed a similar trajectory in terms of demand.

## The set at a glance

Bocchi the Rock is a single stand-alone set, released in English on 14 June 2024, with a total card count of 260 across the booster pack and trial deck. There is no second wave and no follow-up set for this property yet, so like a handful of other newer one-set properties, everything you need to build a complete collection sits in this one release.

The set launched with a Trial Deck (50 cards, designed for new players and playable straight out of the box) alongside the main Booster Pack release. That trial deck structure makes Bocchi the Rock genuinely one of the more beginner-friendly entry points into Weiss Schwarz as a whole, not just into this property.

## What is in the box

Each booster pack contains 9 cards, with 16 packs per display and 18 displays per carton. The set features 100 base card types plus 100 parallel versions, giving a reasonable spread of rarities to chase without the pool being so large it becomes unmanageable for a new collector.

Signature chase cards are a real feature of this release. Packs carry a chance of a hot-stamped signed card from the voice cast, Yoshino Aoyama as Hitori Gotoh, Sayumi Suzushiro as Nijika Ijichi, Saku Mizuno as Ryo Yamada, and Ikumi Hasegawa as Ikuyo Kita. These signed cards sit well above base rarity in both scarcity and price, and they are the cards most Bocchi the Rock collectors are ultimately chasing rather than the common playset cards.

Display boxes also included one of four exclusive PR cards, and each carton came with a stitched playmat exclusive to the English release. As with most carton and display exclusives, once the initial print run sold through, these accessory items became harder to source than the singles themselves.

## Deck building around Kessoku Band

The card pool centres on the four main members of Kessoku Band, with Hitori Goto (performing under her stage persona) as the emotional and mechanical core of most builds. Because this is a single, self-contained set rather than a multi-set property split across archetypes, deck building here is less about choosing between competing factions and more about how you weight support for each band member within the one shared card pool.

This makes Bocchi the Rock a genuinely approachable starting point if you are new to Weiss Schwarz and want to build around a cast you already know from the anime, without needing to cross-reference cards from several different set codes the way you would with a longer-running multi-set property.

## Why Australian collectors are buying in

Bocchi the Rock! benefits from strong crossover demand between anime fans and TCG collectors, similar to what drove interest in other recent slice-of-life and band-themed releases on the Weiss Schwarz platform. The show's popularity translated directly into set demand at release, and that demand has not fully cooled since, particularly for the signature cards and higher rarities featuring Hitori and Nijika.

Because the set is relatively recent and was a mainstream release rather than a limited convention exclusive, supply is reasonably healthy on the secondary market, which keeps prices more accessible than older, harder-to-find sets. That combination, strong character demand plus decent supply, is exactly the kind of set worth watching if you are building a collection with an eye on both playing and long-term value.

## What to check before buying

1. Confirm whether a listing is the signed voice cast version or a standard parallel before paying a premium. Photos should show the hot-stamped signature clearly, not just describe it in the title.
2. Trial Deck cards and Booster Pack cards share the same card pool and can be combined into a single deck, so do not assume you need to buy exclusively from one product type to build a complete deck.
3. Check card condition carefully on higher rarity singles. As a 2024 release, most of the supply in circulation is still fresh, so a card described as played or damaged should be priced well below near-mint listings for the same card.

## How this set compares to other music-themed releases

Bocchi the Rock arrived as part of a wider push of music and band-themed Weiss Schwarz sets around the same period, sitting alongside releases built around other idol and band properties. What sets Bocchi the Rock apart from most of that group is the strength of its source material outside the TCG space. The anime carried genuine mainstream crossover appeal, picking up awards attention and a large audience well beyond the usual anime TCG buyer base, and that broader fanbase has been a steady source of demand for the card set ever since release.

For collectors comparing where to put their budget across several music-themed properties, Bocchi the Rock is generally the safer long-term hold of the group, precisely because the underlying show has staying power rather than being a short-lived seasonal hit. That is not a guarantee of future value, but it is a reasonable factor to weigh if you are choosing between several similar releases with a limited collecting budget.
