# Saekano in Weiss Schwarz: Two Level 3 Builds to Choose Between

Saekano brings a genuinely clear deck building fork into Weiss Schwarz, community deck guides document two distinct level 3 builds to choose between, one centred on Megumi's memory-based combo line and another built around Michiru's field-wide finishing combo. If you already play and want the build detail, jump ahead. If you are newer to Weiss Schwarz, the primer below covers the core rules, with the full mechanics glossary linked at the end.

## The set at a glance

Saekano spans 3 tracked releases, the original How to Raise a Boring Girlfriend set (28 October 2022), a flat follow-up release (May 2023), and Saekano the Movie: Finale (April 2024), totalling 462 cards.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## Two Documented Build Paths

Saekano's confirmed core cast includes Megumi Kato as the main heroine, alongside Eriri Spencer Sawamura and Utaha Kasumigaoka, each with documented climax combos of her own. Community deck guides consistently document two main level 3 builds. The first centres on "Megumi, Ideal Girl," built around sending this level 3 into memory and bringing it back out for a resilient, hard-to-remove threat, confirmed via Bushiroad's own deck recipe material to carry up to six effects in the optimal situation. The second centres on "Michiru of Icy Tail," a build that searches for multiple copies of Michiru and fields them at level 3 aiming to finish the opponent in one coordinated attack. Most competitive decks pick one path rather than trying to blend both, and Utaha's own documented support cards can slot into either build as a flexible piece once the core choice is settled.

## Chase cards and signature pulls

Megumi's confirmed climax combo, "How to Raise a Boring Girlfriend," sends fallen opponent characters directly into their clock, documented as a genuinely game-ending ability in the right situation. Eriri's climax combo, "A Separate Route After 8 Years," is separately documented for players building around her specifically. ## Frequently Asked Questions

**What are the two main level 3 builds for Saekano?**
A "Megumi, Ideal Girl" build centred on a resilient memory-based combo, and a "Michiru of Icy Tail" build centred on fielding multiple Michiru copies for a coordinated finishing attack.

**Should a new player build around Megumi or Michiru?**
That depends on preferred playstyle, Megumi's build favours durability and grinding out an advantage, while Michiru's build favours a more explosive finishing sequence.

**Do later Saekano releases combine with the original trial deck material?**
Yes, confirmed via product listings, later releases share the SHS card number prefix with the original trial deck, meaning collections build forward rather than starting over.

**Do older Saekano sets remain legal in tournament play?**
Weiss Schwarz does not use a rotating standard format, so older cards generally remain playable, though always check a specific event's rules before entering.

**What is Megumi's "How to Raise a Boring Girlfriend" climax combo?**
It is a documented climax combo, confirmed via Bushiroad's own deck recipe material, that sends fallen opponent characters directly into their clock, described as a genuinely game-ending ability in the right situation.

**How does the four copy limit affect choosing between the two documented builds?**
It reinforces committing to one build path, since running four copies of both Megumi's and Michiru's level 3 combo pieces at once would spread your deck too thin to execute either combo reliably in an actual game.
