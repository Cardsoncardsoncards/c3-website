Star Wars Unlimited launched in 2024 and quickly built a strong player community in Australia. It uses an alternating-activation system — each player takes one action at a time rather than completing a full turn — which makes it feel more interactive than most TCGs.

This guide covers the rules you need to play your first game.

## The Goal

Destroy your opponent's Base before they destroy yours.

Your Base has a health value (typically 30). When it reaches 0, you lose. No life totals, no decking out — the single goal is destroying the Base.

## What You Need

Two players. Two 50-card decks. One Leader Card per player. One Base Card per player (separate from the main deck).

Starter Sets come with two ready-to-play decks plus Leader and Base cards for both players. Available at local game stores in Australia for around AU$25 to AU$35.

## Card Types

**Leader Cards**: Your featured character. Leaders can be deployed to the arena mid-game by paying their cost once per game.

**Base Cards**: Your headquarters. Has a health value. Protect it.

**Unit Cards**: Your fighters — ground troops and space ships. Played to two zones: Ground Arena and Space Arena.

**Event Cards**: One-time effects played from hand.

**Upgrade Cards**: Attachments that improve your units.

## The Resource System

Every card in your hand can be played for its effect, or placed face-down as a Resource to generate one resource per turn.

Most cards have a cost in the top-left corner. Pay that many resources by exhausting (tapping sideways) that many resource cards.

This is the same card-as-resource decision seen in Dragon Ball Super — every card is a potential resource.

## How Turns Work (Alternating Activation)

Star Wars Unlimited doesn't have traditional "my turn, your turn" gameplay. Instead, players alternate taking single actions until both players pass consecutively (ending the round).

Each action is one of:

- **Play a card** from your hand
- **Attack** with one of your units or your Leader
- **Use** an action ability
- **Take the Initiative** (a special marker that gives priority advantages)
- **Pass** (skip your action for this round)

Once both players pass without taking an action between them, the round ends. Both players ready their exhausted cards and a new round begins.

## The Two Arenas

Units go into one of two zones: **Ground** or **Space**. Ground units can only attack Ground units (or the Base if your opponent has no Ground units). Space units can only attack Space units (or the Base if your opponent has no Space units).

Units can only attack the opponent's Base directly if there are no enemy units in that same arena.

## How Attacking Works

Declare one of your ready (non-exhausted) units as the attacker and a target. The target can be any enemy unit in the same arena, or the opponent's Base if they have no units in that arena.

Both units deal their Power damage simultaneously. If a unit takes damage equal to or greater than its HP, it's defeated and discarded.

When attacking the Base, the attacking unit deals its Power damage directly to the Base's health total.

## The Leader

Leaders start the game deployed at the side of the battlefield, not in an arena. Once per game, you can pay the Leader's deploy cost to flip it and bring it into the Ground Arena as a powerful unit.
