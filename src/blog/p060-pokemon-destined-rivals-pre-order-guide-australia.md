## What Is Destined Rivals?

Destined Rivals is a Scarlet and Violet era booster set released on 30 May 2025. It is the first Pokemon TCG set to feature Team Rocket as a central faction since the original Team Rocket expansion in 2000. The set includes Giovanni, Archer, and key Rocket-affiliated Pokemon alongside trainer cards, rival characters from the main series games, and the debut of full-art Team Rocket-themed Special Illustration Rares.

The set received broad critical approval from the Pokemon community for its art quality, the strength of its chase card lineup, and the nostalgia factor of the Rocket return. Ten months after release, secondary market prices on the top cards have appreciated rather than depreciated, which is unusual for a Pokemon set at this stage of its lifecycle.

Buy the booster display or ETB depending on budget. The set opens generously. You will not consistently pull the Team Rocket's Mewtwo ex SIR from a single display, but you will pull multiple cards worth keeping and a set that is visually strong throughout.

That said, sealed product speculation always carries risk and results are never guaranteed. Only hold sealed product you are comfortable owning long-term if prices do not move as expected.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Destined Rivals still available in Australia?**
Yes. Booster displays, ETBs, and individual packs remain available on Amazon AU. Stock levels fluctuate so check the current listing for availability.

**What is the best product to buy from Destined Rivals?**
The ETB is the best entry point for most buyers. It includes a valuable promo card, a manageable amount of product, and all the accessories you need to store and play. For volume collectors, the booster display makes more sense.

**Can I still pull the Team Rocket's Mewtwo ex SIR from a box?**
Yes, but the pull rate is approximately 1 in 30 to 40 packs. A single booster display of 36 packs gives you a reasonable but not guaranteed chance. Buying the single from eBay AU is significantly more efficient if that specific card is your target.

**Are Destined Rivals prices going up or down?**
Top chase card prices have appreciated since release rather than declining, which is unusual for a Pokemon set nearly a year after launch. Sealed product prices have also held above MSRP. There is no clear signal that prices will fall significantly in the near term given the sustained demand from the Team Rocket nostalgia factor.

**Is the Japanese version different from the English version?**
Yes. Japanese releases have different card numbering, slightly different art on some cards, and separate product formats. Japanese product is not available through Amazon AU and must be imported separately. The English version is the standard for Australian play and collection.
