# Love Live in Weiss Schwarz: An Active, Growing Multi-Series Catalogue

Love Live is one of the few properties in Weiss Schwarz that is genuinely still growing rather than sitting as a closed legacy catalogue. That ongoing release pattern changes how you should think about collecting and deck building here compared to a franchise that stopped receiving new material years ago. If you already play and want the sub-series breakdown, jump ahead. If you are newer to Weiss Schwarz, the primer below covers the core rules, and the full mechanics glossary is linked at the end for anything not covered here.

## The set at a glance

Love Live spans 8 tracked releases in our database, from the original Love Live! set (23 June 2014) through Love Live! Sunshine!! and its Extra Booster, up to two 2024 releases, the School Idol Festival Series 10th Anniversary Premium Booster and School idol festival 2 MIRACLE LIVE!, totalling roughly 1,570 cards. Note that while the wider Love Live franchise has produced other sub-series such as Superstar and Nijigasaki High School Idol Club in anime and mobile game form, neither currently has a dedicated set in our tracked database, so this guide covers only the confirmed releases above.

## Quick primer: how Weiss Schwarz works

Every deck is 50 cards split across colours, cards get stronger as your clock (a face-down pile built from damage taken) grows, climax cards (up to 8 per deck) combo with character abilities on the right turn, and you can run up to four copies of any named card. For the full breakdown, read our [complete rules glossary](/blog/how-weiss-schwarz-works-rules-deck-building-australia).

## Deck building across Love Live's sub-series

Love Live decks are usually built around one idol group or sub-series rather than mixing across the whole franchise, since each has its own distinct card pool and trait identity. A Sunshine-focused deck plays differently to one built from the original Love Live! cast, even though both carry the Love Live name. With Love Live spanning multiple tracked sub-series, each with its own idol roster, the four copy limit is part of why building here means picking a sub-series and committing to its cast rather than trying to represent every group in one deck.

## Why Love Live's collecting angle matters

What sets Love Live apart from many other Weiss Schwarz properties is that it is not a single finished storyline being mined for one final wave of sets, it is an ongoing franchise still producing new material, most recently in 2024. If you are buying in expecting a fixed, complete catalogue the way you might with a finished series, that is the wrong assumption, new material is a realistic possibility here. Picking a specific sub-series to focus on, based on which idol group you actually follow, will serve you better than buying broadly across all of them at once.

Since this property is still active, it is worth watching for new set announcements rather than assuming the current 8 releases represent the full picture long term. ## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Love Live still receiving new Weiss Schwarz sets, or is the catalogue finished?**
Love Live is genuinely still active, with the most recent tracked release landing in November 2024, unlike properties that have stopped receiving new sets.

**Does every Love Live sub-series, including Superstar and Nijigasaki, have its own Weiss Schwarz set?**
Not currently. Only the original Love Live! line and Sunshine!! have dedicated tracked sets in our database at this time. Superstar and Nijigasaki are real parts of the wider franchise but do not currently have their own confirmed Weiss Schwarz release here.

**Which Love Live sub-series is the best starting point for a new collector?**
That depends on which idol group you follow. Pick the sub-series tied to the group you actually want to collect or play, since there is no single correct entry point across an active, multi-series property.

**Do older Love Live sets remain legal in tournament play?**
Weiss Schwarz does not use a rotating standard format, so older Love Live cards generally remain playable, though always check a specific event's rules before entering.

## What to check before buying

1. Confirm which Love Live sub-series a listing belongs to before buying, since the original line and Sunshine!! have separate card pools and generally do not share strong cross-series synergy.
2. Check whether a card is base rarity or a signed variant before paying a premium, since rarity structures differ release to release across this property's tracked sets.
3. If you are chasing a specific sub-series like Superstar or Nijigasaki, confirm first that a Weiss Schwarz release actually exists for it rather than assuming every anime sub-series has a matching card set.
