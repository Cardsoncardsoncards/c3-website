# The Eminence in Shadow Weiss Schwarz Card Guide (Australia)

The Eminence in Shadow follows Cid Kagenou, a young man obsessed with playing the role of a mysterious background mastermind, who unknowingly becomes an actual overpowered hero after being reincarnated into a fantasy world. It is one of the more distinctive isekai comedies of recent years, built around the joke that Cid's self-styled dramatic monologues and secret organisation roleplay turn out to be genuinely, comically effective. That premise has made for one of the more entertaining recent additions to the Weiss Schwarz English catalogue.

## The set at a glance

The Eminence in Shadow is a single stand-alone set, released as an English Edition Original on 24 April 2026, with a total of 292 cards. Being an English Edition Original means this set was created specifically for the English market rather than being a straight port of a prior Japanese release, which Weiss Schwarz occasionally does for properties with strong Western fan demand.

The card pool is built around Cid in his Shadow persona, alongside the seven members of his secret organisation, Alpha, Beta, Gamma, Delta, Epsilon, Zeta, and Eta, each named after letters of the Greek alphabet in keeping with the show's running gag about secretive, dramatic naming conventions.

## Deck building around Shadow and the Seven Shadows

Because this set draws its cast from Cid's inner circle rather than a sprawling wider world, deck building here has a clear structure. Shadow himself typically anchors the most powerful builds, with the seven named subordinates providing the supporting cast and synergy pieces around him. Each of the seven has a distinct personality and combat style in the source material, and the card pool reflects that with meaningfully different card effects across the group rather than reskinned versions of the same mechanic.

This gives Eminence in Shadow more build variety than some single-set properties manage, since you genuinely have eight distinct characters worth building around rather than one central figure supported by generic filler.

## Chase cards and signature pulls

Every pack carries a chance of a hot-stamped signed card from the voice cast, including Seiichiro Yamashita as Shadow, Asami Seto as Alpha, Inori Minase as Beta, Suzuko Mimori as Gamma, Fairouz Ai as Delta, Hisako Kanemoto as Epsilon, Ayaka Asai as Zeta, and Reina Kondo as Eta. Display boxes also carried one of eight exclusive PR cards, giving collectors a genuinely broad spread of chase items across the whole main cast rather than concentrated around a single character.

As a very recent release, pricing on Eminence in Shadow is still finding its level. Early demand has tracked the show's ongoing popularity, and with the anime continuing to produce new seasons, there is a reasonable chance of continued interest in this set as the story progresses.

The Eminence in Shadow built its audience specifically around subverting isekai tropes rather than playing them straight, which has given it a distinct identity in a genre that can otherwise feel repetitive. ## English Edition Originals versus imported sets

Most Weiss Schwarz English releases are translations of an existing Japanese set, meaning the card pool, rarities, and artwork were already established before the English version arrived. An English Edition Original like The Eminence in Shadow works differently: the set was designed and produced specifically for the English market from the start. For collectors, this matters because there is no Japanese secondary market to reference for comparison pricing, and no risk of an English release under or over correcting against an established Japanese price point. The English release is simply the market for this set.

That also means demand signals for Eminence in Shadow come entirely from the English-speaking fanbase rather than being influenced by Japanese collector behaviour, which can make pricing here move somewhat differently to imported sets. If you are used to tracking Japanese secondary market prices as a guide for English releases, that comparison point simply does not exist for this property, so rely on English market listings and demand trends specifically rather than assuming Japanese pricing patterns will translate across.
