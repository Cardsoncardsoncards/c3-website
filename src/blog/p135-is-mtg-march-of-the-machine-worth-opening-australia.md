## What Is March of the Machine and Why Does It Matter for Australian Players?

March of the Machine is the fifth and final set in the Phyrexian invasion arc following Kamigawa: Neon Dynasty, Streets of New Capenna, Dominaria United, The Brothers' War, and Phyrexia: All Will Be One. The set introduces Battle cards — a new card type representing the planes under Phyrexian siege that can be attacked by opponents and flipped when defeated. The Multiverse Legends bonus sheet contains 65 reprints of iconic legendary creatures including Ragavan Nimble Pilferer, Atraxa Praetors Voice, and The Wandering Emperor in special showcase treatments.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the Multiverse Legends bonus sheet?**
The Multiverse Legends is a 65-card bonus set of legendary creature reprints in special showcase treatments appearing in every March of the Machine pack. Each pack contains one Multiverse Legends card. The sheet includes iconic legends from across MTG history — Ragavan, Atraxa, The Wandering Emperor, Thalia, and others — in a special card treatment. Values range from AU$0.50 to AU$50+ depending on the specific card.

**Is March of the Machine worth opening in Australia?**
At AU$180, March of the Machine offers above-average EV for a 2023 standard set due to the Multiverse Legends bonus sheet. A high-value Legends card in showcase treatment meaningfully boosts box value. Use the C3 EV Calculator for a current personalised assessment.

**What are Battle cards in March of the Machine?**
Battle cards are a new card type introduced in March of the Machine representing planes under Phyrexian attack. Battles enter with a set number of defence counters. Opponents can attack Battles to remove counters. When the last counter is removed, the Battle is defeated, exiled, and the controller casts the transformed card on the back face for free. Battles represent the narrative of defending or losing each plane in the invasion.

**How does March of the Machine compare to Wilds of Eldraine for EV?**
Both sets benefit from bonus sheet mechanics boosting EV above the standard set average. Wilds of Eldraine's Enchanting Tales sheet is stronger for Commander players due to Sylvan Library, Parallel Lives, and Smothering Tithe. March of the Machine's Multiverse Legends sheet has a higher individual card ceiling (Ragavan) but lower average floor. Both sit above typical 2023 standard set EV.
