Ashes of the Empire, the eighth set for Star Wars Unlimited, officially released on 17 July 2026
from publisher Fantasy Flight Games. The set turns the game's focus to the Battle of Endor and the
uncertain years that followed, drawing heavily on Return of the Jedi alongside The Mandalorian,
The Book of Boba Fett and Ahsoka. More than 260 new cards are in the set, and it's already live on
C3's [Star Wars Unlimited card pages](/cards/starwars).

## What's actually new

The headline mechanic is Support, a new keyword that lets a unit enable another friendly unit to
attack immediately, temporarily sharing its own abilities for that attack. Ashes of the Empire also
introduces Advantage tokens, a fleeting resource that rewards aggressive, tempo focused play. Both
mechanics sit on top of an unusually heavy focus on upgrades, giving players more ways to build
around equipping their units than any previous set.

## The theme, and who's in it

Thematically this is an Imperial remnant and Mandalorian heavy set, built around the power vacuum
left after the Empire's collapse. Grogu makes his first Star Wars Unlimited appearance here, fitting
naturally alongside Bo-Katan Kryze and the wider Mandalorian archetype. Ahsoka Tano, Hera Syndulla
and the rest of the Spectres crew return from their Rebels and Ahsoka appearances, and two new
Spotlight Decks lead with Luke Skywalker and Emperor Palpatine as leaders.

## Chase cards and early Australian pricing

The Luke Skywalker and Emperor Palpatine Hyperspace and Showcase variants are the clear chase cards
from this set, alongside premium Mandalorian and Advantage token prints. C3's live pricing already
shows real movement, with [Luke Skywalker, I Can Save Him (Showcase)](/cards/starwars/ashes-of-the-empire-luke-skywalker-i-can-save-him-showcase-771)
and [Ahsoka Tano, Trust in the Force (Showcase)](/cards/starwars/ashes-of-the-empire-ahsoka-tano-trust-in-the-force-showcase-775)
both currently trading well above AU$500. [Grogu, Charming Companion (Showcase)](/cards/starwars/ashes-of-the-empire-grogu-charming-companion-showcase-784)
is sitting over AU$350, reflecting how strong demand is for his first appearance in the game.

## Rotation and what it means for Premier

Ashes of the Empire continues the Symbol B rotation batch, meaning it stays Premier legal until
March 2028. It follows A Lawless Time, released in March 2026, and Fantasy Flight has confirmed a
card cycle that runs between the two sets. Existing leaders like Grand Admiral Thrawn, Rey and
Padmé Amidala all pick up meaningful new support from this set, so players already running those
decks have a reason to look closely at the new list.

## Getting your hands on it

Prerelease events for Ashes of the Empire began the week before launch, and standard boosters,
Prerelease Boxes and limited run Carbonite Edition boosters with exclusive Prestige variants are all
part of the release. Worth noting for competitive players specifically, the set isn't tournament
legal everywhere immediately. The Star Wars Unlimited Galactic Championship in Las Vegas, running
24 to 26 July, is still being played on the Sets 4 to 7 card pool, with Ashes of the Empire joining
Premier legality separately.

## Looking ahead

With Set 9, Homeworlds, already confirmed for release in Q4 2026, Fantasy Flight is keeping Star
Wars Unlimited on the same steady cadence it's held since Spark of Rebellion launched in 2024. Watch
how the Support keyword performs once real tournament data comes in from Planetary Qualifiers on the
new card pool, that's usually the clearest signal of whether a mechanic reshapes the format or
settles in as a supporting piece.

## Buying into the set in Australia

Local game stores are running their own Ashes of the Empire prerelease events off the back of the
17 July launch, and standard boosters are typically the cheapest entry point if you just want to
open packs rather than chase a specific Showcase or Hyperspace card. Booster boxes and Carbonite
Edition boxes both sell for a real premium over singles value early in a set's life, so it's
usually worth checking C3's live pricing before deciding whether box breaking or buying the
specific singles you want makes more sense for your budget. Prices on a brand new set move quickly
in the first few weeks as the market works out what's actually good, so a card sitting high today
isn't guaranteed to stay there once more Planetary Qualifier results come in.

Good Games and other Australian stores typically carry Ashes of the Empire alongside their existing
Star Wars Unlimited stock, so a locals night is often the easiest way to trade into or out of the
cards you need, and it's a good chance to see the Support keyword in action before deciding whether
to build around it yourself.
